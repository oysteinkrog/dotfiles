# Washington State Estate Planning

## Headline Posture

- **No state income tax** (constitutional)
- **State estate tax** with a **$3,076,000** filing threshold / exclusion amount for **2026**
- **Top Washington estate tax rate is now 35%**
- **Community property** state
- **Strong asset-protection** features
- **Capital gains tax** now uses **tiered 2025+ rates: 7% on the first $1,000,000 of taxable Washington capital gains and 9.9% above that**

Verified from Washington Department of Revenue materials on **2026-04-16**:

- Estate tax tables: `https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/other-taxes/estate-tax-tables`
- Estate tax FAQ: `https://dor.wa.gov/taxes-rates/other-taxes/estate-tax/estate-tax-faq`
- Capital gains special notice: `https://dor.wa.gov/forms-publications/publications-subject/special-notices/new-tiered-rates-washingtons-capital-gains-tax`

## WA Estate Tax

- 2026 filing threshold / exclusion amount: **$3,076,000**
- For deaths from **July 1, 2025 through December 31, 2025**, the threshold was **$3,000,000**
- For deaths from **January 1, 2018 through June 30, 2025**, the threshold was **$2,193,000**
- Current rates for deaths on or after July 1, 2025 are materially higher than the older regime and now run up to **35%**
- **Washington does not recognize federal-style portability**; each estate gets its own Washington exclusion amount, so married-couple planning often has to preserve both exemptions deliberately
- Applies to WA residents' worldwide estate + non-residents' WA real estate

### Planning
- Credit-shelter / bypass trust is often central when a married couple wants to preserve both Washington exemptions or control remainder beneficiaries
- Lifetime gifts reduce estate (no WA gift tax)
- 3-year lookback on life insurance ownership changes (IRC § 2035 incorporated)

## Probate

Washington probate:
- **Unsupervised administration:** standard; executor acts without court oversight
- **Personal representative** holds authority
- **TEDRA** (Trust and Estate Dispute Resolution Act) — mechanism for resolving disputes, modifying trusts
- Typically 6-18 months

## Community Property

Washington is community property. Same mechanics as CA, TX, NV, etc.:
- Community: acquired during marriage
- Separate: premarital, inherited, gifted
- **Double step-up in basis** on community at first death

### Community Property Agreement
Unique WA feature:
- Spouses agree to convert all property to community with right of survivorship
- At first death: all community passes to survivor outside probate
- At first death it can collapse a lot of planning into pure survivor ownership, which may be convenient but can also waste first-death Washington estate-tax planning opportunities

Use carefully — loses flexibility of credit-shelter planning.

## Intestacy

RCW 11.04.015:

**Community:** all to spouse

**Separate:**
- Spouse + issue: 1/2 to spouse, 1/2 to issue
- Spouse + no issue: 3/4 to spouse, 1/4 to parents/siblings
- Spouse alone: all
- No spouse: issue, parents, siblings

## Power of Attorney

- Washington adopted UPOAA 2016
- Statutory short-form available
- Springing and immediate both valid
- Banks must accept UPOAA-compliant POA (penalty for wrongful rejection)

## Healthcare Directive and Durable POA for Health Care

- **Health Care Directive** (living will, RCW 70.122)
- **Durable POA for Health Care** (separate document)
- **POLST** widely used

## Medicaid (Apple Health)

- Not income cap state (Medically Needy equivalent)
- 5-year lookback
- MERP in effect
- Community spouse protections per federal

## WA Capital Gains Tax (NEW as of 2022)

Unique to WA:
- Beginning with **tax year 2025** (returns due in **2026**), Washington uses a **tiered capital gains tax**
- First **$1,000,000** of taxable Washington capital gains: **7%**
- Above **$1,000,000** of taxable Washington capital gains: **9.9%**
- Does NOT apply to real estate, retirement, personal residence, livestock
- DOES apply to brokerage, stock, partnership interests
- Washington DOR's June 30, 2025 special notice explains the change and cites ESSB 5813

**Implication:** Washington is still attractive relative to many income-tax states, but it is no longer accurate to describe it as a flat "7% above $250K" regime. For concentrated-position and business-sale planning, model the current tiered rates explicitly.

## Unique WA Features

### Community Property Agreement (above)

### TEDRA
- Powerful trust/estate dispute resolution
- Can modify irrevocable trusts
- Non-judicial settlement agreements
- Decanting-like remedies

### Strong Asset Protection
- Homestead protection is substantial but fact-sensitive; verify the current Washington homestead amount and any county/value-specific application before relying on a dollar figure
- Retirement plans unlimited
- Life insurance cash value exempt
- Homestead survives bankruptcy

### CLAT / CRT Planning
- WA no state income tax makes charitable trust planning attractive for residents
- But capital gains tax now a consideration

### Domestic Asset Protection Trust — NOT Available
WA does not have DAPT statute. Residents seeking self-settled protection use NV, SD, etc.

## Unique to Pacific Northwest

### Cabin / Vacation Home in WA or BC
- Cross-border planning (US-Canada)
- BC ownership complex for US decedents

### Natural Resources
- Timber rights
- Water rights (both surface and groundwater)
- Marine / shellfish operations

## Domicile

- Washington is moderate on domicile enforcement
- Often moving from CA, OR (high tax) to WA
- WA capital gains tax reduces the gap with CA now

## Trust Situs Considerations

- WA has generous trust rules but not dynasty-trust state
- Uniform Statutory Rule Against Perpetuities (150 years)
- No trust income tax
- Many WA residents use AK, NV, SD trusts for longer-term dynasty

## Cost of Planning

- Simple will: $750-$2,500
- Revocable trust: $2,500-$5,000
- WA estate tax planning: $5K-$20K
- HNW: $20K-$100K+

## Common Failure Modes in Washington

1. **Using the old $2.193M / 20% summary after the 2025-2026 changes** — outdated estimates understate exposure
2. **Assuming federal portability helps for WA** — it doesn't
3. **Community property agreement used when credit-shelter needed** — loses tax opportunity
4. **WA cap gains tax ignored** for major sales
5. **Cross-border (BC) assets not addressed**
6. **Timber / natural resource assets** not planned
7. **TEDRA not used** for trust modifications where it could help

## Verification Notes

- Washington's estate tax changed materially starting **July 1, 2025** and again by indexation for **2026**.
- Do not rely on older planner summaries that still show a **$2.193M threshold** or **20% top rate**.
- For live recommendations, log the exact date-of-death range used in `analyses/official-source-log.md` because the correct Washington answer depends on that date.
