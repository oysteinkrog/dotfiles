# State-Specific Estate Planning References

Estate planning is heavily state-specific. The core federal framework (transfer tax, retirement plan rules, SECURE Act) applies everywhere, but virtually everything else — wills, probate, intestacy, community property, homestead, elective share, incapacity documents, Medicaid, state estate tax — varies by state.

## When State Rules Matter Most

- **Probate process:** some states are fast and cheap; others (CA, FL, NY, NJ) slow and expensive
- **Intestacy defaults:** who inherits if no will varies wildly
- **State estate tax:** 17 jurisdictions still impose; most states do not
- **Community property:** 9 states (AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI)
- **Elective share:** spousal right to claim against will varies
- **Homestead protection:** massive variation (FL unlimited, TX strong; most states limited)
- **Medicaid rules:** significant state variation within federal framework
- **Execution requirements:** witnesses, notarization, holographic wills
