# Massachusetts Estate Planning

## Headline Posture

- **State estate tax with $2M threshold** (raised from $1M in 2023)
- **State income tax 5%** (relatively moderate)
- **Slow/expensive probate** in some counties
- **Common-law property** (not community)
- **Uniform Probate Code** adopted 2012

## Massachusetts Estate Tax

**Threshold raised 2023:** $2M (was $1M since 2006).

- Applies to estates over $2M
- Graduated rates: ~0.8% to 16% (top rate on estates over $12M)
- Effective rate complex — uses pre-2001 federal formula with adjustments
- Non-residents owning MA real estate also subject
- **Estate under $2M pays zero**; over $2M, tax on excess

### Planning
- Credit-shelter / bypass trust at first death
- Portability does NOT apply to MA estate tax (unlike federal)
- So credit-shelter planning often matters; do not assume federal portability solves the Massachusetts problem
- Annual gifting reduces MA estate (no MA gift tax; no lookback)
- Lifetime gifts not added back to MA estate (unlike NY's 3-year lookback)

Lifetime gifts are MA-friendly — use them.

## Probate

Massachusetts probate varies by county:
- **Middlesex, Norfolk, Suffolk:** busy, slower
- **Small counties:** faster
- **Uniform Probate Code** since 2012: simpler administration
- **Informal probate** available for simple cases
- **Formal probate** for contested or complex

Typical timeline: 6-18 months.

## Intestacy

MA Uniform Probate Code § 2-102, 2-103:

- **Spouse only (no descendants, no parents):** all to spouse
- **Spouse + descendants all from marriage, no other descendants of spouse:** all to spouse
- **Spouse + descendants with children from prior relationships:** first $100K + 1/2 of balance to spouse; rest to descendants
- No spouse: descendants; parents; siblings; etc.

## Elective Share

MA G.L. c. 191 § 15:

- Surviving spouse can claim against will
- Complicated formula based on children, will provisions
- Often takes 1/3 of personal property + life estate in 1/3 of real property
- Can be waived by prenup

## Non-Community Property

MA is common-law property. Each spouse owns separately what they earn; joint ownership by election.

## Power of Attorney

Massachusetts POA:
- No mandatory statutory form (but widely used forms)
- Durable POA recognized
- Springing POA allowed but problematic
- Third-party acceptance variable
- RUFADAA adopted

## Healthcare Proxy and Living Will

- **Healthcare proxy** (G.L. c. 201D) — specific statutory form
- **Living will:** MA doesn't have a statutory living will statute but advance directives recognized via case law and common-law rights
- **MOLST** used for end-of-life

## Medicaid (MassHealth)

MA MassHealth specifics:
- **Not an income cap state** (Medically Needy)
- **5-year lookback**
- **MERP** in effect
- **Community spouse protections** per federal
- Home care programs more generous than many states
- MA has PACE programs widely

## Unique MA Features

### Declaration of Homestead
MA homestead requires filing:
- **Automatic homestead:** $125K protection
- **Declared homestead:** $500K protection
- **Must file declaration** with Registry of Deeds
- Applies only to primary residence

### Durable Power of Attorney
- Specifically "durable" must be stated
- Otherwise ends at incapacity

### Stepchild Intestate Right
Do not casually assume "stepchild means zero issue" if the family facts include equitable-adoption-style reliance, guardianship history, or a long parent-child record that could invite litigation.

### Realty Trust (Nominee Trust)
- Unique MA structure
- Trustees hold legal title; beneficiaries hold equitable
- Used for privacy in real estate
- Different from conventional living trust

### Cape and Vacation Homes
- MA coastal property planning
- Shared family cottage structures
- LLC or family partnership

## Domicile Considerations

MA is moderately aggressive on domicile:
- Usually follows federal residency rules
- Part-year residence common (Cape Cod, Vermont)
- Less audit pressure than NY

## Trust Situs Considerations

- MA uses a statutory RAP framework with a 90-year vesting alternative rather than South-Dakota-style perpetual dynasty planning
- MA taxes trust income at resident level
- MA residents often use SD, NV, DE situs for dynasty trusts

## Recent Legislation

- **2023:** estate tax threshold raised to $2M (from $1M)
- **2012:** UPC adopted
- **2016:** RUFADAA adopted

## Cost of Planning

- Simple will: $750-$2,500
- Revocable trust package: $2,500-$6,000
- HNW / MA estate tax planning: $10K-$40K+

## Common Failure Modes in Massachusetts

1. **Estate $2M-$5M without planning** — MA estate tax on entire excess
2. **Relying on federal portability for MA** — doesn't apply
3. **No declared homestead** — reduced protection
4. **Realty trust used incorrectly** — creates confusion
5. **Stepchildren unclear inclusion/exclusion** — equitable adoption doctrine
6. **Moving from MA to NH / VT for retirement without proper closure** — MA audits
7. **Healthcare proxy form not MA-specific** — hospital may question
8. **Living will assumed to work like POLST** — different legal status
