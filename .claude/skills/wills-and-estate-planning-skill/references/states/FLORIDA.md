# Florida Estate Planning

## Headline Posture

- **No state income tax, no state estate tax, no state gift tax, no state inheritance tax**
- **Unlimited homestead protection** (most generous in US)
- **Strong asset-protection laws** (tenants-by-entirety, head-of-household wage protection)
- **Common domicile destination** from high-tax states
- **Lady Bird deed** practice available (enhanced life estate)

## Why FL Popular for HNW Relocation

- No state income tax
- No estate tax
- Powerful creditor protection
- Deep Florida trust-and-estate bar
- Large wealth-services ecosystem

## Florida Estate Tax

Florida has none. Constitutional amendment prohibits state estate/inheritance tax (Art. VII § 5). Federal tax still applies.

## Florida Homestead

Among the most powerful in US:

### Protection Against Creditors
- **Unlimited value** on homestead (subject to acreage limits)
- **Acreage:** up to 1/2 acre in municipality; up to 160 acres outside
- **Exceptions:** mortgage holder, mechanic's lien, tax lien, HOA
- Survives bankruptcy (with some new limits for recent purchases)

### Protection at Death — Forced Heirship
- Homestead descends to surviving spouse and minor children by force of law
- **Cannot devise homestead to anyone other than spouse** if surviving spouse exists
- Minor children's rights if no spouse
- If the decedent leaves a spouse and descendants, the default descent rule is specialized; under Fla. Stat. 732.401 the surviving spouse generally takes a life estate with remainder to descendants unless the spouse timely elects a one-half tenant-in-common interest instead

### Implication for Planning
- **Cannot use homestead for credit-shelter trust** if spouse alive (unless spouse consents to specific structure)
- **Homestead in revocable trust** valid per case law (complicated)
- **Spousal consent to non-default treatment** required
- **Lady Bird deed** can sometimes preserve homestead-style benefits while naming a remainder beneficiary, but the deed language and the exact homestead fact pattern should be verified before relying on that outcome

## Lady Bird Deed (Enhanced Life Estate Deed)

Unique to FL, TX, MI, VT, WV:

- Grantor retains full control during life (can sell, mortgage, devise)
- Upon death, property passes to named remainder beneficiary outside probate
- **Preserves homestead status** during life
- **Preserves step-up in basis**
- Often used in Florida elder-law practice for probate avoidance and Medicaid-sensitive planning, but the Medicaid-transfer and estate-recovery consequences should be verified for the exact fact pattern before they are treated as settled

Widely used in FL for simple real-estate-only estates.

## Probate

Florida probate:

- **Formal administration:** for estates over $75K or complex (12-18 months typical)
- **Summary administration:** estates under $75K or decedent dead 2+ years
- **Disposition without administration:** very small estates
- Florida Probate Code quite detailed

**Costs:** attorney's fee often 3% of estate value (presumed reasonable); personal representative similar. Can be negotiated.

## Intestacy

Florida Statutes § 732:

- **Spouse + no descendants or no descendants outside marriage:** all to spouse
- **Spouse + descendants all from marriage, all spouse's descendants all from marriage:** all to spouse
- **Spouse + descendants not all from marriage, or spouse with others:** 1/2 to spouse, 1/2 to descendants
- No spouse: descendants; parents; siblings; etc.

## Non-Community Property

Florida is common-law property (not community). Property acquired by each spouse separately unless titled jointly.

## Power of Attorney

Florida Statutes § 709:

- Under Fla. Stat. § 709.2108, a post-October 1, 2011 Florida POA is generally exercisable when executed; future-date or springing-contingency language is generally ineffective except for limited statutory carve-outs
- Statutory form provided but not mandatory
- **Banks cannot unreasonably refuse** — penalty for wrongful rejection
- Third-party reliance protected for good-faith acceptance
- Digital asset authorization recognized (RUFADAA)

## Healthcare Surrogate and Living Will

Separate documents:
- **Designation of healthcare surrogate** (Florida Statutes § 765)
- **Living will** separate
- **Preneed guardian** designation
- Florida has specific statutory forms

## Medicaid (Florida)

Florida is strict:
- **Income cap state** — requires Miller Trust (Qualified Income Trust) if income over cap
- **5-year lookback**
- **MERP (estate recovery)** but many exemptions
- **Community spouse protections** per federal minimums

## Tenants by Entirety (Strong)

Florida recognizes TBE for:
- Real estate
- Personal property
- Bank accounts
- Brokerage accounts

**Protects property from creditors of ONE spouse only.** Powerful asset-protection tool for married couples.

## Unique Florida Features

### Lady Bird Deed (above)

### Florida Land Trust
- Illinois-style land trust
- Privacy (beneficial owner confidential)
- Used for real estate investment

### Asset Protection
Florida has multiple strong creditor-protection features:
- Unlimited homestead (above)
- Tenants-by-entirety
- Life insurance cash value (unlimited)
- Annuities (unlimited)
- Head-of-household wages
- Inherited IRAs (recent case law)

### Snowbird Residency
Many clients split time FL/NY or FL/OH, etc. Domicile documentation critical.

## Domicile Establishment

Florida residency checklist:
- FL driver's license
- FL voter registration
- FL vehicle registration
- FL homestead exemption filing (by March 1 of year after move)
- FL tax returns (no income tax, but declaration)
- FL bank accounts
- FL doctors, dentist, clubs
- day-count and lifestyle evidence showing Florida as the real center of life
- FL address for federal tax returns
- Declaration of Domicile recorded in county

**Homestead exemption filing** is the clearest legal declaration of FL residency. Deadline: March 1. Failure loses year of eligibility.

## Pre-Move Planning (from NY, CA, MA, etc.)

1. Establish FL presence (close on home, register, etc.)
2. File Declaration of Domicile
3. File Homestead Exemption
4. Close or reduce old-state ties
5. Preserve the evidence trail long enough to withstand the former state's audit window
6. Execute new estate plan with FL law
7. Revise trusts (situs, trustee)
8. Update POAs with FL form

## Trust Situs Considerations

- Florida is not a dynasty-trust state (360-year rule)
- FL no income tax on trusts (similar to NV, SD)
- But FL doesn't have directed-trust statute as strong as SD, or DAPT like NV/SD
- Often FL residents use SD or NV trust for long-duration planning

## Current-Law Reminder

- Florida trust and probate statutes are amended often enough that old seminar outlines age badly
- Verify the live homestead, elective-share, e-will, and trust-code provisions before making an execution or relocation recommendation

## Cost of Planning

- Simple will: $500-$2,000
- Revocable trust: $2,000-$5,000
- HNW planning: $10K-$50K+
- FL has many estate attorneys; competitive pricing

## Common Failure Modes in Florida

1. **Domicile claim from FL not supported** — former state still taxing
2. **Homestead not filed by March 1** — lose year of exemption
3. **Trying to devise homestead around spouse** — invalid
4. **Lady Bird deed used wrong** — loses advantage
5. **Miller Trust missed** — Medicaid denied for over-income
6. **TBE property divided in plan** — lose protection
7. **Moving to FL but keeping NY apartment and lifestyle** — NY audits, reasserts residency
8. **Old-state POA used in FL** — bank-side friction or refusal despite facial validity
