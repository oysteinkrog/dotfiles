# Tier 4: Ultra High Net Worth (UHNW)

## Profile

- **Net worth:** ~$15M to ~$100M
- **Typical profile:** founders post-exit, C-suite at large company, successful private equity / hedge fund professional, multi-generational family wealth, real estate developer, physicians who also invest, trust beneficiaries who've grown the assets
- **Primary concerns:** federal estate tax (significant tax at this level), generational transfer optimization, asset protection, family governance, charitable integration, international planning, state tax arbitrage

## Core Document Checklist

**All Tier 3 documents, plus planning modules that often deserve modeling at this level:**
- Multi-generational dynasty trust (GST exempt) when long-horizon family retention is actually desired
- IDGT sale for growth assets when the growth / AFR spread and cash-flow mechanics justify it
- SLAT or other spousal-access planning with careful non-reciprocity analysis
- GRATs (often rolling or layered) for volatile or high-growth holdings when mortality and asset behavior support it
- ILIT with large policy when liquidity, equalization, or estate-tax funding needs justify the overhead
- Private trust company or family-directed trustee structure once scale, trust count, and governance capacity support it
- Family limited partnership (FLP) or LLC for investment assets when governance and valuation goals are real, not cosmetic
- Comprehensive charitable structure (private foundation, DAF, CRT, CLT, or hybrids) matched to actual philanthropic goals
- Formal family governance (family council, investment committee, next-gen education) when wealth is large enough to create governance risk, not just tax risk
- Trust situs analysis in NV/SD/DE/WY/TN or similar jurisdictions when duration, tax, privacy, or directed-trust features matter
- Domicile analysis and planning
- Art, wine, yacht, aircraft, and other specialty-asset planning
- Pre-immigration or expatriation planning when non-U.S. facts are in play
- Generation-skipping transfer tax planning

## Decisions to Make at This Tier

### 1. Maximum use of exemption under current law

At $50M+ net worth, the federal exclusion is still not enough to remove transfer-tax risk. The old near-term sunset story is stale, but delayed exemption deployment can still be very expensive once growth, liquidity, and future legislative risk are modeled.

- Often model dynasty-trust, SLAT, and sale-to-trust structures against partial or full use of the current-law exemption rather than assuming full use is automatic
- Combined with GST allocation: $30M grows to $300M+ over 50 years outside transfer tax
- SLAT(s) maintain access through spouse
- IDGT sale for additional assets: sell $X to trust for note, growth above AFR escapes estate

### 2. Trust situs

For long-duration trusts, situs in favorable state is table-stakes:
- South Dakota: perpetual duration, no income tax, strong DAPT, directed-trust
- Nevada: perpetual (365 years), no income tax, strongest DAPT
- Delaware: perpetual for personalty, no income tax on non-resident trust income
- Wyoming: 1,000-year dynasty, DAPT, no income tax

Home state matters for grantor income tax (CA, NY will tax trust income even if situs moved).

### 3. Directed trust structure

Separate functions:
- **Administrative trustee** (corporate, in favorable-situs state): compliance, records
- **Investment direction**: family member or committee
- **Distribution committee**: family or independent fiduciaries
- **Trust protector**: third party with power to modify, change trustees, change situs

### 4. Family governance

- **Family mission statement**
- **Family constitution / compact** (values, succession principles)
- **Family council** (adult family members meeting regularly)
- **Investment policy statement**
- **Next-gen education** (financial literacy, preparation for wealth)
- **Annual family meeting**
- **Professional staff** (family office basics)

### 5. Charitable structure

- **Private Foundation** — 5% annual distribution, donor control, investment growth, legacy vehicle
- **Donor-Advised Fund** — simpler, immediate deduction, grant-making flexibility
- **Charitable Remainder Trust** — income stream + charity, good for concentrated stock
- **Charitable Lead Trust** — gift to family at end, charity during life (good in low-rate environments)
- **Pooled Income Fund** — charitable alternative to CRT
- **Qualified Charitable Distribution** — from IRA, up to the current annual IRS-indexed limit starting at age 70.5+

Many UHNW families run hybrid: foundation for generational charitable legacy + DAF for nimble annual gifting.

### 6. Asset protection and privacy

- Domestic Asset Protection Trust (NV, SD, DE, AK, WY)
- LLCs for each investment property or business interest
- Family limited partnership with gifting of limited interests
- Trust-owned real estate (rather than direct title)
- Privacy: Delaware/Nevada LLC anonymity

### 7. Concentrated stock / founder shares

- QSBS (Section 1202) — acquisition-date-specific exclusion rules; model the current 3 / 4 / 5-year phases and per-taxpayer caps before assuming either the old $10M / 5-year regime or the newer post-enactment regime
- Charitable stacking via CRT/DAF
- 10b5-1 plan for phased sale
- Exchange fund (non-taxable diversification)
- Gift to dynasty trust pre-IPO (valuation opportunity)

### 8. International / foreign assets

- Foreign trust reporting (Form 3520, 3520-A)
- FBAR (FinCEN 114)
- PFIC (foreign mutual fund) rules
- Non-US spouse QDOT / treaty / portability planning
- Pre-immigration trust if coming to US
- Expatriation planning (covered expatriate rules)

### 9. Private aviation, yachts, etc.

- Lease-back structure (depreciation + operational control)
- LLC ownership (liability)
- State registration tax arbitrage
- Estate tax inclusion vs. exclusion

## Common Profiles at This Tier

### Profile A: Tech Founder Post-Exit
- 45, married, 2 young kids, $40M net worth: $30M post-IPO cash/taxable, $5M home, $2M retirement, $3M pre-IPO shares still vesting
- **Plan (pre-exit already recommended, post-exit still doable):** SLATs funded using the full current-law exemption; dynasty trust in SD; ILIT with $10M second-to-die policy; FLP for investment assets; charitable strategy (DAF to bridge, private foundation after; maybe CRT); Roth conversion post-income drop
- **Initial cost:** $100K-$300K

### Profile B: Senior Partner at PE / Law Firm
- 55/53, married, 3 adult kids (one in business, two not), $25M net worth: $3M home, $2M vacation home, $8M retirement, $5M private equity interests (GP/LP), $5M public markets, $2M other
- **Plan:** grantor trusts for private equity interests (high-growth); credit shelter/QTIP for state tax; dynasty trust; family LLC for vacation home; succession plan for family business
- **Initial cost:** $60K-$150K

### Profile C: Multi-generational Family Wealth
- 65/63, $80M family net worth (much in trusts from parents), 4 kids (some struggling, some successful), 8 grandkids
- **Plan:** sophisticated trust modifications (decanting from old trusts to new situs); multi-generational governance; HEMS distributions with incentive provisions; dynasty + GST planning; charitable pillar; private trust company consideration
- **Initial cost:** $50K-$200K (depends on existing trust complexity)

### Profile D: Physician Group Owner
- 58/55, married, 2 college-age kids, $18M net worth: $1.5M home, $4M practice value, $2M retirement, $7M taxable (significant cap gains), $1M real estate, $2M cash and other
- **Plan:** practice succession (buy-sell with partners); grantor trust planning for practice interest; SLAT for spousal access; ILIT for debt coverage; real estate LLC; charitable integration; asset protection given malpractice exposure (DAPT in NV or SD)
- **Initial cost:** $50K-$120K

### Profile E: Professional Athlete / Entertainer
- 30, married, 2 young kids, $25M net worth but high earnings / short career, $5M home, $8M investments, concentrated contract rights
- **Plan:** domicile planning (establish in no-tax state); SLAT for spouse; dynasty trust; asset protection (career-ending injury risk); life insurance planning; post-career income planning; restrictions on discretionary spending via trust structure
- **Initial cost:** $75K-$200K

## Cost Range

- **Initial planning:** $50K-$300K depending on complexity
- **Trust funding, entity formation, appraisals:** $25K-$100K
- **Annual maintenance (attorney, CPA, trustee, appraisals):** $25K-$200K
- **Private foundation setup:** $25K-$75K (plus ongoing compliance ~$15K+/year)
- **Family office (full-service):** 30-80 bps of AUM; typically $500K+/year
- **Insurance premiums on ILIT:** often $50K-$500K/year
- **Tax-savings benefits:** commonly $10M-$100M+ over generations

ROI on sophisticated planning at this tier is typically dramatic.

- Family office administrator (if established)
- Family therapist / governance consultant (for family-dynamics issues)

## Anti-Patterns Specific to This Tier

- **Failing to model exemption deployment just because the old sunset was repealed** — growth, legislative risk, state tax, and liquidity can still make delay costly
- **Reciprocal trusts** (spouses creating nearly-identical SLATs) — IRS uncrosses them, both included in estates
- **Old trusts with stale terms** — don't use modern provisions (directed trust, protectors, decanting)
- **Ignoring grantor trust vs. non-grantor status** — different income tax and estate tax outcomes
- **No use of valuation discounts** — missing 20-40% discount opportunity on transfers
- **Private foundation used for personal / family benefit** — self-dealing penalties
- **ILIT funded but premiums paid wrong way** — triggers gift tax or inclusion
- **Art collection undocumented** — large estate-tax appraisal surprise
- **No next-gen preparation** — wealth destroyed by heirs unready for it
- **Succession plan promised but not executed** — family conflict at transition
- **Domicile claim but home state disputes** — estate taxed twice
- **International assets not reported** — massive penalties under FBAR/FATCA

## Milestones to Graduate to Tier 5

- Net worth exceeds $100M
- Multiple operating businesses
- Dynasty trust portfolios reaching $100M+
- Significant international footprint
- Family office established with professional staff
- Generational wealth clearly intended
