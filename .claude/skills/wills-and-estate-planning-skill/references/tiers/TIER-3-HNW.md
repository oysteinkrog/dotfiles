# Tier 3: High Net Worth (HNW)

## Profile

- **Net worth:** ~$3M to ~$15M
- **Typical profile:** executives, successful professionals, small business owners, inherited wealth, multiple properties, concentrated employer stock, meaningful investment portfolios
- **Primary concerns:** federal estate tax once the current-law exclusion is genuinely in play, state estate tax, generation skipping, charitable integration, business succession, asset protection, income tax coordination, and the basis-vs-transfer-tax tradeoff

## Core Document Checklist

**All Tier 2 documents, plus strategy items to evaluate rather than assume:**
- ILIT for large policies when liquidity or state/federal estate-tax modeling makes the overhead worthwhile
- Credit-shelter / bypass / QTIP trust planning when state tax, blended-family facts, or remainder-control goals justify it
- GST-exempt dynasty trust when multigenerational exemption deployment is actually part of the mission
- Grantor-trust / IDGT planning for business or high-growth assets when transfer-tax modeling supports the complexity
- Annual gifting program ($19,000/person in 2026) and 529 super-funding when the cash-flow, basis, and family-goal tradeoffs work
- Buy-sell agreement and business succession plan when a closely held business exists
- Prenup / postnup review for second marriages or meaningful separate-property planning
- Asset-protection trust (DAPT) only if liability exposure and jurisdictional analysis support it
- Non-citizen-spouse planning: QDOT / treaty / portability analysis when relevant
- Charitable planning: DAF, CRT, CLT, or simpler direct-bequest approaches as appropriate
- Coordination with wealth manager, CPA, insurance broker

## Decisions to Make at This Tier

### 1. Use of exemption intentionally under current law
**Current posture:** the federal exclusion is $15M in 2026 under current law and is indexed thereafter, but future legislation can still change the landscape.

**Strategies:**
- SLAT (Spousal Lifetime Access Trust) — transfer assets when the estate is plausibly above the current-law exclusion and family access through spouse still matters
- Dynasty trust gift (GST exempt)
- Gift to IDGT plus sale for installment note
- GRAT (for high-volatility / high-growth assets)

**Trade-offs:**
- Lose step-up in basis on gifted assets (heirs pay capital gains)
- Lose control/access to transferred assets
- Complexity and irrevocability
- State gift tax (CT only, currently)

### 2. Business succession
**If small business owner:**
- Buy-sell agreement funded by life insurance
- Family members in business vs. outside: how to treat equitably?
- Non-voting vs. voting interests
- Grantor trust sales of interests (valuation discounts for minority/lack of marketability)
- S-corp vs. C-corp succession considerations
- Key-employee planning (phantom stock, vesting trusts)

### 3. Retirement accounts post-SECURE
- Large IRAs often create 10-year distribution pressure for non-spouse heirs
- Roth conversion strategy (pay tax at lower rates now)
- Charitable remainder trust as IRA beneficiary
- QCD (Qualified Charitable Distribution) after age 70.5

### 4. Concentrated stock position
**Options:**
- Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT) — diversify, defer gain, income stream, charity remainder
- Exchange fund (private diversification vehicle)
- 10b5-1 plan for gradual diversification
- Section 1042 ESOP rollover (if private business)
- Gifting to ILIT or dynasty trust (use exemption)

### 5. Real estate holdings
**Strategies:**
- QPRT for primary or vacation residence
- FLP / LLC with gifting of interests + valuation discounts
- GRAT for appreciating investment properties
- State-appropriate Lady Bird deed or TOD deed where available and actually preferable to trust-based ownership

### 6. State estate tax
High-tax states:
- NY (cliff regime; see the current New York state file for the verified threshold)
- MA ($2M, newly raised from $1M)
- OR ($1M), WA (see the current Washington state file for the verified threshold and rates)
- MN ($3M), IL ($4M)
- CT (unified state gift/estate system; verify the current official amount in the state file before relying on it)

**Strategies:** domicile planning, credit-shelter structure, gifts to use state exemption (most states don't tax lifetime gifts), move to low-tax state.

### 7. Asset protection
- DAPT in NV, SD, AK, DE, WY
- Professional liability: malpractice, D&O coverage adequate?
- LLC structure for rental properties
- Tenants-by-entirety for marital assets (in states where recognized)

## Common Profiles at This Tier

### Profile A: Senior Executive, Public Company
- 55/53, married, 2 adult kids, $5M net worth: $2M home, $1.5M 401(k)/IRA, $1M taxable (including $400K concentrated employer stock), $500K deferred comp, $3M term life, vested but unexercised ISOs
- **Plan:** joint revocable trust with credit shelter; ILIT for $3M life insurance; 10b5-1 plan for stock diversification; Roth conversion analysis; SLAT to use exemption; beneficiary coordination with deferred comp plan
- **Total cost:** $15K-$35K initial + ongoing

### Profile B: Small Business Owner
- 52/50, married, 3 kids (one in business), $8M net worth: $700K home, $500K retirement, $5M business value, $1.5M rental real estate, $300K other
- **Plan:** buy-sell agreement with life insurance; grantor trust sale of business interests to IDGT; LLC for real estate; succession plan for child in business with offsetting equalizers for others; ILIT; revocable trust shell
- **Total cost:** $25K-$75K (business valuation + succession complexity)

### Profile C: Retired Couple, $6M
- 72/70, married, 3 kids, grandchildren, $6M net worth: $1.2M home, $2.5M IRA (husband), $1.5M IRA (wife), $500K taxable, $300K cash/CDs
- **Plan:** revised plan post-SECURE (10-yr rule); Roth conversions; QCDs; dynasty trust for grandchildren using GST exemption; credit-shelter planning for state tax; LTC insurance evaluation; dementia directive
- **Total cost:** $10K-$25K

### Profile D: Inheritance Recipient
- 45, single, inherited $4M from parents in 2024, own modest prior assets $500K
- **Plan:** trust setup, consideration of charitable goals (no kids), step-up in basis to preserve, domicile planning if moving, asset-protection considerations
- **Total cost:** $5K-$15K

### Profile E: Blended Family, HNW
- 55/50 second marriage, $4M each premarriage, 4 kids between them (2 each), combined $9M
- **Plan:** prenup with estate provisions; each revocable trust with QTIP; coordinate beneficiaries; life insurance in ILIT as equalizer; conversation about which assets stay separate vs. combine
- **Total cost:** $20K-$40K (family complexity)

## Cost Range

- **Attorney fees (initial):** $10K-$40K for comprehensive plan
- **Business-owner complexity:** +$10K-$30K
- **Trust funding and transfers:** $2K-$10K
- **Ongoing legal review (annual/triennial):** $2K-$5K
- **CPA coordination on estate/gift-tax returns (706, 709):** $1K-$5K/year
- **Corporate trustee fees (if used):** 50-100 bps on trust assets
- **Insurance premium (ILIT):** varies massively; term: low; permanent: $10K-$100K+/year

Cost-to-benefit is strongly favorable at this tier. A $30K plan that saves $2M in estate tax is clearly worth it.

## Attorney Engagement

**Look for:**
- AEP (Accredited Estate Planner) or ACTEC Fellow
- Estate planning as sole or primary practice
- Team approach (drafting attorney, tax counsel, trust administration)
- Experience with your specific issues (business, stock options, non-citizen spouse)
- Familiarity with your state's estate tax

**Typical team:**
- Estate planning attorney (quarterback)
- CPA / tax advisor
- Financial advisor (wealth manager)
- Insurance professional (if ILIT or buy-sell funded by insurance)
- Business appraiser (if business interests)
- Trust company or corporate trustee (for certain trust types)

**Fees are often flat or capped** at this tier. Hourly can surprise. Insist on scope-capped engagements.

## Anti-Patterns Specific to This Tier

- **Reflexive gifting because an older sunset-era playbook said "use exemption now"** — often wrong when basis, control, access, and current federal/state thresholds are modeled correctly
- **Over-funding a bypass trust in the post-portability world** — unnecessary complexity
- **Failing step-up basis planning** — gifts to heirs lose step-up (sometimes keep the asset)
- **Incomplete gift to intentionally defective grantor trust** — triggers estate inclusion
- **GRAT funded with wrong asset** — low appreciation kills the strategy
- **ILIT with unpaid premiums or missed Crummey notices** — creates gift tax issues
- **SLAT as reciprocal trusts** — reciprocal trust doctrine, both included in estates
- **Business succession plan with no buy-sell funding** — widow forced to sell distressed
- **Concentrated stock position held for sentimental reasons** until market crash
- **Family member trustee with no real fiduciary training** — creates liability and errors
- **Ignoring state gift tax (CT)** — lifetime gifting still taxed
- **Domicile not actually established** — two states both claim estate-tax jurisdiction

## Milestones to Graduate to Tier 4

- Net worth passes $15M-$25M
- Liquidity event (business sale, IPO)
- International assets or non-US family members
- Dynasty/multi-generational planning priority
- Founder/executive with pre-IPO stock
- Significant private equity or closely-held business value
