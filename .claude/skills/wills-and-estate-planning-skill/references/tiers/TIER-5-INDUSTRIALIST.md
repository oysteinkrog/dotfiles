# Tier 5: Industrialist / Dynasty-Scale Wealth

## Profile

- **Net worth:** $100M to $10B+
- **Typical profile:** founder of large private or public company, mega-fund GP/founder, multi-generational family industrialist, sovereign-scale fortune holder
- **Primary concerns:** dynasty-scale wealth transfer, family governance spanning generations, legacy institutions (foundations, universities), maximum optimization of exemption + GST, international complexity, privacy, philanthropy, impact investing, succession of operating businesses, avoiding family dysfunction

## Core Document Checklist

**All Tier 4 documents, plus governance and institution-building layers that often deserve deliberate modeling at dynasty scale:**
- Private trust company (family-owned) when trustee scale, control goals, and governance maturity justify replacing or supplementing outside trustees
- Family office (full-service: investment, administrative, legal, tax, insurance, lifestyle) when the family really has the asset base and personnel appetite to sustain it
- Perpetual or very long-duration dynasty-trust architecture with distribution standards matched to the family's actual philosophy
- Layered / laddered GRATs and multiple IDGTs when the asset mix and family cash-flow discipline support them
- Strategic QSBS analysis / stacking with acquisition-date-specific Section 1202 caps and holding-period rules
- Significant private foundation or other major charitable platform when the family wants an actual institution, not just a tax deduction
- Cross-border trust structures when the family is genuinely international
- Impact investing / program-related investment structures when mission investing is real rather than performative
- Prenup / postnup expectations for adult family members where family-governance culture supports that approach
- Family Constitution and related governance charters
- Succession protocols for operating businesses
- Leading-edge digital asset planning (crypto, tokenized assets, digital identity, NIL / IP control at scale)
- Memorialization / legacy project only if it fits the family's actual values and governance priorities

## Decisions to Make at This Tier

### 1. Governance Architecture

A billion-dollar family needs an **operating structure** like a company:

- **Family Council** — elected/appointed from adult family members; governs family affairs
- **Investment Committee** — sets investment policy; may include independent members
- **Distribution Committee** — decides trust distributions; often includes non-family
- **Executive Committee** — day-to-day oversight of family office
- **Board of Advisors** — external expertise
- **Mission / Values Committee** — stewardship of family legacy
- **Next-Gen Education Committee** — prepares rising generation

This is effectively a public-company governance model applied to private family wealth.

### 2. Private Trust Company (PTC)

At $200M+ trust assets, a family may establish its own PTC instead of using corporate trustees:

**Benefits:**
- Full control over trustee function
- Family (with advisors) as directors
- Coordinated administration across many trusts
- Cost-efficient at scale
- Located in favorable state (SD, NV, WY, NH, TN)

**Requirements:**
- Minimum capital requirements (varies by state: $200K to $5M)
- Board of directors
- Qualified officers and staff
- Regulatory compliance (varies; some states lighter than others)
- Annual exam / audit

**Cost:** $250K-$2M/year to operate depending on structure.

### 3. Succession of operating businesses

For continuing operating companies:

- **Voting trusts** to concentrate control
- **Dual-class stock** (founders retain voting, family holds economic)
- **Family employment policies** (who can work in the business; under what conditions)
- **Performance reviews** for family employees
- **Exit provisions** for family members who leave the business
- **Board composition** (family, independent, professional)
- **Equalization** for non-business-active family members (cash, other assets, insurance)

### 4. Philanthropy at scale

- **Private Foundation:** 5% payout; governance structure; investment pool; program staff
- **Donor-Advised Fund(s):** supplements foundation for flexibility
- **Charitable Lead Trust** — significant in low-rate environments
- **Impact investments** — mission-aligned investing with PRI/MRI
- **Named institutional giving** — buildings, chairs, programs
- **Supporting organizations** — intermediate between DAF and private foundation
- **Community foundation partnerships**

Families at this tier often give hundreds of millions to billions to charity over their generations.

### 5. Privacy and security

- **Asset anonymization** via NV/DE LLCs, trust structures
- **Personal security** (executive protection, residence security, travel security)
- **Media management** (personal foundations, controlled communications)
- **Social-media policies** for family members (especially minor grandchildren)
- **Kidnap & ransom insurance**
- **Prenup confidentiality clauses**
- **Non-disclosure for staff** (family office, household)

### 6. International planning

If any international nexus:
- **Dual-domicile planning**
- **Treaty planning** (US tax treaties with jurisdiction of interest)
- **Non-US family members** — separate planning track
- **Foreign property** — titled, managed, reported correctly
- **Pre-immigration / expatriation planning**
- **Offshore trusts** in favorable jurisdictions (Cayman, Jersey, Singapore) for non-US beneficiaries
- **Reporting compliance:** 3520, 3520-A, 5471, 8938, FBAR, etc.

### 7. Next-generation preparation

The #1 destroyer of dynasty wealth is unprepared heirs.

- **Financial literacy curriculum** from age 10+
- **Values and mission education**
- **Philanthropic engagement** (junior board, matching grants)
- **Work experience outside family business** (required often)
- **Mentors outside family**
- **Therapist or coach** (growing up with wealth is complicated)
- **Age-staggered trust distributions with incentives** (work, education, milestone achievements)

## Common Profiles at This Tier

### Profile A: Founder, Still-Operating Tech Business
- 60, married, 4 kids (2 in business, 2 not), $500M net worth: $400M company (still 40% ownership), $50M public stock, $30M real estate, $20M other
- **Plan:** dynasty trust + GST allocation; IDGT sale of company interests; SLAT × 2 for spousal access; private foundation ($50M+); business succession with dual-class stock; family constitution; family office; next-gen education and employment policies
- **Initial planning:** $500K-$2M; ongoing $500K-$5M/year

### Profile B: Family Wealth Holder (Inheritor-Industrialist)
- 55/53, inherited $300M from parents, grew to $800M via investments, 3 kids (college age)
- **Plan:** restructure old family trusts (decanting); modern governance structure; multi-billion-dollar philanthropic foundation; private trust company; dynasty planning for grandchildren; sophisticated charitable strategy
- **Initial:** $1M-$3M; ongoing $500K-$3M/year

### Profile C: Public Company CEO
- 55, married, 3 kids, $250M via founder stock + compensation, much in public stock still
- **Plan:** 10b5-1 gradual diversification; charitable stacking; SLAT + dynasty trust; ILIT with $50M+ policy; GRAT strategy for stock appreciation; family office establishment
- **Initial:** $300K-$800K

### Profile D: Private Equity Founder, Mid-50s
- 55, married, 2 kids, $1B+ in various GP interests across funds, complex compensation (carried interest, management company equity)
- **Plan:** carried interest planning (grantor trust pre-waterfall); management-company equity planning; dynasty trust structure; full-service family office; impact investing program; significant philanthropy
- **Initial:** $500K-$2M

### Profile E: Multi-Generational Family Office
- 7th-generation family, $3B+ spread across dozens of trusts, 60+ family members across 4 active generations
- **Plan:** ongoing; family governance is primary challenge; generational transitions; philanthropic legacy continuity; succession of family office leadership; rising-generation integration
- **Ongoing:** $5M-$20M/year in professional services + family office operating cost

## Cost Range

- **Initial sophisticated planning:** $500K-$5M
- **Ongoing annual:** $500K-$10M+
- **Family office:** $3M-$50M/year depending on scale
- **Private trust company:** $500K-$5M/year
- **Private foundation:** $500K-$10M/year to administer (depending on giving volume)
- **External legal / tax advisory:** $500K-$5M/year

At this tier, cost is a tiny percentage of wealth. The opportunity cost of *not* optimizing far exceeds fees.

## Anti-Patterns Specific to This Tier

- **Treating wealth as only a tax problem** — ignoring governance, family dynamics, legacy
- **Patriarch/matriarch refusing to communicate** plans to rising generation until too late
- **Insufficient preparation of heirs** — "shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations" ($300M lost to heirs blowing it)
- **Overly restrictive trusts** (incentive clauses too narrow) — family conflict and litigation
- **Ignoring family black sheep** — litigation when they feel excluded
- **Family foundation used for personal benefit** — self-dealing, intermediate sanctions
- **Treating private trust company as family tool rather than regulated fiduciary** — regulatory action
- **Succession of operating business to unqualified family member** — company destroyed
- **Philanthropic strategy without mission or staff** — impact dilution, family conflict
- **International structures without full tax reporting** — massive FBAR/FATCA penalties
- **Celebrity / high-profile family member** creating PR disasters
- **Family office staffed by loyalists rather than competent professionals** — fraud, theft, suboptimal decisions

## Philosophical Considerations

At this tier, the question isn't "how do I minimize tax?" but:

- **What are the family's values?**
- **How much is enough to pass to heirs?** (Many industrialists give the bulk to charity.)
- **What legacy institutions do we build?**
- **How do we prepare multiple generations?**
- **What impact do we want to have on the world?**
- **How do we avoid being destroyed by our own wealth?**

Warren Buffett, Bill Gates: "the giving pledge" — give the majority to charity. Founder families must wrestle with similar questions.
