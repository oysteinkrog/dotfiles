# Comprehensive Intake Questionnaire

This is the **full question bank**. Do **not** ask all of these in one session. The interviewer (you, Claude) selects relevant questions based on the adaptive flow in [INTERVIEW-FLOW.md](../methodology/INTERVIEW-FLOW.md), the user's energy, and the actual fact pattern.

Each question has a `// trigger` and `// drives` annotation explaining when it is usually useful and what it informs. Treat the trigger labels as prompts, not commands. A question marked `always` means "normally important," not "ask mechanically no matter what."

---

## Section A — Orientation

A1. What made you decide to do this now? *// trigger: always; drives: tone, urgency, focus*

A2. Have you done any prior estate planning? Do you have an existing will, trust, POA, or healthcare directive? When? In what state? *// trigger: always; drives: revision vs. fresh start*

A3. If yes — where are the originals stored? Who knows where? *// trigger: prior plan exists*

A4. What's your age and date of birth? *// trigger: always; drives: tier and life-stage routing*

A5. What state do you consider home? Where is your driver's license issued? Where do you vote? *// trigger: always; drives: domicile*

A6. Are you a U.S. citizen? Permanent resident? Other status? *// trigger: always; drives: estate-tax exposure*

A7. Are there any health diagnoses or events that bring urgency to this — recent diagnosis, hospitalization, family history? *// trigger: always; drives: capacity documentation, expedited timeline*

A8. Is there anyone you'd like in the room (spouse, partner, adult child) for these conversations, or is this just you? *// trigger: always; drives: session structure*

## Section B — Marital and Partnership

B1. What's your current marital status? (single, married, divorced, separated, widowed, unmarried partnership) *// trigger: always*

B2. If married:
- B2a. Spouse's full legal name, age, citizenship status?
- B2b. How long have you been married?
- B2c. Is this your first marriage? Theirs?
- B2d. Did you have a prenup or postnup? Where is it?
- B2e. Are you in a community-property state (AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI)? Did you previously live in one?

B3. If divorced:
- B3a. When was the divorce finalized? In what state?
- B3b. Do you have a copy of the divorce decree? Does it require maintaining life insurance, alimony, anything ongoing?
- B3c. Do you have any children with the ex-spouse?
- B3d. Have you updated all beneficiary designations since the divorce?

B4. If separated but not divorced:
- B4a. How long have you been separated?
- B4b. Is divorce planned, in progress, or unlikely?
- B4c. What's the current state of joint accounts, joint property, beneficiary designations?

B5. If widowed:
- B5a. When did your spouse die?
- B5b. Was a Form 706 filed for portability? *// drives: DSUE preservation*
- B5c. Is the prior spouse's estate fully settled?

B6. If unmarried partner:
- B6a. How long have you been together?
- B6b. Is there a cohabitation agreement, domestic partnership registration, or civil union?
- B6c. Did you live in a state recognizing common-law marriage during your relationship?
- B6d. Have you titled assets jointly? Named each other as beneficiaries?
- B6e. Same-sex couple — when did you marry vs. when did the relationship begin?
- B6f. Polyamorous household with multiple partners — are all partners legally recognized in any way?

B7. Future possibilities:
- B7a. Likelihood of remarriage in next 5 years?
- B7b. If you remarried, how would your priorities change?

## Section C — Children and Descendants

C1. Do you have any children? *// trigger: always*

For each child:
- C2. Full legal name, date of birth?
- C3. From this relationship or a prior one?
- C4. Biological, adopted, or stepchild raised but not adopted?
- C5. Citizenship/residency?
- C6. Marital status, dependents of their own?
- C7. Financial situation — independent? Dependent on you? In financial distress?
- C8. Health — any chronic conditions, disabilities, mental illness, addiction, severe special needs?
- C9. Receiving any government benefits — SSI, Medicaid, Section 8, SNAP, VA, disability?
- C10. Any concerning relationship — predatory spouse, controlling partner?
- C11. Any history of substance abuse, financial irresponsibility, gambling, legal issues?
- C12. Pending or recent bankruptcy or major debt?
- C13. Quality of relationship with you?
- C14. Likely heir of the family business (if applicable)?
- C15. Actively involved in any family enterprise?

C16. Are there any children **not** legally yours that you want to provide for? Stepchildren, godchildren, mentees, children you helped raise?

C17. Any children that don't know you exist — possibility of unknown biological children from earlier relationships? *// trigger: with discretion; drives: explicit pretermitted-heir clauses*

C18. Any frozen embryos, sperm, eggs in cryostorage? Any plans for posthumous reproduction? *// trigger: relevant age + ART history*

C19. Any children adopted or born after your last estate plan?

C20. If children are minors:
- C20a. Who would you want as guardian if both parents died? Backup?
- C20b. Have you discussed this with the proposed guardian?
- C20c. Is the proposed guardian in a different state? Would the children move?
- C20d. Should the guardian of the person be the same as the trustee for the children's money?
- C20e. At what age should each child receive control of their inheritance?

## Section D — Other Family

D1. Are your parents living? *// trigger: always*

For each living parent:
- D2. Age, health status?
- D3. Are they financially independent or do you support them?
- D4. Do you expect to inherit from them? Have they done their estate planning?
- D5. Do they have long-term care needs now or expected?

D6. Siblings:
- D7. For each: name, age, relationship quality, financial situation, any disabilities or struggles?
- D8. Any siblings you'd want to include in your estate plan?
- D9. Any sibling relationships that might cause conflict among your children?

D10. Other relatives or close non-family:
- D11. Nieces, nephews, godchildren you want to remember?
- D12. Long-term employees, caregivers, friends?
- D13. Mentees, students, colleagues?

## Section E — Charitable Intent

E1. Do you currently make charitable donations? Approximately how much per year?

E2. Are there specific organizations that matter deeply to you?

E3. Religious institution, alma mater, cause?

E4. Would you like to leave a portion of your estate to charity? *// trigger: indicates philanthropic intent*

E5. Specific dollar amount, percentage, or specific asset?

E6. Are you interested in a more structured vehicle — donor-advised fund, private foundation, charitable trust?

E7. Do you want your children involved in the philanthropy after you're gone?

E8. Any specific naming, restriction, or recognition concerns?

## Section F — Real Estate

F1. Do you own a primary residence? *// trigger: always*

F2. If yes:
- F2a. Address, approximate market value?
- F2b. Mortgage balance? Lender? Interest rate?
- F2c. How is title held? (Sole, joint tenancy, tenancy by entirety, community property, trust, LLC)
- F2d. Any liens, easements, or encumbrances?
- F2e. Recent appraisal?

F3. Do you have a reverse mortgage? *// drives: special death rules*

F4. Vacation home or second home?
- F4a. Same questions as F2
- F4b. State where located (matters for ancillary probate)
- F4c. Used by family members, rented, both?

F5. Other real estate?
- F5a. Rental properties — number, location, ownership structure (LLC?), management?
- F5b. Land, undeveloped property?
- F5c. Mineral, oil, gas, water, timber rights?
- F5d. Conservation easements?

F6. Foreign real estate?
- F6a. Country, value, titling, local-law issues, forced-heirship exposure?

F7. Time shares?
- F7a. Where, ongoing fees, intent (keep / disclaim)?

F8. Cooperative apartment (NYC, Chicago)?
- F8a. Co-op corporation rules on inheritance and transfer

## Section G — Financial Accounts

G1. Banking:
- G1a. Checking, savings, money market — institution, approximate balance, titling, TOD/POD designation?
- G1b. CDs?
- G1c. Safe deposit box — institution, location, who has access?

G2. Taxable brokerage/investment accounts:
- G2a. Institution, approximate balance, titling, TOD designation?
- G2b. Margin loan against the account?
- G2c. Concentrated single-stock position (>20% of total)?
- G2d. Highly appreciated low-basis legacy holdings?
- G2e. Pledged-asset line (collateralized lending)?

G3. Retirement accounts (401(k), IRA, Roth, SEP, SIMPLE, 403(b), 457, pension, nonqualified deferred comp):
- G3a. For each: institution, approximate balance, named primary and contingent beneficiaries, when last updated?
- G3b. Are any pre-tax vs. Roth? *// drives: charitable bequest strategy*
- G3c. Any required minimum distributions in progress?
- G3d. Inherited IRAs?

G4. Health Savings Account (HSA):
- G4a. Balance, beneficiary, plans for use during life?

G5. 529 plans:
- G5a. For each: account owner, beneficiary, successor owner or other plan-designated successor, approximate balance?

G6. Annuities:
- G6a. Type (immediate, deferred, variable, fixed, indexed), beneficiary?

G7. Cash value life insurance:
- G7a. Owner, insured, beneficiary, death benefit, cash value, type?

G8. Series EE/I bonds, Treasury direct holdings? *// often forgotten*

## Section I — Business Interests

I1. Do you own any portion of a private business? *// trigger: always*

For each business interest:
- I2. Business name, type of entity (sole prop, partnership, LLC, S-corp, C-corp, professional corp)?
- I3. Your ownership percentage?
- I4. Approximate value? Last formal valuation?
- I5. Other owners — names, relationship, ownership?
- I6. Buy-sell agreement in place? Funded by life insurance?
- I7. Operating agreement / shareholders' agreement — does it restrict transfer at death?
- I8. Are any family members already involved in the business?
- I9. Identified successor? Trained?
- I10. Industry, key dependencies on you specifically?
- I11. Personal guarantees you've signed for the business?
- I12. Are there environmental liabilities, regulatory issues, pending litigation?

I13. Investment partnership interests:
- I14. Hedge fund, PE, VC, real estate syndicate?
- I15. For each: fund name, commitment, called/uncalled capital, GP, illiquidity period, transfer restrictions?
- I16. Carried interest holdings?
- I17. Any K-1 partnerships?

## Section P — Goals and Values

P1. Imagine 30 years from now. What do you hope is still true because of decisions you make in this plan?

P2. If you had to summarize your hopes for your family in one sentence, what would it be?

P3. Do you want to leave equal shares to children, or is "equitable" different from "equal" in your case? Why?

P4. Are there particular life lessons, values, or stories you want to pass down?

P5. Are there specific messages you'd want to leave for individual people?

P6. How do you want to be remembered?

P7. Are there family members you want to specifically include or specifically exclude, and why?

P8. Anyone you want to apologize to or thank?

P9. What's your biggest fear about how this plays out after you're gone?

P10. What's the worst-case scenario you're trying to prevent?

## Section Q — Incapacity and End-of-Life

Q1. If you couldn't manage your finances tomorrow due to illness or injury, who would you want to step in? Backup?

Q2. If you couldn't speak for yourself in the hospital, who would you want making medical decisions? Backup?

Q3. Are these the same person or different? Why?

Q4. Have you discussed these roles with the people you'd want to name?

Q5. End-of-life preferences — for each scenario, what would you want?
- Q5a. Terminal illness with no chance of recovery — ventilator? Feeding tube? CPR? Hospice?
- Q5b. Permanent unconsciousness — same questions
- Q5c. Severe dementia, physically healthy — aggressive treatment? Antibiotics? Hand-feeding? Memory care vs. home?

Q6. Are you a registered organ donor?

Q7. Body donation to research or anatomical study?

Q8. Disposition of remains:
- Q8a. Burial (where, what type — traditional, green)?
- Q8b. Cremation (and what to do with the cremains)?
- Q8c. Newer options — aquamation, natural organic reduction (composting), space burial?
- Q8d. Religious requirements?

Q9. Funeral preferences:
- Q9a. Service style — religious, secular, celebration of life?
- Q9b. Specific music, readings, speakers?
- Q9c. Location?
- Q9d. Pre-paid arrangements?

Q10. Any cemetery plot, mausoleum, family plot already owned?

Q11. Have you ever been hospitalized for a mental-health episode? *// drives: psychiatric advance directive*

Q12. Family history of dementia, Alzheimer's, severe mental illness?

Q13. Substance-use history? *// drives: protective trust if relapse possible*

Q14. Dementia-specific scenarios: if you reached late-stage Alzheimer's, what would you want?

Q15. Are there specific people you'd want barred from visiting you in the hospital, or making decisions on your behalf?

Q16. Are there specific people you'd want notified at certain stages — first hospitalization, terminal diagnosis, death?

## Section S — Fiduciary Selection

S1. **Executor (personal representative)** — primary candidate, why?
- S1a. Backup executor?
- S1b. Have you asked them?
- S1c. Are they willing? Capable? Local?
- S1d. Should the will waive the bond requirement?
- S1e. Statutory executor fee or waived?
- S1f. Co-executors with corporate trustee for complexity?

S2. **Trustee** for any trust to be created — primary candidate?
- S2a. Backup trustee?
- S2b. Independent corporate trustee considered?
- S2c. Trust protector?
- S2d. Investment committee separate from administrative trustee?

S3. **Guardian for minor children** (if applicable) — primary candidate?
- S3a. Backup?
- S3b. Have you asked? Discussed?
- S3c. Any geographic disruption to children?
- S3d. Different person for guardian-of-person vs. guardian-of-property?

S4. **Durable financial POA** — agent? Backup?

S5. **Healthcare agent** — agent? Backup?

S6. **Digital fiduciary** — same as executor or different?

S7. **IP/literary executor** if creator portfolio?

S8. Any family members you would specifically want NOT to be a fiduciary?

## Section T — Communication and Transparency

T1. Have you ever discussed your estate plans with your family? When? What was the response?

T2. Do you intend to hold a family meeting?

T3. Are you comfortable telling your heirs the broad shape of the plan, or do you want it to be private until your death?

T4. Are there any decisions in your plan that would surprise your heirs? Why?

T5. Any siblings of your heirs (your children's spouses) you want to consider?

T6. Are there family secrets or histories that should be acknowledged in the planning conversation?

T7. Would you participate in a recorded interview to capture your stories and values for an ethical will?

## Section U — Practical Logistics

U1. Where will the originals of your documents be stored?

U2. Who will know they exist and where to find them?

U3. Do you maintain a "death binder" or "if I die tomorrow" file?

U4. Where are your passwords stored? Is the recovery path for the password manager or vault documented separately?

U5. Where is the seed phrase for any cryptocurrency wallet?

U6. Who has access to your safe deposit box?

U7. Who knows the combination to any home safe?

U8. Do you have a current advisor team — attorney, CPA, financial advisor?

U9. Are they listed in your letter of instruction?

U10. When was your plan last reviewed? Last fully refreshed?

U11. What review cadence makes sense for you going forward?
