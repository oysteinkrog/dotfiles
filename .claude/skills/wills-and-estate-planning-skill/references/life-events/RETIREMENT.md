# Retirement

Retirement changes liquidity, dependency patterns, account composition, and incapacity risk.

## Immediate Priorities

- Re-check beneficiaries on retirement accounts and annuities
- Revisit long-term-care planning and incapacity logistics
- Revisit spending support for surviving spouse / partner
- Reassess whether old life-insurance structures still make sense

## Questions To Ask

- What income streams begin or end at retirement?
- Is the house still appropriate for aging in place?
- Are adult children becoming more financially relevant?
- Does retirement increase the risk of cognitive decline planning becoming urgent?

## Planning Adaptations

- Simplify where simplification genuinely reduces failure risk
- Review trusts as beneficiary of retirement assets with SECURE-Act sensitivity
- Re-run liquidity and survivor-income analysis
- Add practical access planning for bills, caregiver hiring, and medical decision-making

## Documents To Collect

- Retirement-account statements and beneficiary records
- Pension election paperwork
- Social Security and annuity income information
- Long-term-care and disability coverage materials

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming the plan from peak earning years still fits retirement reality
- Ignoring beneficiary drift on large IRA balances
- Delaying incapacity-document refresh until after decline is obvious
