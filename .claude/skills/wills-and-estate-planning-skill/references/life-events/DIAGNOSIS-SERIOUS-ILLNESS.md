# Life Event: Serious Illness Diagnosis

A serious diagnosis (cancer, ALS, Alzheimer's, organ failure) compresses the estate-planning timeline from "someday" to "now." Priorities shift to activating incapacity plans, executing last-minute planning, and ensuring the plan can be administered by loved ones.

## Immediate Priorities (First Week)

### Capacity Question
If diagnosis involves cognitive decline (dementia, brain tumor, mental illness):
- Assess current capacity
- Document capacity NOW with attorney/physician
- Execute documents while capacity is clear

### Emergency Team Assembly
- Estate attorney (if existing; find one quickly if not)
- Healthcare agent (confirm willingness, discuss wishes)
- Financial POA (confirm willingness)
- Executor / trustee (confirm willingness)
- Primary caregiver (identify, arrange)

### Document Audit
- Locate all existing estate documents
- Verify they're current and executed
- Identify gaps

### Immediate Updates
- Healthcare POA — still alive? Still appropriate?
- HIPAA — who can access records?
- Financial POA — is it effective now?
- Living will — does it still reflect wishes?

## Within First Month

### POLST / MOLST
For patients whose current illness or frailty makes bedside medical orders important:
- Discuss with physician
- Bright-colored form signed by physician
- Covers: CPR / no CPR, aggressive vs. comfort care, antibiotics, feeding tube
- Distribute: wallet, refrigerator, PCP, hospital, family, nursing home if applicable

### Update / Create Core Documents
If not already done:
- [x] Will
- [x] Revocable trust (if desired)
- [x] Financial POA (immediate)
- [x] Healthcare POA
- [x] Living will / advance directive
- [x] HIPAA authorization
- [x] POLST / MOLST if current illness or frailty makes bedside medical orders appropriate
- [x] Dementia directive (if dementia)
- [x] Disposition of remains document

### Beneficiary Audit
- All retirement accounts
- All life insurance
- All POD/TOD accounts
- Trust provisions
- Verify all current

### Cost Coverage Planning
- Health insurance confirmation
- LTC insurance claim initiation (if applicable)
- Disability insurance claim
- Medicaid planning if applicable (crisis mode)

### Communicate
- Family meeting (see FAMILY-COMMUNICATION.md)
- Executor / trustee briefed on role
- Healthcare agent briefed on preferences
- Guardian (if minor kids) briefed

## Specific Illness Considerations

### Cancer
- **Treatment decisions** — documented preferences
- **Clinical trial participation** — authorization
- **Hospice planning** — when to transition
- **Fertility preservation** if treatment affects (for younger patients)
- **Pain management preferences**

### Dementia (Alzheimer's, Other)
- **Dementia-specific directive** (not covered by standard living will)
- **Stopping artificial nutrition/hydration** when can't recognize family
- **Hand-feeding** decisions
- **Capacity documentation** urgent while still capable
- **Memory care facility** consideration

### ALS / Degenerative Neurological
- **Feeding tube** decision (time-limited trial vs. permanent)
- **Ventilator** decision
- **Tracheostomy** decision
- **Quality of life thresholds** documented
- **If the user is considering medical aid in dying, verify the current law and procedural requirements in the relevant state immediately** rather than relying on a memory list

### Heart / Organ Failure
- **Transplant list** considerations
- **Mechanical assist device** (LVAD, etc.)
- **Dialysis** decisions
- **ICU vs. hospice** preferences

### Terminal Pediatric
- Different decision-making (parents, not patient)
- Research participation
- Memorial planning sensitivity

## Rapid-Response Estate Planning

### If Substantial Assets + Short Timeline
- **Lifetime transfers only after modeling basis, capacity, control, liquidity, and timing risk** — avoid reflexive last-minute gifting
- **ILIT work is fact-specific** — transferring an existing policy shortly before death can fail because of the 3-year rule, so do not treat ILIT setup as a generic bedside fix
- **Accelerate charitable giving** — deduction now, at lifetime peak of giving
- **Roth conversions only if bracket math and time horizon justify them** — serious illness does not automatically make conversion wise
- **Basis-step-up considerations** — holding appreciated assets vs. selling
- **Medicaid planning** if appropriate (crisis mode)

### If Deathbed Planning (Days / Weeks)
- Focus on immediate access and administration, not optimization
- Ensure executor has everything needed
- Clear family communication
- Palliative focus

## Medicaid Crisis Planning

If long-term care needed imminently and insufficient wealth:

- **Spousal protections** maximum use
- **Exempt-asset conversion** (pay off mortgage, home improvements)
- **Miller Trust** if in income-cap state
- **Personal care contract** for family caregiver
- **State-specific spousal-transfer / revised dispositive-plan work** only with elder-law counsel who understands the local crisis-planning rules
- Engage CELA (Certified Elder Law Attorney) immediately

See [MEDICAID-PLANNING.md](../incapacity/MEDICAID-PLANNING.md).

## Ethical Will / Legacy Work

With compressed timeline, focus on:
- **The Four Things** (Byock): forgive me, I forgive you, thank you, I love you
- **Video/audio recordings** for loved ones
- **Stories to preserve**
- **Blessings and messages**

Often the most meaningful work, separate from legal plan.

## Ongoing Management During Illness

### Financial Management
- POA activated (or self-managed if able)
- Bill pay automation
- Financial advisor communication
- Spousal / family oversight

### Medical Management
- Healthcare agent on call
- HIPAA authorizations for all providers
- Coordinated care (geriatric care manager if complex)
- Palliative vs. curative choices

### Emotional Management
- Therapy / counseling for patient and family
- Hospice chaplain (if applicable)
- Support groups
- Caregiver respite

## Hospice Transition

When curative treatment stops:
- Medicare Hospice Benefit (at-home or facility)
- Coverage: medications, equipment, nursing, chaplain, social work, bereavement
- Can continue 6+ months in many cases
- Comfort-focused care

Hospice should trigger a reread of the current POLST, advance-directive language, and bedside goals-of-care documentation.

## End-of-Life

### Medical Aid in Dying (where legal)
Requirements vary by jurisdiction and change over time. Verify the current state-law map and the local procedural rules before treating this as an option.

Common requirements include:
- Terminal diagnosis (6 months or less)
- Two physician certifications
- Two requests (written + oral, separated in time)
- Mental capacity
- Self-administration of medication
- Residency in state

### Home Death vs. Hospital vs. Hospice Facility
Document preferences; arrange accordingly.

## Common Failure Modes During Illness

1. **Delay in updating plan** — capacity lost, plan outdated
2. **Healthcare agent not briefed** — second-guessing at bedside
3. **Family disagreement** without agent clearly authorized
4. **No POLST** for serious illness — hospital and EMS teams may treat under ordinary emergency protocols until current orders are available
5. **Emergency room treatment contrary to wishes** — documents not available
6. **Hospice too late** — missed comfort benefits
7. **Last-minute gifting without exemption capacity** — triggers gift tax surprises
8. **Home death wish but no plan** — ends up in hospital
9. **Family conflict over decisions** — no clear authority
10. **Legacy work deferred** — died without sharing final messages
