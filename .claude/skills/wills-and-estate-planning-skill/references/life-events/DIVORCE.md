# Life Event: Divorce

Divorce is one of the most critical estate plan triggers. The second most common source of unintended inheritances (after beneficiary neglect) is divorced spouses still listed as beneficiaries.

## Automatic Legal Changes on Divorce

Varies by state, but common:
- **Will provisions favoring ex-spouse** usually revoked by statute in most states
- **Joint tenancy** may be severed by statute, decree, or follow-up deed work depending on the state and the asset
- **Beneficiary designations** — VARIES; many jurisdictions DO NOT revoke automatically
- **Powers of attorney** — revocation rules vary more than people assume; do not rely on automatic revocation
- **Healthcare proxy** — same; revoke and replace actively
- **Pre-marital agreement** terms typically still apply
- **Trust beneficiary provisions** may or may not be affected depending on governing law and document structure; review directly rather than assuming

**Dangerous defaults:** ex-spouse often still named on:
- 401(k), IRA, pension
- Life insurance
- Bank accounts (POD)
- Brokerage (TOD)
- Healthcare proxy
- Financial POA
- Trust (as trustee or beneficiary)

## Urgent Updates (Within 30 Days of Final Divorce)

### Beneficiary Designations
- [x] All 401(k) plans (current and prior employers)
- [x] All IRAs
- [x] Life insurance (employer group term, personal policies)
- [x] Bank accounts with POD
- [x] Brokerage with TOD
- [x] Annuities

**Warning:** Federal ERISA law preempts many state automatic-revocation rules for ERISA retirement plans. Even if state law says ex-spouse should be treated as revoked, the plan administrator may still follow the plan's beneficiary designation unless the federal-law path says otherwise. Update actively.

### Will and Trust
- [x] Revoke old will, create new
- [x] Amend trust or restate
- [x] Update guardian nominations for minor children
- [x] Update executor (if ex was named)
- [x] Update trustee (if ex was named)

### Powers of Attorney
- [x] Revoke old financial POA (send notice to institutions)
- [x] Create new financial POA
- [x] Revoke old healthcare POA
- [x] Create new healthcare POA
- [x] Revoke HIPAA authorization (or update)

### Titling
- [x] Sever joint tenancy property (often required by divorce decree)
- [x] Retitle accounts now single
- [x] Document property division per decree

## Intersection with Divorce Decree

### Life Insurance Required by Decree
- Many decrees require maintaining life insurance for:
  - Child support (while kids minors)
  - Spousal maintenance (term of payments)
- Ex-spouse may be required beneficiary
- Check decree carefully before "removing" ex

### QDRO (Qualified Domestic Relations Order)
- Court order dividing qualified retirement plan
- Separate from divorce decree
- **Required** for dividing 401(k), 403(b), pensions
- Ex-spouse becomes alternate payee with own benefit
- Confirm QDRO executed and plan accepted

### IRA Division
- Divided via "transfer incident to divorce" (not QDRO)
- Must be correct documentation to avoid tax
- Each ex-spouse ends with own IRA

### Split of Real Estate
- Title transfer per decree
- Deed changes
- Mortgage assumption or refinance
- Cost basis: carryover (not step-up) on divorce transfer

### Alimony / Maintenance
- Post-2019 (TCJA): no longer deductible to payer or income to payee
- Impacts cash flow planning
- May require life insurance for security

### Child Support
- Lifetime obligation until kids adult
- Estate obligation if payer dies before kids adult (in some states)
- Life insurance typically required

## Specific Estate Planning Considerations

### Guardian for Minor Children
- Usually ex-spouse has custody rights
- If ex-spouse dies, your guardian nomination activates
- Some parents nominate new primary (grandparent, sibling) to prevent ex's new spouse / family from custody
- Discuss openly with extended family

### Trust for Kids (to Protect From Other Parent)
- Establish trust for kids with independent trustee
- Other parent cannot access
- Specifies how funds used for kids
- Continues past majority if desired

### Prenup / Postnup for New Relationship
- If re-partnering, new prenup
- Address prior-marriage kids
- Consider QTIP for new spouse / kids from prior

### Premarital Agreement Still Applicable
- Pre-existing prenup typically still controls
- Property division per prenup
- But child support not addressable in prenup

## Post-Divorce Estate Plan

### Typical Rebuild
1. **Revoke prior documents**: will, trust, POAs
2. **Create new will**: new beneficiaries, executor, guardian
3. **Create new trust** (if applicable): funded with post-divorce assets
4. **New POAs**: financial + healthcare
5. **New HIPAA**
6. **New disposition-of-remains document**
7. **Audit all beneficiary designations**
8. **Retitle real estate** per decree
9. **Letter of instruction** updated
10. **Digital asset inventory** updated (remove shared access, rotate credentials, and update recovery contacts)

### Special Considerations

**If You Have Kids:**
- Guardian carefully chosen (may not be ex's family)
- Trust for kids with independent trustee
- Life insurance continues (per decree and for their benefit)

**If Business Ownership:**
- Buy-sell implications if ex co-owned
- Valuation and division documented
- New operating agreements without ex

**If Real Estate:**
- Divorce decree property division
- Retitle per decree
- Refinance if mortgage joint

**If Joint Debt:**
- Divorce decree allocation
- Credit card responsibility
- Mortgage / HELOC continuation

## Health Insurance After Divorce

- COBRA for up to 36 months (typically)
- ACA marketplace for longer-term
- Kids often covered via one parent's plan (per decree)

## Retirement Account Considerations

### Inherited IRA Rules (If Ex Was Beneficiary)
- If ex is named and you die before updating: ex inherits
- Usually not what's wanted

### Post-Divorce Roth Conversion
- Often makes sense in year of divorce (lower income, filing status change)
- Tax planning for both ex-spouses

## Common Failure Modes

1. **Beneficiary not updated** — ex inherits everything despite new will
2. **ERISA plan beneficiary** stays with ex despite state automatic-revocation
3. **Healthcare POA with ex-spouse** — ex makes medical decisions during emergency
4. **Joint tenancy not severed** — ex ends up with your share
5. **QDRO not executed** — retirement split pending years
6. **No life insurance for child support** — kids uncovered if payer dies
7. **New spouse issue** — new marriage but old prenup no longer applies
8. **Trust naming ex as trustee** — ex managing your kids' inheritance
9. **Emergency contacts still ex** — at work, medical providers
10. **Digital accounts with ex's email** as recovery
11. **Storage unit / safe deposit box** still joint
12. **Post-marital assets traced to premarital asset** — commingling issues

## Timeline

### Day of Final Divorce Decree
- Beneficiary updates (most urgent)

### Within 30 Days
- Comprehensive beneficiary audit
- Revoke POAs
- Sever joint tenancy
- Update emergency contacts

### Within 90 Days
- New will
- New trust (if applicable)
- New POAs
- New HIPAA

### Within 6 Months
- Full estate plan review
- Tax planning for divorce year
- Insurance review
- Retitling complete
