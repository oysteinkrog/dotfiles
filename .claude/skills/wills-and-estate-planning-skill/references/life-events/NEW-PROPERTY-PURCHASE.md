# New Property Purchase

Real estate is where title mechanics quietly overwhelm will language.

## Immediate Priorities

- Confirm how title is being taken
- Decide whether the property belongs in a revocable trust
- Review mortgage, insurance, homestead, and state-law consequences

## Questions To Ask

- Primary residence, vacation home, rental, or mixed use?
- In-state or out-of-state?
- Joint title, tenancy by the entirety, community property, LLC, or trust?
- Is a TOD deed or Lady Bird deed part of the strategy?

## Planning Adaptations

- Update the asset inventory and titling audit immediately
- If this creates a second-state footprint, review ancillary probate and domicile issues
- If it is a family-use property, address governance and exit rights now

## Documents To Collect

- Purchase contract and closing statement
- Recorded deed when available
- Mortgage / HELOC documents
- Insurance declaration page

## Common Mistakes

- Taking title in the wrong form and hoping the will fixes it later
- Forgetting to fund the revocable trust after closing
- Buying a vacation home and leaving later co-ownership rules to chance
