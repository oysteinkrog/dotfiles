# Starting a Business

Business formation introduces liability, transfer restrictions, and succession questions immediately.

## Immediate Priorities

- Identify entity type and ownership structure
- Determine whether a buy-sell or operating-agreement update is needed
- Revisit insurance, disability, and key-person planning
- Re-run the user’s personal estate plan to account for the new business asset

## Questions To Ask

- Is this a solo business or multi-owner venture?
- Are there equity promises, vesting, or cap-table complexity?
- Who would run or sell the business on incapacity or death?
- Would the family actually want to own this business?

## Planning Adaptations

- Add transfer restrictions and succession mechanics to the business review
- Separate management succession from economic inheritance where needed
- Revisit liquidity if the business becomes the dominant asset
- If founder equity could become very large, route to advanced-planning and tax overlays

## Documents To Collect

- Entity formation documents
- Operating agreement / shareholders agreement
- Cap table
- Insurance policies
- Buy-sell drafts, if any

## Common Mistakes

- Building business value before creating a death / disability transfer plan
- Leaving founder equity to multiple heirs without governance or sale mechanics
- Ignoring personal guarantees and debt
