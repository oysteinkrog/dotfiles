# Death of a Family Member

The death of a spouse, parent, child, or named fiduciary is often both a grief event and a planning reset.

## Immediate Priorities

- Update beneficiaries and fiduciaries
- Determine whether portability, disclaimer, or trust-administration steps are time-sensitive
- Reassess the user’s own plan in light of the changed family system

## Questions To Ask

- Who died, and what role did they play in the plan?
- Were they a spouse, beneficiary, guardian, executor, trustee, or healthcare agent?
- Is there a 9-month disclaimer window or estate-tax filing deadline running?
- Does the death materially change fairness expectations among survivors?

## Planning Adaptations

- Remove the deceased person everywhere they appear
- Re-run survivor-income, liquidity, and decision-making analysis
- If first spouse died, review portability and survivor-trust structure
- If a child died, review descendant / per-stirpes assumptions

## Documents To Collect

- Death certificate
- Estate or trust documents for the deceased if relevant
- Current beneficiary confirmations
- Any portability or probate filings

## Common Mistakes

- Updating only the will and forgetting beneficiary forms
- Missing Form 706 portability timing
- Leaving a deceased person as backup fiduciary for years
