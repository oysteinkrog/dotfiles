# Inheritance Received

An inheritance often changes both the asset map and the family dynamics.

## Immediate Priorities

- Identify what was inherited and how it is titled
- Confirm basis and date-of-death value where relevant
- Decide whether to keep inherited assets separate or commingled
- Re-run beneficiary and tax exposure analysis

## Questions To Ask

- Was the inheritance outright, in trust, or via beneficiary designation?
- Is the inherited asset appreciated, illiquid, or emotionally charged?
- Does the inheritance push the user across a state or federal transfer-tax planning threshold?
- Is a disclaimer still available?

## Planning Adaptations

- Preserve separate-property records where that matters
- Re-check creditor and divorce exposure
- Revisit charitable or dynasty-planning options if scale changed materially
- Update the user’s own dispositive plan so the inherited wealth does not flow by accident

## Documents To Collect

- Estate closing statement or inventory if available
- Beneficiary distribution statements
- Appraisals or valuation reports
- Trust instruments for inherited trusts

## Common Mistakes

- Losing separate-property tracing immediately
- Spending or re-titling inherited assets before understanding tax and family consequences
- Assuming inherited retirement assets work like owned retirement assets
- Failing to revisit the whole estate plan after a major increase in wealth
