# Moving States

An interstate move can silently break an otherwise decent plan.

## Immediate Priorities

- Confirm new domicile and supporting facts
- Re-check will execution and self-proving rules
- Re-check POA and healthcare-directive acceptance in the new state
- Re-check homestead, elective-share, and community-property consequences
- Re-check any transfer-on-death deed or enhanced-life-estate assumptions

## Questions To Ask

- Where is the new permanent home?
- Is there real property remaining in the old state?
- Was the prior plan drafted with old-state-specific forms or references?
- Does the move create or eliminate state estate or inheritance tax?

## Planning Adaptations

- Re-run the domicile analysis
- Revisit trust funding if the move changes probate or homestead strategy
- Confirm whether old documents remain valid but still should be refreshed
- Review advisor, fiduciary, and executor practical suitability under the new geography

## Documents To Collect

- New deed / lease / driver’s license / voter registration evidence
- Prior estate-plan documents
- Property records in both states
- Insurance and tax records showing the new base of life

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming validity equals adequacy
- Forgetting ancillary probate on out-of-state property
- Missing changes to healthcare-directive forms and bank POA practices
- Leaving old-state fiduciaries who are no longer practical
