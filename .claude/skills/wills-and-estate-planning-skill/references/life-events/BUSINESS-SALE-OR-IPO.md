# Life Event: Business Sale / IPO / Liquidity Event

A liquidity event is the single largest opportunity for estate-planning leverage. The best planning happens BEFORE the event; the best mistakes happen after.

## Timing Framework

### Pre-LOI (Earliest, Best)
- Shares cheap, valuation uncertain
- Maximum gifting / trust funding leverage
- QSBS planning still possible
- Most flexibility

### Post-LOI, Pre-Close
- Higher valuation but not confirmed
- Window shrinking
- Many strategies still available

### Post-Close
- Valuation crystallized
- Planning shifts to post-liquidity strategies
- Charitable and diversification focus

### Post-Liquidity (Money in Bank)
- Diversification
- New domicile possible
- Comprehensive estate plan reset
- Philanthropic structure
- Asset protection

## Pre-Liquidity Planning (Most Important)

### Gift Shares to Grantor Trust (IDGT or SLAT)

Before the transaction:
- Transfer shares at current FMV
- Use gift-tax exemption ($15M 2026)
- Future growth (including transaction premium) escapes estate
- Valuation discounts (minority, marketability): 30-50%

**Example:**
- Founder owns 20% of $50M company = $10M
- Gift 10% to grantor trust at $5M (pre-discount) = $2.5M (after 50% discount) of exemption used
- Company sells for $500M: 10% = $50M in trust, no estate tax

### IDGT Sale for Installment Note

For larger estates or when gift exhausts exemption:
- Seed trust with small gift (10% of contemplated sale value)
- Sell additional shares to trust for installment note (at AFR)
- Note pays interest; principal repayment
- Growth above AFR escapes estate entirely
- Very powerful for pre-liquidity assets

### GRAT for Uncertain Growth

If uncertain about growth:
- Transfer shares to GRAT
- Annuity back to grantor
- If shares appreciate beyond hurdle rate: residue to beneficiaries tax-free
- If not: shares return to grantor (no loss)
- "Heads I win, tails I don't lose"

### QSBS (Section 1202) for C-Corp

- Section 1202 is now **acquisition-date-specific** under current law, not a single "5 years / $10M" rule
- Post-enactment stock can follow a phased **3 / 4 / 5-year** exclusion regime, while older stock can remain on the older timeline and cap
- Per-taxpayer dollar limits can differ depending on acquisition date
- **Stacking:** gift pre-IPO shares to trusts or other taxpayers only after checking original-issue rules, holding-period treatment under the transfer structure, and whether the stock still qualifies

### Charitable Vehicles

**Before sale (appreciated stock):**
- Contribute shares to DAF or CRT
- Immediate deduction at FMV
- No capital gains on sale

**After sale (cash):**
- Cash to DAF / private foundation / CRT
- Lower tax leverage (no gain avoidance)
- But still valuable for charitable intent

### Domicile Planning Pre-Sale

For high-state-income-tax resident:
- Move to no-tax state (FL, TX, NV, WY, TN, SD) before sale
- Save state tax on gain (13.3% in CA; 10.9% in NY)
- **Must actually move** — former state will audit
- Timeline: move 6-24 months before close, ideally
- Document thoroughly

**Note:** CA's SB 131 closed NING/DING loophole; Washington's new 7% capital gains tax; other states catching up.

## Mid-Transaction Planning

### 83(b) Elections (If Restricted Stock)
- Make within 30 days of grant
- Pays ordinary tax on current FMV
- Starts LTCG clock

### Stock Option Strategy
- ISO: exercise at optimal AMT point
- NQSO: exercise / sell timing
- Consider transfer of NQSO to trust if plan allows

### Acceleration Clauses
- Understand what vests at sale
- Plan gifts before or after acceleration

### Rollover Equity
If staying post-acquisition:
- Rollover shares in acquirer
- Tax-deferred in most structures
- Post-close planning for rollover

## Transaction Structure

### Stock Sale
- Capital gains treatment
- QSBS applies if eligible
- Cleaner for seller

### Asset Sale
- Ordinary income on most of gain
- More complex tax
- Buyer may prefer for basis step-up

### Hybrid (338(h)(10), 336(e))
- S-corp can elect asset-sale tax treatment on stock sale
- Allows basis step-up for buyer

Your tax treatment affects post-close planning priorities.

## Post-Close Planning

### Immediate (Within 1 Week)
- Secure the proceeds (don't over-concentrate)
- Initial tax consultation (know the tax bill)
- Temporary asset allocation (conservative until plan developed)

### Within 30 Days
- Comprehensive estate plan review
- Insurance: life, disability, LTC review
- Emergency fund adjustments
- Financial advisor / planner engagement

### Within 90 Days
- New or updated estate documents
- Revised trust funding (post-liquidity assets)
- ILIT for any large insurance
- Domicile finalization (if moving)

### Within 6 Months
- Charitable structure finalized
- Investment strategy implemented
- Entity cleanup (closing old entities)
- Family governance discussions

### Within 12 Months
- Philanthropic activities launched
- Next-gen education initiated
- Personal plan (next venture? retirement? travel?)
- Full tax return filed with sale

## Post-Liquidity Estate Plan Rebuild

### Revocable Trust Expansion
- Fund with new liquidity
- Update terms for larger estate
- Successor trustee considerations

### SLAT / Dynasty Trust
- Use remaining exemption
- Deploy the current-law exemption intentionally after modeling basis, control, and liquidity tradeoffs
- Fund with appreciating assets

### ILIT for Large Insurance
- Permanent life insurance often bought post-liquidity
- ILIT holds to remove from estate

### Asset Protection
- DAPT if elevated liability risk
- Entity structure for investment properties
- Umbrella insurance

### Charitable Structure
- Private foundation (for $10M+ charitable intent, long-term)
- DAF (for flexibility)
- CRT (for income stream + charity)
- Named giving / institutional commitments

### Family Office (If Large Enough)
- Typical threshold: $100M+ for full family office
- Multi-family office at $25M-$100M
- Bill-pay and coordination service at $10M+

### Next-Gen Planning
- Trust structures for children
- Education
- Family governance

## Common Liquidity Event Scenarios

### Scenario A: Tech IPO, $20M Liquid
- 35, married, 2 young kids, pre-IPO value $5M, post-IPO $20M (still locked up)
- Pre-IPO: gift 20% of shares to grantor trust
- Post-IPO: 10b5-1 plan; charitable structure; SLAT with exemption
- Revocable trust; ILIT for $10M term

### Scenario B: Founder Acquisition, $50M
- 42, married, 3 kids, sold to public acquirer for $50M equity + earnout
- Pre-close: SLAT + dynasty trust funded; QSBS utilized
- Post-close: diversification; charitable CRT; family office lite

### Scenario C: PE Founder, $200M
- 55, married, adult kids, $200M net worth post-fund-close
- Full UHNW planning; dynasty trust; private foundation; asset protection; family office

### Scenario D: Serial Founder, $500M
- 50, second successful exit, $500M, no prior planning
- Need to catch up: comprehensive planning, exemption deployment analysis, domicile move

## Common Failure Modes

1. **Pre-liquidity planning deferred** — "we'll do it after we close" → growth in estate, exemption wasted
2. **Valuation discounts missed** — gifting shares without proper discount analysis
3. **QSBS status lost** through secondary sales or entity changes
4. **Domicile claim not established** before sale — home state taxes
5. **Rollover equity estate-planning ignored** — post-close situation unclear
6. **Charitable deduction not maximized** — cash instead of appreciated stock
7. **83(b) missed** early on — vesting tax later
8. **Concentrated position post-liquidity** — company troubles before diversification
9. **ILIT funded wrong way** — gift tax on premiums
10. **10b5-1 plan violates rules** — insider-trading exposure
11. **Emotional spending post-liquidity** — dissipation of proceeds
12. **No family conversation** about new wealth — shock, resentment

## Team Required

- Estate planning attorney (with liquidity event experience)
- M&A / corporate attorney
- Tax CPA with transactional experience
- Financial advisor / wealth manager
- Investment banker (pre-sale)
- Business appraiser (for gifts)
- Insurance broker
- Trust company (for dynasty situs)
- Family therapist / consultant (family dynamics)

## Cost Considerations

- Pre-liquidity planning: $25K-$250K depending on complexity
- Post-liquidity rebuild: $50K-$500K depending on scale
- Ongoing: $25K-$1M+/year
- ROI: typically 10-100x in tax savings alone

Cost is almost irrelevant compared to optimization value at this tier.
