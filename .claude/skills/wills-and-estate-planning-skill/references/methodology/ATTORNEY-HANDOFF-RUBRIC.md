# Attorney Handoff Rubric

The handoff packet should let the attorney start at a high level rather than repeating
basic intake from scratch. This file scores whether the workspace is ready for that.

Output to:

- `analyses/attorney-handoff-readiness.md`

---

## Scoring Categories

Score each category 0-5.

### 1. Facts and Documents

- Are the key family, asset, debt, and beneficiary facts documented?
- Are critical source documents present or explicitly missing?

### 2. Law Verification

- Have volatile state / federal issues been verified and logged?
- Are unresolved questions clearly separated from settled points?

### 3. Structure Decisions

- Has the user made the major value and architecture choices?
- Are the remaining attorney questions specific rather than vague?

### 4. Implementation Readiness

- Are beneficiary, titling, deed, and funding tasks identified?
- Is signing readiness mapped?

### 5. Litigation / Conflict Readiness

- Are unequal-treatment, capacity, and family-conflict risks surfaced?
- Is there a mitigation strategy?

### 6. Fiduciary Readiness

- Have primary and backup agents been scored?
- Are there obvious conflicts or weak candidates left unresolved?

### 7. Advisor Efficiency

- Would a competent attorney be able to draft efficiently from this packet?

---

## Rating Bands

- `26-35` = green, strong handoff
- `16-25` = yellow, usable but incomplete
- `0-15` = red, too many open foundations

---

## Required Output Sections

1. score by category
2. total score / band
3. biggest blockers to efficient drafting
4. what to gather before meeting counsel
5. what is safe to send now vs what should wait

---

## Anti-Pattern

Do not call the handoff "complete" just because a plan report exists.
The handoff is complete when the packet is efficient for counsel to use.
