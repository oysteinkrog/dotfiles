# Plan Maintenance OS — Annual, Event-Driven, and Deep-Review Cadence

Estate plans decay. The maintenance system is part of the plan, not an afterthought.

This file powers:

- `deliverables/review-schedule.md`
- periodic plan refreshes
- fiduciary viability checks

---

## Maintenance Levels

### Level 1 — Annual Light Review

Run once per year even if nothing dramatic happened.

Checklist:

- Confirm domicile and mailing addresses
- Confirm every named executor / trustee / agent is still appropriate
- Confirm beneficiary designations still reflect intent
- Confirm major asset inventory is current
- Confirm digital-access contacts still work
- Confirm long-term-care / disability / life insurance still match reality

### Level 2 — Life-Event Review

Run immediately after:

- marriage
- divorce / separation
- birth / adoption
- death of spouse / child / named fiduciary
- inheritance received
- interstate move
- major purchase or sale of real estate
- business formation / sale / liquidity event
- material health change
- major tax-law change

Default rule: if any of these occur, do not wait for the next annual cycle.

### Level 3 — Full Deep Review

Run every 3 to 5 years even if the plan feels stable.

Checklist:

- Re-read all controlling documents
- Re-run tax exposure analysis
- Re-run liquidity analysis
- Re-run red-flag triage
- Re-run stress-test scenarios
- Reconfirm the implementation / funding ledger

---

## Fiduciary Viability Audit

At each maintenance cycle, ask:

- Is this person still alive, healthy, local enough, and trusted?
- Are they better with money than the beneficiary they would control?
- Would they cooperate with the rest of the family?
- Are they now in addiction, divorce, creditor, or litigation trouble?
- Would naming them create resentment or deadlock?

If confidence drops, replace or add backups now, not later.

---

## Beneficiary and Titling Sweep

Perform a systematic sweep:

1. Retirement accounts
2. Life insurance
3. Annuities
4. POD / TOD bank and brokerage accounts
5. Real-estate title
6. Trust funding schedules
7. Business transfer restrictions

For each item ask:

- What controls today?
- Is that what the user wants now?
- Does it still match the will / trust?

---

## Access Drill

At least annually, test whether the plan is usable:

- Can the spouse / executor locate the documents?
- Can the successor trustee identify all accounts?
- Is the digital inventory current?
- Are the institution contacts and advisor contacts current?
- Does anyone know where the original signed documents are?

If the answer to any of these is "probably," treat it as a failure.

## Operating Rule

If the plan is too fragile to survive one year without memory-dependent heroics, the
problem is not maintenance discipline. The plan itself is underbuilt.
