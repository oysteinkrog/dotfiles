# Fiduciary Scoring

People often choose executors, trustees, guardians, and agents emotionally or by habit.
This file forces a more explicit comparison.

Output to:

- `analyses/fiduciary-bench-scorecard.md`

---

## Roles To Score

- executor / personal representative
- successor trustee
- guardian for minors
- financial POA agent
- healthcare agent
- trust protector
- business continuity operator if needed

---

## Scoring Dimensions

Use a 1-5 scale.

### Willingness

Have they actually agreed to serve?

### Competence

Can they handle paperwork, conflict, deadlines, and money?

### Integrity

Would other beneficiaries trust them with power?

### Availability

Are they reachable, local enough, and likely to have time?

### Age / Health

Will they probably still be able to serve when needed?

### Neutrality

Are they too interested, too controversial, or too entangled?

### Domain Fit

For the specific role, are they a good match?

Examples:

- healthcare empathy for healthcare agent
- operational competence for executor
- parenting values for guardian
- investment / discretion judgment for trustee

---

## High-Risk Combinations

Flag explicitly:

- estranged sibling as executor
- one child executor over hostile siblings
- elderly parent as backup guardian
- co-trustees likely to deadlock
- beneficiary with addiction / creditor risk as trustee
- out-of-state fiduciary where local presence matters

---

## Anti-Pattern

Do not default to:

- oldest child
- nearest relative
- spouse for every role
- "all children together" as co-fiduciaries

without explicit scoring and tradeoff discussion.
