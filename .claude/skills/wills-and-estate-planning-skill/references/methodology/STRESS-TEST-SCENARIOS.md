# Stress-Test Scenarios — Break the Plan Before Life Does

A plan is not complete until it has been tested against ugly facts. Run these scenarios
before finalizing `deliverables/plan-report.md`.

Write results to `analyses/stress-test-scenarios.md`.

---

## Scenario 1 — Death Tonight

Questions:

- What assets transfer tonight, and by what mechanism?
- Which assets are frozen until probate?
- Who has immediate cash for funeral, mortgage, payroll, tuition, and living expenses?
- Can the right person find the documents within hours?
- Do beneficiary forms tell the same story as the will and trust?

Failure signs:

- Estate or ex-spouse named on core beneficiary accounts
- No emergency access packet
- No liquidity outside probate

---

## Scenario 2 — Three-Year Incapacity

Questions:

- Who can actually access accounts and manage bills?
- Are institutions likely to honor the existing POA?
- Is trust funding sufficient for successor-trustee management?
- Who makes medical decisions and where is the directive located?
- Does the plan still work if the incapacitated person never regains capacity?

Failure signs:

- Springing POA with medical-certification trap
- Successor trustee cannot act because assets were never titled into trust
- No HIPAA / health-care authorization

---

## Scenario 3 — Simultaneous Deaths

Questions:

- What if both parents die in the same event?
- Do guardianship and trustee backups still work?
- Do survivorship assumptions distort tax or beneficiary outcomes?
- Is there a common-disaster clause / survivorship period issue to raise with counsel?

Failure signs:

- Only one fiduciary named
- Beneficiary logic assumes sequential deaths only

---

## Scenario 4 — Survivor Remarries

Questions:

- Are first-marriage children still protected?
- Can the survivor redirect everything through new beneficiary forms or a new will?
- Are retirement accounts and homestead rights consistent with the intended protection?

Failure signs:

- "Trust my spouse to be fair" with no structure
- No QTIP / separate-share / protected remainder design where needed

---

## Scenario 5 — Named Fiduciary Fails

Questions:

- What if the executor dies first, declines, moves abroad, or becomes incapacitated?
- Is there at least one competent backup?
- Would backup choices create family warfare?

Failure signs:

- No successors
- Co-fiduciary deadlock risk

---

## Scenario 6 — Illiquid Estate / Cash Crunch

Questions:

- Can taxes, debts, and support obligations be paid within 9 months?
- Would the family business, farm, or real estate be sold under pressure?
- Is insurance, reserve cash, or section 6166 analysis needed?

Failure signs:

- Wealth exists mostly in closely held or illiquid assets
- Equalization obligations exceed liquid assets

---

## Scenario 7 — Digital Lockout

Questions:

- Can the executor identify and access email, phone, password manager, cloud storage, and exchange accounts?
- Are seed phrases stored safely but locatable?
- Are legacy contacts or recovery instructions actually configured?

Failure signs:

- Self-custodied crypto with no documented recovery process
- Key communications account locked behind a dead phone number

---

## Scenario 8 — Family Conflict

Questions:

- Which decisions will surprise people?
- Are any choices likely to be interpreted as favoritism?
- Is there a narrative / communication plan for unequal treatment or asset concentration?

Failure signs:

- Unequal inheritance with no explanation
- Shared lumpy asset with no governance plan

---

## Scenario 9 — Cross-State Friction

Questions:

- Which state controls probate, homestead, elective share, and incapacity forms?
- Is ancillary probate likely?
- Are execution formalities for old documents still acceptable after the move?

Failure signs:

- Old plan from one state, current domicile in another, real estate in a third

## Operating Rule

Do not ask only whether the documents are valid.

Ask whether the family can actually use the plan under stress, confusion, and grief.
