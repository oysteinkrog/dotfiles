# Asset Protection Planning

For individuals in high-liability professions (surgeons, developers, executives, business owners) and HNW+ families with substantial creditor or litigation exposure.

## Layered Defense Model

Asset protection is **layers**, not a single structure. Most attacks are defeated at the first layer; serious attacks require deeper layers.

```
Layer 1: Insurance (umbrella, professional, D&O, E&O) — first line, cheapest
Layer 2: Entity isolation (LLCs, S-corps for business activities)
Layer 3: Marital + retirement statutory protections (TBE, ERISA, IRA)
Layer 4: Family LLPs/LLCs with charging-order limitation
Layer 5: Domestic asset protection trusts (DAPTs in NV/SD/AK/DE/WY)
Layer 6: Offshore trusts (Cook Islands, Nevis, Belize)
```

## Critical Timing: BEFORE the Claim

Transfers AFTER a claim arises (or is reasonably foreseeable) are typically **voidable as fraudulent transfers** under the Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act (now Uniform Voidable Transactions Act).

**Plan when there's no problem.** Asset-protection planning during a lawsuit is too late and may itself be evidence of bad faith.

## Layer 1: Insurance

### Umbrella Liability

- $5M-$10M typical for HNW households
- Cheap ($1K-$3K/year)
- Covers personal liability beyond home/auto policy limits
- Protects against most "neighbor sues you" scenarios
- **First line of defense — buy this before anything else**

### Professional Liability

- Malpractice (physicians, attorneys, accountants)
- E&O (consultants, real estate agents, investment advisors)
- D&O (corporate directors, board members)
- Tail coverage when leaving practice

### Other

- Cybersecurity / data breach
- Employment practices (if employer)
- Property and casualty (adequate replacement coverage)

## Layer 2: Entity Isolation

### LLCs for Business

- Operating business in LLC, not personally
- Each rental property in separate LLC
- Limited liability for entity debts (personal protection)
- Charging-order limitation in many states for multi-member LLCs
- Operating agreement defining management, distributions

### S-Corp Election

- Tax efficiency for self-employment
- Same liability protection as LLC
- Coordinates with retirement plan strategies (Solo 401(k), DB plan)

## Layer 3: Statutory Protections

### Retirement Accounts

- **ERISA-qualified plans (most 401(k)s)**: very strong creditor protection in bankruptcy AND outside bankruptcy
- **IRAs**: ~$1.5M creditor protection in bankruptcy (federal); state-law protection varies outside bankruptcy
- **Inherited IRAs**: SCOTUS held NOT protected in bankruptcy (Clark v. Rameker); some states have enacted state protection

### Homestead Exemptions

- **Florida + Texas**: unlimited homestead exemption (subject to acreage limits)
- **California**: indexed statutory homestead band; verify the current amount before relying on it
- **Most other states**: $25K-$200K
- Protected from most creditors (not IRS, not divorce, not federal restitution)

### Tenancy by the Entirety (TBE)

- Available in some states (FL, MA, NJ, OH, PA, others) for married couples
- Protects from creditors of ONE spouse (not joint debts)
- Powerful in malpractice scenarios (only the doctor-spouse is liable)

### Spousal Property in Community-Property States

- Community property treatment varies; consult state-specific rules
- "Joint and several" liability of community property may eliminate this protection

## Layer 4: Family Limited Partnerships and LLCs

### Mechanics

1. Form FLP/FLLC; transfer investment assets to it
2. Family members hold limited / non-managing interests
3. Senior generation typically holds general / managing interest
4. Operating agreement restricts transfers, distributions

### Charging Order Limitation

- Creditor of a partner/member can obtain "charging order"
- Charging order = right to receive distributions IF and WHEN made
- **No right to force distributions, manage, or liquidate**
- Effectively makes the interest worthless to outside creditors
- Strongest in NV, WY, DE, AZ, AK (single-member LLC charging-order protection)

### Tax + Estate Planning Bonus

- Valuation discounts (lack of marketability, lack of control) — 20-40% discount common
- Used for gift/estate tax efficiency in HNW transfers

### Watchouts

- Must respect formalities (separate accounts, real distributions, regular meetings)
- IRS scrutiny of FLPs with no business purpose (only valuation discount)
- §2036 inclusion if grantor retains too much control

## Layer 5: Domestic Asset Protection Trusts (DAPTs)

### Mechanics

1. Grantor creates self-settled spendthrift trust in DAPT-friendly state
2. Grantor can be a discretionary beneficiary
3. Independent trustee in DAPT state administers
4. Statutory bar on creditor claims after waiting period (typically 2 years for non-fraudulent transfers)
5. DAPT-state law applies if drafted properly

### DAPT States (Strongest)

- **Nevada**: 2-year statute, no exception creditors, very strong
- **South Dakota**: similar to Nevada plus dynasty trust capability
- **Alaska**: original DAPT state; 4-year statute
- **Delaware**: 4-year statute; some exception creditors
- **Wyoming**, **Tennessee**, others: weaker but functional

### Limits

- Federal courts and non-DAPT-state courts may not honor DAPT protection
- Pre-existing creditors (at funding) typically not blocked
- Federal tax claims, child support, alimony often exception creditors
- Bankruptcy court may unwind transfers within 10 years (BAPCPA §548(e))

## Layer 6: Offshore Trusts

### Jurisdictions

- **Cook Islands**: most aggressive protection; very short statute
- **Nevis**: similar; strong
- **Belize**: strong but somewhat newer
- **Bermuda, Cayman, BVI**: less protection-focused

### Mechanics

- Trust established under foreign law
- Foreign trustee
- Grantor often "fled to" trust under duress provisions if attacked
- Attacker must litigate in foreign court (expensive, often impossible)

### Compliance Cost

- FBAR (FinCEN 114) annual filing
- FATCA (Form 8938) annual filing
- Form 3520 (gift to foreign trust) on funding
- Form 3520-A annual reporting
- Penalties for non-compliance: severe (often percentage of asset value)

### Cost

- Setup: $25K-$100K+ legal
- Annual: $5K-$25K trustee fees + compliance
- Justified for net worth $5M+ with serious liability concern

## Coordination With Estate Planning

- DAPT can be drafted as dynasty trust (multi-generational protection)
- ILIT provides asset protection for life insurance proceeds
- FLP/FLLC interests can be transferred to dynasty trust
- Coordinate marital trusts with creditor-protection provisions

## Common Failure Modes

1. **Last-minute planning** — fraudulent transfer; voided
2. **Inadequate insurance** — first line of defense weak
3. **Co-mingling personal and entity** — pierces corporate veil
4. **Failure to respect entity formalities** — entity disregarded
5. **DAPT in user's home state** — court likely doesn't apply DAPT law
6. **Single-member LLC in non-protective state** — charging-order limitation lost
7. **Offshore trust without compliance** — penalties exceed protection value
8. **Asset protection visible to opposing counsel** — settlement leverage lost
9. **Forgetting professional liability tail coverage** at retirement
10. **Inheritance from parent's IRA** held outside protection (Clark v. Rameker)

## State-Specific Quick Reference

Homestead amounts are a moving target and can be indexed, county-sensitive, or fact-pattern-sensitive. Treat the table below as a directional screening aid, not a final legal conclusion.

| State | Homestead | TBE | DAPT | Single-Member LLC Protection |
|-------|-----------|-----|------|------------------------------|
| Florida | Unlimited | ✅ | ❌ | Weak (without statute) |
| Texas | Unlimited (acreage) | ❌ | ❌ | Weak |
| Nevada | Strong statutory homestead; verify current amount | ❌ | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong |
| South Dakota | $60K | ❌ | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong |
| Delaware | $125K | ❌ | ✅ Moderate | Moderate |
| Alaska | $54K | ❌ | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong |
| Wyoming | $20K | ❌ | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong |
| California | Statutory homestead band; verify current amount | ❌ | ❌ | Weak |
| New York | County-based statutory band; verify current amount | ✅ | ❌ | Weak |
