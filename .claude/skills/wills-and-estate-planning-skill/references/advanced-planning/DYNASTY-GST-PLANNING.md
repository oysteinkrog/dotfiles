# Dynasty Trusts and GST Planning

For families seeking multi-generational wealth transfer free from the federal transfer-tax system at each generational shift.

## What Dynasty Trusts Do

Without a dynasty trust, every generational transfer triggers transfer tax (estate or GST). Wealth that compounds over 4 generations gets hit 3 times — at each child's, grandchild's, and great-grandchild's death — with 40% federal tax (plus state). Compounded, this can reduce inheritance by ~80% over 4 generations.

A properly funded dynasty trust:
- Allocates GST exemption ($15M per person in 2026)
- Holds assets for multi-generational benefit
- Distributes income/principal under HEMS to descendants
- **Bypasses estate tax at each beneficiary's death** (assets stay in trust, not in beneficiary's estate)
- Provides creditor protection (spendthrift)
- Provides divorce protection in most states

## When to Use

**Strong indicators:**
- Estate above federal exemption ($15M+) AND wealth intended for descendants
- Family business or real estate intended to be held for generations
- Concerns about heirs' financial sophistication or marriage stability
- State allows long-duration trusts (no rule against perpetuities, or very long perpetuities period)

**Skip if:**
- Estate below federal exemption (no transfer-tax savings to capture)
- Family unlikely to value multi-generational structure
- All assets intended to be liquidated within 1-2 generations

## Key Drafting Elements

### GST Exemption Allocation

- Must affirmatively allocate GST exemption on Form 706 or 709 to make the trust GST-exempt
- $15M per person (2026); not portable
- Use it during life or at death — but not portable
- Once allocated, trust is GST-exempt for its duration

### Trust Situs

State law governs how long the trust can last. Choose situs in a state that has:
- Abolished or extended the rule against perpetuities (Delaware, South Dakota, Nevada, Wyoming, Alaska, Tennessee — perpetual or 365+ years)
- No state income tax on trusts (SD, NV, WY, AK, TN, FL, TX, NH, WA)
- Strong asset protection
- Directed-trust statutes (separate trustee from investment authority)

### Distribution Provisions

- Discretionary distributions to descendants under HEMS
- Trustee independence from beneficiaries
- Trust protector with power to modify, decant, change trustees
- Spendthrift provision (creditor and divorce protection)

### Trustee Selection

- Corporate trustee in chosen situs state (e.g., SD trust company)
- Family member as co-trustee or distribution committee
- Trust protector to remove trustees and adapt over time

## Worked Example

**2026**: Grantor (UHNW) creates dynasty trust in South Dakota; funds with $15M of family business interests. Allocates full $15M GST exemption.

**2050**: Trust now holds $60M (4x growth). Grantor's children receive distributions for HEMS but trust never liquidates.

**2075**: Children die. Trust assets ($120M) stay in trust — NOT in children's estates. No estate tax at children's deaths.

**2100**: Trust now $300M. Grandchildren receive distributions; trust continues for great-grandchildren.

**Compare to no dynasty trust**: Same $15M would have been hit by 40% estate tax twice (children's and grandchildren's deaths) = $192M lost to estate tax. Dynasty trust saved $192M+.

## State Comparison

| State | Perpetuities | State Income Tax on Trusts | Asset Protection |
|-------|-------------|----------------------------|------------------|
| **South Dakota** | Perpetual | None | Excellent (DAPT, dynasty, directed) |
| **Nevada** | 365 years | None | Excellent (DAPT, dynasty) |
| **Delaware** | Perpetual for personalty | Yes if NR beneficiary | Strong |
| **Alaska** | 1000 years | None | Strong |
| **Wyoming** | 1000 years | None | Strong |
| **Tennessee** | 360 years | None | Strong |

South Dakota commonly chosen for combination of perpetual duration, no state income tax, and trust-friendly courts.

## Coordination With Other Strategies

- **Sale to IDGT** funds the dynasty trust efficiently
- **GRATs** can be funded with dynasty-trust remainder
- **ILIT** can be a sub-trust of the dynasty trust
- **Family LLC/FLP** held inside dynasty trust for valuation discounts plus generational protection

## Tax Drawback: No Step-Up

Assets in dynasty trust do NOT get step-up in basis at successive deaths. For trust intended to hold growth assets long-term, this is acceptable. For assets that will be liquidated, the lost step-up may exceed the GST savings.

**Best dynasty-trust assets:**
- Family business interests (will be held, not liquidated)
- Real estate intended for generational use
- Growth-stage stocks (long-term hold)
- Life insurance owned by ILIT-as-sub-trust

**Worse dynasty-trust assets:**
- Highly appreciated stocks intended for sale
- Assets where heirs will need liquidity

## Common Failure Modes

1. **GST exemption not allocated** — trust subject to GST tax at each generation
2. **Wrong situs** — state income tax or short perpetuity period
3. **No trust protector** — trust frozen as drafted forever
4. **Beneficiary as trustee** — IRS may include in estate
5. **Distributions exceed HEMS** — IRS may include in estate
6. **Coordination failure with other trusts** — reciprocal trust doctrine
7. **Investment failure** — trust corpus depleted before generational benefit realized
8. **Family loses interest** in multi-generational structure — modern decanting needed
9. **Beneficiary becomes uncooperative** — silent/quiet trust provisions help

## Documentation Required

- Trust agreement (drafted by attorney specializing in dynasty trusts)
- Form 706 or 709 with GST allocation
- Annual fiduciary income tax returns (Form 1041)
- Trustee succession documentation
- Trust protector appointment
- Investment policy statement
- Coordination with state-of-situs trust company
