# Intellectual Property and Royalties

If the decedent created copyrightable works, held patents/trademarks, or licensed any IP:

## Asset Types

- **Copyrights** — last 70 years after author's death (works post-1978); renewal rules complex for older works
- **Patents** — require maintenance fees + active enforcement
- **Trademarks** — require continuous use and periodic renewal
- **Royalty contracts** — book, music, film, TV, streaming, mechanical licenses
- **Active IP** — YouTube channels, Substack, Patreon, podcasts generating ongoing income
- **NIL rights** — name, image, likeness; especially relevant for AI-era deepfake protection
- **Oil/gas/mineral royalties** — see [FARMLAND-MINERAL-RIGHTS.md](FARMLAND-MINERAL-RIGHTS.md)

## Special Considerations

- **Specialized IP/literary executor** — standard executors typically not equipped to negotiate licensing or manage copyrights; appoint a specialist
- **Renewal calendars** — patents and trademarks lapse if maintenance fees missed
- **Active management** — YouTube channel without continuation goes dark; Patreon subscriptions cancel; royalty checks misdirected without active address management
- **Posthumous publication** — what about manuscripts in progress? Permission to alter, complete, or destroy?
- **Author's surviving spouse termination rights** — for pre-1978 works, surviving spouse has statutory termination rights under Copyright Act

## Planning Steps

1. Inventory all IP holdings
2. Document royalty contracts and payment addresses
3. Designate IP/literary executor with renewal calendar
4. Consider IP-specific trust or LLC to hold and manage
5. Address NIL rights — explicit consent or denial of posthumous AI use
6. For active creator accounts: succession plan (continue, transfer, sunset)
7. Coordinate with IP-specialist attorney

## Common Failure Modes

1. Trademark lapses while estate sorts out — reverts to public
2. Patents abandoned for missed maintenance fees
3. Author's manuscripts destroyed or altered against intent
4. Royalty checks bounce back to publisher; family loses income
5. AI-era unauthorized deepfakes of decedent's voice/image
