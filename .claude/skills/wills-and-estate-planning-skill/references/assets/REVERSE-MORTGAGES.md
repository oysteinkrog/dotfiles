# Reverse Mortgages

A reverse mortgage is often an FHA-insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), though proprietary products also exist, and the death-stage rules require focused handling.

## What Happens at Death

The loan becomes due and payable when the **last borrower dies** or permanently leaves the home.

## Heirs' Options and Timeline

Reverse-mortgage timelines are layered, not a single clean deadline. Treat the file as urgent: the estate or heirs should contact the servicer immediately, because notice / certification windows can open quickly, while the practical sale-or-refinance payoff window is usually measured in months, not days. As a planning baseline, heirs often get about six months, with up to two 90-day extensions when sale or refinancing is being actively pursued and documented.

| Option | What Happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Sell the home** | Use proceeds to pay loan balance. Excess equity → estate. |
| **Keep the home** | Pay off loan balance from cash, or refinance to traditional mortgage |
| **Walk away** | Sign deed in lieu of foreclosure. No personal liability — HECM is non-recourse. |
| **Short-sale at 95% of appraised value** | If loan balance exceeds appraisal. FHA insurance covers shortfall. |

## Critical Non-Recourse Rule

HECMs are **non-recourse** loans. If the balance exceeds the home's value, heirs can satisfy the loan by paying **95% of the appraised value** — they are NOT personally liable for any shortfall beyond that.

## Eligible Non-Borrowing Spouse

A spouse who:
- Was married to the borrower at origination
- Is listed in loan documents as a non-borrowing spouse
- Continues to live in the home

…may be able to **stay in the home** under HUD's Mortgage Optional Election (MOE) Assignment process or related HUD protections. This is not automatic. The surviving spouse should respond immediately to servicer notices and provide any required certification / occupancy documentation. Do not assume unlimited time, but also do not reduce the analysis to one oversimplified deadline.

## Pre-Death Communication With Heirs

One high-leverage pre-death communication task: **tell your adult children about the reverse mortgage**.

Many adult children find out about a parent's reverse mortgage only after the parent dies, discover they cannot afford to keep the home they grew up in, and are shocked and angry.

Family-communication failure is often the real driver of ugly outcomes, not the reverse mortgage itself.

## Coordination With Estate Plan

- **Will** should not promise the home to specific heirs without addressing the loan
- **Letter of instruction** should clearly mention the reverse mortgage and provide the loan-servicer contact information
- **Estate liquidity and administration** — early servicer notices matter, so have the servicer contact information and loan file accessible immediately after death
- **Heirs' decision matrix** — pre-discussion of which heir wants the home, who can afford to refinance

## Common Failure Modes

1. **Heirs don't know about the reverse mortgage** until after death — surprise + grief + financial pressure
2. **Estate ignores early servicer notices / documentation requests** — timelines compress and foreclosure risk escalates
3. **Heir tries to "inherit" the home** without addressing loan — discovers they can't afford it
4. **Surviving non-borrowing spouse** assumes protection is automatic and misses certification / occupancy requirements
5. **Letter of instruction omits the loan** — executor caught off guard

## The Conversation to Have

For a parent considering or already with a reverse mortgage:

> "If something happens to you, the reverse mortgage becomes due. The kids will have a few months to either pay it off, refinance, or sell. They need to know it exists, who the lender is, and what their options will be. Let's set up a family conversation now so it's not a surprise later. Then I'll write the loan information into your letter of instruction so the executor has it immediately."
