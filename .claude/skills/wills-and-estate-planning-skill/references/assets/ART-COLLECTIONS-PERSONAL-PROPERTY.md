# Art, Collections, Cars, Jewelry, Wine, Personal Property

Personal property fights are disproportionately bitter — items often the most sentimentally loaded, hardest to divide.

## Strategies for Division

| Method | Best For | Drawback |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Specific bequests** in will/memorandum | Items with clear primary heir | Doesn't cover unlisted items |
| **Equalization by appraised value** | High-value art, jewelry | Requires appraisals |
| **Rotating draft** | Books, furniture, photos | Slow but fair |
| **Auction among heirs** with credit | Mixed-value items | Requires valuation |
| **Sell and split proceeds** | Items no one passionately wants | Loses sentimental items |

## Personal Property Memorandum

Most states allow a separate **personal property memorandum** referenced in the will but updatable without re-execution. Lists who gets what tangible item. Powerful, prevents fights.

## Art

- Qualified appraisals required for estate-tax purposes if estate files Form 706
- IRS Art Advisory Panel scrutinizes valuations >$50K
- Document provenance, store with item
- Insurance during administration

## Cars

Title transfer typically through DMV with death certificate + letters. Many states allow TOD on vehicle title.

## Jewelry

Heirloom pieces — appraisals + photos + sentimental matching to recipients.

## Wine and Whiskey Collections

State-law restrictions on private alcohol transfers. Major auction houses (Sotheby's, Christie's, Zachy's, Acker) handle estate sales with compliance built in.

## Common Failure Modes

1. "Divide personal effects fairly" with no mechanism — guarantees fight
2. No appraisals — heirs disagree about value
3. Sentimental items sold to outsiders against intent
4. Insurance gap during administration — theft uncovered
5. State alcohol-transfer rules ignored — wine cellar handled improperly
