# Pets and Animals

Pets are property, not beneficiaries, under U.S. law. But they are loved ones.

## Options

### Pet Trust (Widely Authorized; Verify Local Drafting Rules)

A legal trust with:
- **Designated caregiver** — who actually cares for the pet
- **Trustee** — manages and disburses funds
- **Funding** — sufficient for the pet's expected lifetime
- **Enforcer** — third party who ensures caregiver follows trust terms
- **Remainder beneficiary** — leftover funds go here at pet's death

Most U.S. jurisdictions now recognize some form of pet-trust planning, but duration limits, enforcement mechanics, and drafting conventions can still vary at the local level.

### Informal Arrangement

Leave the pet to a named person along with a cash bequest. Less enforceable but simpler. Risk: caregiver pockets cash and surrenders pet.

### Breed-Specific or Animal Welfare Organization

Many accept pets with an accompanying donation. Establish relationship while alive.

## Special Considerations

| Pet Type | Considerations |
|----------|----------------|
| Dogs/Cats | Standard 10-15 year lifespan; modest funding |
| Parrots | 50-80 year lifespans; multi-generational caregiver planning |
| Tortoises | 80-100+ years; truly multi-generational |
| Horses | $5K-$15K/year boarding + vet; 20-30 year lifespan; equine trust specialty |
| Koi/Aquariums | Specialized care; ongoing equipment maintenance |
| Service animals | May need retraining if handler dies |
| Livestock | Part of farm operation; usually transferred with farm |

## Typical Funding

- **Standard pet**: $10K-$30K covers food, vet, boarding for typical lifespan
- **Horse**: $200K-$500K endowment for lifetime
- **Exotic with long lifespan**: $50K+ depending on cost of care

## Multiple Pets

Specify whether bonded pets must stay together or can be split among households.

## Common Failure Modes

1. No pet plan — pet ends up at shelter
2. Pet left to "whoever wants him" — nobody does
3. Cash bequest to caregiver who pockets it and surrenders pet
4. No backup caregiver named
5. Underfunded for exotic / long-lived species
6. Service animal not addressed in handler's plan
