# Investment Partnership Interests (PE, VC, Hedge Fund, Real Estate Syndicate)

## Special Issues at Death

- **Transfer restrictions** — partnership agreements often require GP consent or ROFR before interest can pass (even to a trust)
- **Capital accounts** — Section 754 election can adjust inside basis to match heir's stepped-up outside basis
- **K-1 reporting straddles death** — partnership often issues corrected K-1s months late
- **Illiquidity** — fund interests cannot be sold into probate; expect to hold for remaining fund life
- **Capital calls** — committed but uncalled capital is a real obligation of estate or heir
- **Negative-basis interests** — accumulated distributions exceeding basis can trigger phantom gain on transfer
- **Depreciation recapture** — partial recapture survives the step-up
- **Valuation** — minority/marketability discounts; specialized appraisal required
- **GP / carried interests** — successor planning critical; carry transferability varies by fund

## §754 Election

The partnership can elect under IRC §754 to **adjust inside basis** of partnership assets to match the heir's stepped-up outside basis at death. Without it, the heir inherits partnership tax attributes that don't reflect their actual basis.

Coordinate with GP soon after death. GP must make the election; cannot be reversed easily.

## Estate Liquidity for Capital Calls

If decedent had committed but uncalled capital, the estate must continue to fund. Reserve liquidity. Otherwise:
- Default penalties apply
- Forfeited interest possible
- Lawsuits from GP/co-investors

## Planning Steps

1. **Identify every partnership interest** — start with K-1s from past 3-5 years
2. **Read each partnership agreement** for transfer/death provisions
3. **Notify GPs promptly** — some agreements require notice within set period
4. **Coordinate with GPs** on transfer paperwork and §754 election
5. **Reserve liquidity** for ongoing capital calls
6. **For GP/carry interests** — successor planning critical
7. **Valuation** — coordinate with GP on FMV at date of death

## Common Failure Modes

1. Estate misses GP notification deadline — penalty
2. No §754 election — heir inherits awkward basis position
3. Capital call defaults — interest forfeited
4. Carry interest dies with founder without successor plan
5. Trust receives interest without GP consent — transfer voidable
