# Institutional Operations Playbook

Some assets fail at the institution / paperwork layer rather than the legal-theory layer.
Use this file when the plan touches institutions that have their own transfer and control
mechanics.

---

## 529 Plans

- Verify owner, successor owner, and beneficiary
- Check whether the plan allows successor-owner designation and how it is updated
- Coordinate with minor-child or special-needs planning
- Record institution process in `deliverables/institution-contact-matrix.md`

## HSA

- Verify named beneficiary
- Distinguish spouse vs non-spouse outcome
- Make sure the beneficiary map reflects the tax consequence, not just the control path

## Equity Compensation and Stock Plans

- RSUs, stock options, ESPP, deferred compensation, and restricted stock often have
  employer-plan rules that do not behave like plain brokerage assets
- Verify plan documents, beneficiary options, transfer restrictions, and contact path
- Coordinate with the user's employer portal and any stock-plan administrator

## Private Funds and Syndications

- Read subscription docs, operating agreement, side letters, and K-1 history
- Identify transfer restrictions, consent requirements, capital-call exposure,
  and death / incapacity clauses

## Family LLC Interests

- Check operating agreement
- Identify manager authority, voting vs nonvoting interests, transfer restrictions,
  and buyout mechanics
- Do not assume membership interests can be reassigned casually

## Buy-Sell Agreements

- Identify triggering events, valuation mechanics, insurance linkage, and who actually
  has authority to act after death or incapacity
- Make sure the estate plan and buy-sell terms do not point in different directions
