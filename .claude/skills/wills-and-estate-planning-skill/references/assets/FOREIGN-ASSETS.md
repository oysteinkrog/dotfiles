# Foreign Assets and Cross-Border Issues

## Reporting Requirements

- **FBAR (FinCEN 114)** — required if foreign financial accounts > $10,000 aggregate at any point in year
- **FATCA (Form 8938)** — additional reporting for higher thresholds; varies by filing status
- **Form 3520** — foreign gifts > $100K (annual reporting)
- **Form 3520-A** — foreign trust reporting
- **Form 5471** — foreign corporation ownership

Penalties for non-filing are severe (often percentage of unreported asset value).

## Foreign Real Estate

- Generally subject to local inheritance laws (forced heirship in civil-law countries)
- Also subject to U.S. estate tax if decedent is U.S. person
- Often requires separate "situs will" drafted under local law
- Foreign probate typically required for transfer

## Foreign Trusts

- Complex reporting (Forms 3520, 3520-A)
- Anti-abuse rules (throwback rules for undistributed income)
- Outbound transfer rules
- Don't create without specialty cross-border counsel

## Estate Tax Treaties

- U.S. has estate tax treaties with ~20 countries (UK, Germany, France, Canada, Australia, etc.)
- Reduces double taxation
- Treaty rules govern residency and taxation
- Specific to U.S. + treaty country pair

## Forced Heirship

- France, Spain, Italy, most Latin America, much of Africa, Saudi Arabia
- Children entitled to "reserved portion" — cannot be devised away
- Louisiana retains a remnant for under-24 or disabled children
- Plan with local counsel; U.S. will may not control foreign property

## Non-Resident Alien Estate Tax

- NRAs subject to U.S. estate tax on U.S.-situs assets
- Exemption only $60,000 (vs. $15M for U.S. persons)
- U.S. real estate, U.S. corporate stock = U.S. situs
- Hold via foreign corporation to avoid (with careful planning)

## Distributions to Foreign Beneficiaries

- 30% withholding on most U.S.-source income (treaty may reduce)
- Currency conversion considerations
- Foreign country may tax inheritance (no, low, or high rates depending on country)
- Banking restrictions for sanctioned countries

## Coordinating Counsel

- U.S. estate-planning attorney
- Local counsel in each country with assets
- Cross-border tax specialist (CPA with international experience)
- Coordinate both wills (U.S. + foreign) for consistency
