# Farmland, Ranch, Mineral, Water, and Timber Rights

## Farmland and Agricultural Operations

### Section 2032A Special-Use Valuation

Lets qualified family farms be valued at **agricultural use** rather than **highest-and-best-use**, potentially reducing estate tax substantially. Requirements:
- Used for farming by decedent or family for 5 of last 8 years
- Active management for 10 years post-death
- Heirs related to decedent
- Recapture tax if farm sold or stops being farmed within 10 years
- Maximum reduction: **$1.46M for decedents dying in 2026**; verify the indexed amount for later years

### Section 6166 Deferral

Farms that qualify as closely held businesses can defer estate tax over 14 years (4-5 years interest only, then principal+interest).

### Successor Farmer Identification

Not every child wants to farm. Equitable treatment requires planning:
- Active farmer child gets the operation + appropriate financial support
- Non-farming children get cash, insurance, or other assets
- Buyout agreements with deferred payments to non-farming siblings

### Operation Components

Each its own asset:
- Land (acreage, water rights, mineral rights)
- Equipment, machinery
- Livestock, growing crops
- USDA program participation
- Conservation easements
- Storage facilities, structures

### Heirs' Property Problem

Multi-generational undivided ownership by many distant relatives — particularly significant in Black-owned farmland. Partition actions cause forced sales below market. Cleanup requires:
- Title research
- Quiet-title action
- Partition agreement among heirs
- Possibly LLC or trust to consolidate

## Mineral Rights

In many western states, surface and subsurface rights are separately owned.

- Family may own minerals under land they no longer own (severed estates)
- Royalty income continues for decades, sometimes generations
- Specialized title research often required to confirm ownership
- Consolidation in trust or LLC simplifies management
- Old family deeds, lease records, royalty statements, county recorder's records — all relevant

## Water Rights

Western prior-appropriation doctrine: water rights are **separately transferable** assets with their own seniority systems.

- "First in time, first in right"
- Senior water rights are valuable in drought regions
- Beneficial use must continue or right may be lost
- Some states allow leasing without losing seniority

## Timber and Other Severed Rights

- Timber rights — separately conveyed, valued, taxed
- Wind rights — emerging market for wind farm leases
- Solar rights — similar
- Easements — utility, conservation, access

## Common Failure Modes

1. Failure to elect §2032A — significant tax overpayment
2. Recapture tax on §2032A election — sale within 10 years triggers
3. Heirs' property never resolved — partition forced
4. Mineral rights separation forgotten — heirs unaware of asset
5. Water rights lapse for non-use
6. Conservation easement violated — penalty + deduction recapture

## Planning Recommendations

- Inventory all severed and surface rights
- Confirm current valuation; engage agricultural appraiser
- Discuss successor identification with family early
- Coordinate with farm-credit lender on succession
- Specialized agricultural attorney essential
