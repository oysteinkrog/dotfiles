Questions to ask before hiring an estate-planning attorney.

## About Their Practice

1. What percentage of your practice is estate planning vs. other areas?
2. How many years have you focused on estate planning?
3. What's your typical client profile? (Are they like me?)
4. Do you specialize in [my specific situation: blended family / business succession / SNT / etc.]?
5. Are you a certified specialist in your state? (CA, FL, NM, NC, OH, PA, TX, WA have certification)
6. Do you have any state-bar disciplinary history?

## About the Engagement

1. Will you personally handle my matter, or will associates do most of the work?
2. Who will be my main point of contact?
3. What's your fee structure? Flat fee for basic package? Hourly for additional?
4. What's included in the flat fee? What triggers additional charges?
5. Will I get a written engagement letter?
6. What's typical turnaround time for documents?
7. Do you offer ongoing maintenance / annual reviews?

## About My Plan Specifically

1. Based on my situation (Tier [X], [overlays]), what's your recommended structure?
2. Do you typically use [revocable trust / will only / specific structure] for my profile?
3. How would you handle [specific concern: blended family / business / vulnerable heir]?
4. Have you done much work with [specific issue: NFA gun trust / SNT / DAPT / international]?
5. How do you coordinate with my CPA and financial advisor?
6. Do you offer the "coordinated advisor meeting" annually?

## About State-Specific Issues

1. What's your assessment of my state estate tax exposure?
2. How do you handle out-of-state real property?
3. If I'm planning a domicile change, what's your experience defending domicile audits?
4. What's your view on [state-specific tool: TOD deed / homestead / community property election]?

## About Specific Documents

1. Do you draft using state-of-the-art post-SECURE Act trust language for retirement beneficiaries?
2. How do you handle digital asset access / RUFADAA authorization?
3. Do you include trust protector provisions and decanting language in irrevocable trusts?
4. How do you handle Crummey notice administration for ILITs?
5. Do you provide a trust-funding memo or implementation checklist after signing?
6. Will you help review beneficiary forms and deed / title updates, or is that outside scope?
7. If my case has high contest risk, what litigation-hardening steps do you normally recommend at signing?

## About Execution Logistics

1. How do you handle witness, notary, and self-proving requirements in this state?
2. If capacity or undue-influence risk exists, what execution safeguards do you recommend?
3. If I moved states recently, do you recommend full redrafting or can existing documents be repapered strategically?
4. How do you handle out-of-state real-estate deeds or ancillary-probate prevention?
5. Will your office supervise signing and produce a final executed-document package checklist?
6. For bedside or urgent signing situations, what safeguards do you require?
7. How do you document capacity / undue-influence protection when the facts are sensitive?

## About Communication and Updates

1. How often do you recommend reviewing the plan?
2. What life events trigger you reaching out to me?
3. Do you provide a client portal or document organizer?
4. How do you handle questions between annual reviews?

## About Conflict of Interest

1. If you represent both spouses, how do you manage potential conflicts?
2. Will you require us to sign a conflict-of-interest waiver?
3. Are any of your other clients (current or former) likely to be parties to my plan (named beneficiaries, fiduciaries)?

## About Your Network

1. Do you work regularly with CPAs, financial advisors, insurance agents you'd recommend?
2. Do you have a corporate trustee you trust if I want a professional fiduciary?
3. Do you have specialty referrals (NFA gun trust attorney, SNT attorney, international tax)?
4. If foreign property or forced-heirship issues arise, do you coordinate with foreign local counsel?

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Doesn't ask about your goals (just sells documents)
- Pushes a one-size-fits-all package without understanding your situation
- Reluctant to provide written engagement letter
- Won't discuss fees clearly
- Denigrates other professionals
- Promises results that depend on facts they haven't verified
- Recommends complex structures (offshore, FLP, DAPT) without clear benefit for your situation
- Claims to be a tax specialist but isn't a CPA / tax attorney with credentials
- Pressure tactics ("we need to do this immediately")
