**This skill produces educational drafts for review by a licensed attorney in the user's state. It is not legal, tax, or financial advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship.**

## What This Skill Does

- Conducts an interactive intake to surface the highest-value facts about your situation
- Produces a comprehensive plan report with recommended structure
- Generates draft documents and templates (asset inventory, beneficiary map, letters of instruction, ethical wills, family meeting agendas, executor playbooks)
- Identifies issues that require attorney attention
- Provides educational reference material on the areas that appear relevant to the facts developed in the session
- Produces an attorney-handoff packet with the questions you should ask

- **Does not** constitute legal, tax, or financial advice

- **Does not** execute, witness, notarize, file, or record any document
- **Does not** substitute for state-specific analysis — state laws vary substantially

## Who Should Treat a Licensed Attorney as Essential

For anyone with:
- Estate above the smallest state estate-tax threshold ($1M in Oregon, ~$2M in Massachusetts)
- Minor children
- Blended family
- Privately held business
- Disability or special needs in the family
- Real estate in multiple states
- Foreign assets or non-U.S. heirs
- Significant cryptocurrency
- Firearms, especially NFA items
- Recent diagnosis or known capacity concern
- Any unusual or contested family situation

— hire a qualified estate-planning attorney licensed in your state.

## State Law Variation

Estate planning is **predominantly state law**. Critical state-specific rules include:

- Will execution formalities (witnesses, notarization, holographic acceptance, electronic acceptance)
- Spousal elective share and forced-share rules
- Community property vs. common law marital regime
- Homestead protections
- TOD deed availability
- Trust situs and rule against perpetuities
- State estate tax and inheritance tax (thresholds, rates, portability)
- Medicaid lookback and asset protection rules
- Probate procedures and small-estate thresholds
- Gun trust and NFA item handling
- Disposition of remains rules
- Common-law marriage recognition

Every recommendation in this skill should be **verified with an attorney in your state** before relying on it in the real world.

## Tax Law Variation

Federal estate, gift, and GST tax law changes. Annual exclusion adjusts for inflation. State tax rules change. Always verify current-year numbers from primary sources before any live filing.

## Time Sensitivity

Some recommendations have hard or fast-moving deadlines:
- **Form 706 portability election** — generally requires timely filing within 9 months of death or the extended deadline, though certain portability-only estates may still qualify for IRS relief under Rev. Proc. 2022-32
- **Qualified disclaimers** — within 9 months of death
- **§754 election** — partnership tax-year-end
- **Reverse mortgage / HECM response windows** — servicer notice and response timing move quickly, so the estate or heirs should contact the servicer immediately
- **Crummey notices on ILITs** — annual
- **5-year Medicaid lookback** — plan in advance
