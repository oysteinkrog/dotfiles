# Ethical Will / Legacy Letter Template

**Non-legal document passing down values, life lessons, family history, and wisdom. Often the document heirs treasure most.**

Format options:
- Written letter
- Audio recording
- Video recording
- Combination

## Suggested Structure

### 1. Opening

Why I'm writing this. What I hope you'll know.

### 2. Family History

- Where we came from
- Who came before us — grandparents, great-grandparents
- Stories from my parents and grandparents
- The family I was born into
- Major events that shaped our family

### 3. My Life Story (Brief)

- Where I grew up
- What I cared about as a child
- How I met your other parent (if applicable)
- Your births / how you came into my life
- Defining moments
- The work I did and what it meant to me
- Where I lived
- Friends who shaped me

### 4. Values I Hope You Carry

- What matters most
- How I tried to live (and fell short)
- The principles I tried to keep
- What I learned about money, work, love, conflict, faith

### 5. Lessons Learned (from Successes and Failures)

- Mistakes I made
- What I'd do differently
- What worked
- What I'm proudest of
- What I most regret

### 6. Apologies

- To anyone I hurt
- For things left unsaid
- For times I wasn't there

### 7. Gratitude

- To family
- To friends
- To people who helped me at critical moments
- For the life I had

### 8. Specific Messages

For each child, grandchild, or other significant person — a personal message:

**To [Name]:**

> [Specific things only you would say to them. Memories you share. Hopes for their future. Things you saw in them that you want them to know you saw.]

### 9. Hopes for Future Generations

- What I hope my grandchildren and great-grandchildren know
- What I hope for the family
- What I hope for the world

### 10. Final Words

What I want to be the last thing you read from me.

---

## Companion Materials

Consider also:
- Photo album with captions explaining who is in each photo
- Recipe book with stories about each dish
- Audio recordings of you reading important letters
- Video recordings of you telling specific stories
- Family tree with stories about each person
- Letter to be opened at specific milestones (children's wedding, grandchildren's graduation, your 100th birthday)
