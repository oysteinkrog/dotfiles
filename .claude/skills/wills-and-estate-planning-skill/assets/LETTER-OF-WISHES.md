**For each trust created in your plan, write a letter to the trustee guiding their discretion. NOT legally binding — but invaluable for the trustee's judgment.**

## To: [Trustee Name]
## Trust: [Trust Name and Date]
## From: [Grantor Name]
## Date: [Date]

Dear [Trustee],

This letter accompanies the [Trust Name] dated [date]. It is not legally binding. The terms of the trust control. But this letter records my wishes and reasoning so that you can exercise your discretion in a way I would have approved.

## Beneficiary Background

[For each beneficiary, write a paragraph in your own words about who they are, their financial situation, their character, your concerns and hopes for them.]

**Example:**
> My son [Name] is [age]. He has struggled with [substance / financial / relational] issues. He is currently [in treatment / making progress / etc.]. I love him deeply and want this trust to support him in becoming the person I believe he can be — not to enable continued [problem].

## My Hopes for the Trust

[Write what you hope the trust accomplishes for this beneficiary.]

> I want this trust to provide [Name] with stable housing, medical care, treatment, and basic needs throughout their life. I want it to be available for genuine opportunities — education, vocational training, business start-up — but not to fund [risky behavior / lifestyle inflation / etc.].

## Specific Guidance

### What I'd Want You to Approve
- Direct payment of rent / mortgage on modest housing
- Health insurance and medical care
- Mental-health treatment and substance-abuse treatment
- Education and vocational training
- Reasonable transportation
- Modest celebrations and travel
- Emergency expenses

### What I'd Want You to Be Cautious About
- Cash distributions to the beneficiary (prefer direct-to-provider payment)
- Lump sums above $X without a clear plan
- Distributions during periods of [active addiction / instability / etc.]
- Distributions that would disqualify means-tested benefits
- Distributions to or for a partner you suspect is exploitive

### What I'd Probably Decline
- Funding luxury purchases (sports cars, expensive jewelry, etc.)
- Distributions during active substance abuse without treatment
- "Loans" to friends/partners
- Bail funds for criminal charges (use your judgment on minor matters)

## People Whose Counsel to Consult

When making difficult decisions, please consider input from:

- [Spouse / family member]: [Name, relationship, contact]
- [Treatment professional]: [Name, contact]
- [Trusted advisor]: [Name, contact]

You retain full discretion. They are advisors, not co-decision-makers.

## What I'd Want at the End

When [Beneficiary] dies, any remainder should pass to [contingent beneficiaries] per the trust terms. [Add any specific wishes about the remainder.]

## In Closing

Trustees have a hard job. You're balancing my wishes, the legal terms of the trust, and the real-time needs of someone I love. I trust your judgment. Take care of yourself. Reach out to my family / advisors when you need a sounding board.

With gratitude,

[Grantor signature]

[Date]
