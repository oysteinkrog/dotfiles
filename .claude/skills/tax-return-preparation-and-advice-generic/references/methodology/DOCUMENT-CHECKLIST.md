# Comprehensive Tax Document Checklist

Universal document checklist for US tax return preparation. Organized by category with document descriptions, form numbers, and notes on what each document contains. Not every item applies to every taxpayer -- use as a master inventory and mark items as applicable or N/A.

---

## 1. Prior Year Returns and Carryforwards

| Document | Why Needed |
|----------|------------|
| Prior year federal return (Form 1040, all schedules) | Baseline for comparison; carryforward amounts; depreciation schedules |
| Prior year state return(s) | State-specific carryforwards; verify state-specific elections |
| Prior year local/city return(s) | If applicable (NYC, Philadelphia, Ohio cities, etc.) |
| Prior year amended returns (Form 1040-X) | If corrections were made |
| IRS correspondence or notices | CP2000, audit letters, installment agreements |
| State tax authority correspondence | Notices, assessments, payment plans |
| Depreciation schedules (Form 4562 detail) | Asset-by-asset listing with basis, method, life, prior depreciation |
| Carryforward worksheets | NOL, capital loss, passive loss, charitable, business credits, AMT credit, foreign tax credit |
| Prior year estimated tax payment records | Amounts and dates of all 4 quarterly payments |
| Extension payment records | Amounts paid with Form 4868 |
| Prior year refund amount | Taxable if state refund was received and taxpayer itemized |

## 3. Income Documents

### Employment Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Wages, salary, tips | W-2 | All employers; verify Box 12 codes (retirement, HSA, etc.) |
| Allocation of tips | Form 4137 | If tips not reported to employer |
| Household employee wages received | Schedule H | If taxpayer was a household employee |

### Self-Employment / Business Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Non-employee compensation | 1099-NEC | Freelance, consulting, gig economy |
| Payment card / third-party payments | 1099-K | Platforms (Uber, Etsy, PayPal, Venmo, etc.) |
| Gross receipts records | Books/records | P&L statement, accounting software export |
| Cash income not reported on 1099 | Bank deposits, invoices | All income must be reported regardless of 1099 |

### Investment Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Interest income | 1099-INT | Banks, bonds, treasury securities |
| Dividend income | 1099-DIV | Qualified vs. ordinary dividends (Box 1a vs 1b) |
| Brokerage statements | 1099-B | Sales proceeds, cost basis, holding period |
| Consolidated brokerage statement | 1099-Composite | Combines INT, DIV, B, OID, MISC |
| Original Issue Discount | 1099-OID | Bonds purchased at discount |
| Mutual fund distributions | Year-end statements | Including reinvested dividends |
| ESPP (Employee Stock Purchase Plan) | 1099-B + 3922 | Basis adjustment needed for qualifying dispositions |
| ISO (Incentive Stock Options) | 3921 | AMT adjustment; basis tracking |
| RSU (Restricted Stock Units) | W-2 + 1099-B | Income on W-2; basis = FMV at vest |
| NQSO (Non-Qualified Stock Options) | W-2 + 1099-B | Spread on exercise included in W-2 |

### Crypto / Digital Assets
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Exchange transaction history | 1099-DA (new) or 1099-B | Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini, etc. |
| DeFi transaction records | Wallet records | Swaps, liquidity provision, staking rewards |
| Mining/staking income | Records | Ordinary income at FMV when received |
| Airdrop income | Records | Ordinary income at FMV when received |
| NFT sales | Records | Capital gain/loss; may also have creator royalties |
| Cost basis records | Purchase records | FIFO, LIFO, specific identification -- must be consistent |

### Rental and Real Estate Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Rental income received | 1099-MISC or records | From tenants; include security deposits if not returned |
| Rental platform income | 1099-K | Airbnb, VRBO, etc. |
| Royalty income | 1099-MISC Box 2 | Oil/gas, mineral rights, intellectual property |
| Real estate sale | 1099-S | Gross proceeds; may also get HUD-1/Closing Disclosure |
| Farm income | Schedule F | Agricultural operations |

### Retirement and Pension Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| IRA distributions | 1099-R | Box 7 code determines taxability |
| Pension/annuity distributions | 1099-R | May be partially taxable (basis recovery) |
| 401(k)/403(b) distributions | 1099-R | Including rollovers (code G) |
| Roth conversions | 1099-R | Code 02 or 07; fully taxable if no basis |
| Social Security benefits | SSA-1099 | Up to 85% taxable based on provisional income |
| Railroad retirement | RRB-1099 | Similar treatment to Social Security |

### Government and Other Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Unemployment compensation | 1099-G | Fully taxable at federal level |
| State/local tax refund | 1099-G | Taxable if taxpayer itemized in prior year |
| Gambling winnings | W-2G | Losses deductible to extent of winnings (Schedule A) |
| Alimony received | Records | Taxable if divorce/separation before 2019 |
| Jury duty pay | Records | Employer reimbursement may offset |
| Cancellation of debt | 1099-C | Taxable unless insolvent or bankrupt (Form 982) |
| Prizes and awards | 1099-MISC | Fair market value |
| Bartering income | 1099-B | Fair market value of goods/services received |
| HSA distributions | 1099-SA | Taxable + 20% penalty if not for qualified medical |

### Pass-Through Income
| Document | Form | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Partnership income/loss | Schedule K-1 (1065) | Includes ordinary income, guaranteed payments, capital gains, rental, credits |
| S-Corporation income/loss | Schedule K-1 (1120-S) | Distributions, wages (W-2 separate), basis tracking |
| Trust/estate income | Schedule K-1 (1041) | Beneficiary's share of trust income |
| REIT/PTP income | Varies | May have multiple K-1 components and QBI implications |

## 4. Deduction Documents

### Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)
| Document | Category | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Mortgage interest | 1098 | Box 1; acquisition debt vs. HELOC matters |
| Mortgage points paid | 1098 Box 6 | Deductible in year paid (purchase) or amortized (refi) |
| Mortgage insurance premiums (PMI) | 1098 Box 5 | Deductibility subject to AGI limits and legislative renewal |
| Property tax bills/receipts | Real estate tax | Subject to the personal SALT cap on Schedule A |
| State/local income tax paid | W-2 Box 17, estimates, prior balance | Subject to the personal SALT cap on Schedule A |
| State/local sales tax | Records/tables | Alternative to income tax; use IRS calculator for table amount |
| Vehicle registration (ad valorem portion) | Registration | Only the value-based portion is deductible |
| Charitable cash contributions | Receipts, bank statements | Written acknowledgment required for $250+ |
| Charitable non-cash contributions | Receipts, photos, appraisals | Qualified appraisal required for items > $5,000 |
| Charitable mileage | Mileage log | 14 cents/mile (2024) for charity driving |
| Medical/dental expenses | EOBs, receipts, insurance statements | Only amount exceeding 7.5% of AGI |
| Medical miles driven | Log | Standard medical mileage rate |
| Long-term care insurance premiums | Statements | Age-based limits on deductible amount |
| Casualty/theft losses | Police reports, insurance, appraisals | Only federally declared disaster areas (post-TCJA) |
| Gambling losses | Session logs, W-2G | Limited to gambling winnings |

### Above-the-Line Deductions
| Document | Deduction | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Student loan interest | 1098-E | Up to $2,500; phases out at higher income |
| Tuition and fees | 1098-T | For education credits (not a direct deduction post-2020) |
| Educator expenses | Receipts | Up to $300 for qualifying K-12 educators |
| HSA contributions | 5498-SA, records | Employer contributions on W-2 Box 12 code W |
| IRA contributions | 5498 | Deductibility depends on income and plan coverage |
| Self-employed health insurance | Premium statements | 100% of premiums; cannot exceed SE net income |
| SE tax deduction | Calculated | 50% of SE tax is above-the-line |
| Alimony paid | Records, decree | Deductible if divorce/separation before 2019 |
| Moving expenses | Records | Military only (post-TCJA) |
| Penalty on early savings withdrawal | 1099-INT Box 2 | CD early withdrawal penalties |

## 12. Optimization Phase Checklist

After all documents are gathered and the return is drafted, use this checklist to verify optimization:

### Income Optimization
- [ ] All income sources reported (cross-reference bank deposits)
- [ ] Qualified vs. ordinary dividend classification verified
- [ ] Capital gains holding periods verified (LTCG vs. STCG)
- [ ] Tax-exempt interest reported but not taxed (municipal bonds)
- [ ] Social Security taxability calculated correctly
- [ ] Cancellation of debt exclusion applied if insolvent

### Deduction Optimization
- [ ] Standard vs. itemized comparison performed
- [ ] SALT deduction optimized ($10,000 cap; PTET alternative)
- [ ] Charitable bunching / DAF strategy evaluated
- [ ] QBI deduction calculated (Section 199A) with all limitations
- [ ] Home office deduction: simplified vs. regular compared
- [ ] Vehicle: standard mileage vs. actual expense compared
- [ ] Retirement contribution maximized (all available plans)
- [ ] HSA contribution maximized
- [ ] Self-employed health insurance deduction claimed
- [ ] Student loan interest deduction claimed

### Credit Optimization
- [ ] Child Tax Credit / Other Dependent Credit claimed
- [ ] Education credits evaluated (AOTC vs. LLC)
- [ ] Child and Dependent Care Credit claimed
- [ ] Clean Vehicle Credit claimed if applicable
- [ ] Residential energy credits claimed
- [ ] Foreign Tax Credit vs. deduction compared
- [ ] Retirement Saver's Credit evaluated
- [ ] General Business Credits reviewed (Form 3800)

### Entity and Timing Optimization
- [ ] S-Corp vs. sole proprietorship comparison performed
- [ ] Reasonable salary analysis documented (if S-Corp)
- [ ] Retirement contribution vehicles compared (SEP vs. Solo 401k vs. DB plan)
- [ ] Income timing evaluated (defer or accelerate)
- [ ] Roth conversion opportunity evaluated
- [ ] Tax-loss harvesting opportunities identified
- [ ] Carryforwards from prior years utilized where possible

### Compliance and Risk
- [ ] FBAR required? (Foreign accounts > $10,000 aggregate)
- [ ] FATCA reporting required? (Form 8938)
- [ ] Cryptocurrency question answered on Form 1040
- [ ] All K-1s received and reconciled
- [ ] Estimated tax penalty evaluated; annualization if beneficial
- [ ] AMT calculated and compared
- [ ] Underpayment penalty computed or safe harbor verified
- [ ] Return cross-references prior year for consistency
- [ ] All elections properly made on return (de minimis, etc.)
