# US Tax Deadlines Calendar

Complete calendar of federal and common state tax deadlines, irrecoverable elections, and strategic planning windows. All dates assume calendar-year taxpayers. When a deadline falls on a weekend or federal holiday, it moves to the next business day.

---

## January

| Date | Deadline | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Jan 1** | Tax year begins | New year's tax planning window opens |
| **Jan 1** | Roth conversion window opens | Can convert traditional IRA to Roth for new tax year |
| **Jan 1** | Annual gift exclusion resets | New $18,000/$36,000 (2024) annual exclusion per donee |
| **Jan 10** | Singled-out: Form W-4 exemption renewal | Employees claiming exempt must file new W-4 |
| **Jan 15** | **Q4 Estimated Tax Payment (prior year)** | Form 1040-ES; last quarterly payment for prior tax year |
| **Jan 15** | Farmer/fisherman special rule | If 2/3+ of gross income is farming/fishing, single estimated payment due |
| **Jan 31** | **Employer W-2 / 1099-NEC deadline** | Must be furnished to recipients and filed with SSA/IRS |
| **Jan 31** | File-and-pay alternative to Q4 estimated | Filing full return with payment by Jan 31 avoids Q4 penalty |
| **Jan 31** | Form 1099-NEC due to IRS | Non-employee compensation reporting |
| **Late Jan** | 1099-B, 1099-DIV, 1099-INT issued | Brokerage/bank statements begin arriving |

**Planning Window:** Review prior year for carryforwards. Begin gathering documents. Model Roth conversion opportunities for the new year.

## March

| Date | Deadline | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Mar 1** | Farmer/fisherman return due | If did not pay estimated tax by Jan 15, must file and pay by Mar 1 |
| **Mar 15** | **S-Corp return due (Form 1120-S)** | Calendar-year S-Corps; K-1s due to shareholders |
| **Mar 15** | **Partnership return due (Form 1065)** | Calendar-year partnerships; K-1s due to partners |
| **Mar 15** | S-Corp extension (Form 7004) | 6-month extension to September 15 |
| **Mar 15** | Partnership extension (Form 7004) | 6-month extension to September 15 |
| **Mar 15** | **S-Corp election (Form 2553)** | For calendar-year corps wanting S-Corp status for current year |
| **Mar 15** | **PTET election deadline (many states)** | State-specific; some as early as March 15 or even earlier |
| **Mar 15** | FSA grace period ends (if applicable) | Plans with grace period (not all have this) |
| **Late Mar** | K-1s from S-Corps and partnerships | Needed before individual return can be completed |

**Planning Window:** Critical month. S-Corp elections and PTET elections are often irrecoverable if missed. Ensure all entity-level returns are filed or extended.

## April

| Date | Deadline | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Apr 1** | Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) | First RMD for taxpayers who turned 73 in prior year (one-time delay) |
| **Apr 15** | **Individual return due (Form 1040)** | Or file Form 4868 for 6-month extension |
| **Apr 15** | **Tax payment due** | Extension extends filing, NOT payment. Pay estimated tax owed. |
| **Apr 15** | **IRA/Roth IRA contribution deadline** | For prior tax year. NO extension possible. Absolute deadline. |
| **Apr 15** | **HSA contribution deadline** | For prior tax year. NO extension possible. |
| **Apr 15** | **Coverdell ESA contribution deadline** | For prior tax year |
| **Apr 15** | Gift tax return (Form 709) | If gifts exceeded annual exclusion |
| **Apr 15** | Trust/Estate return (Form 1041) | Calendar-year trusts and estates |
| **Apr 15** | **Q1 Estimated Tax Payment (current year)** | Form 1040-ES for current tax year |
| **Apr 15** | FBAR due (FinCEN 114) | Auto-extended to October 15 |
| **Apr 15** | Form 5500 (calendar year plans) | Employee benefit plan annual return (extension available) |
| **Apr 15** | Excess IRA contribution removal | Remove excess + earnings to avoid 6% penalty |
| **Apr 15** | Recharacterization deadline | For prior year IRA contributions |

**Planning Window:** The most critical deadline cluster. IRA and HSA deadlines cannot be extended. File extension if not ready, but pay estimated tax to avoid penalties and interest.

## Irrecoverable Deadlines (No Recovery If Missed)

These deadlines, once passed, cannot be retroactively met. The opportunity is permanently lost.

| Deadline | Date | Consequence |
|----------|------|-------------|
| IRA/Roth IRA contribution | April 15 (no extension) | Year's contribution room lost forever |
| HSA contribution | April 15 (no extension) | Year's contribution room lost forever |
| Solo 401(k) adoption / contribution timing | Verify live | Depends on entity type and whether the contribution is an elective deferral or employer contribution |
| Roth conversion | December 31 | Cannot convert for prior year |
| 1031 exchange identification | 45 days from sale | Exchange fails; gain fully taxable |
| 1031 exchange completion | 180 days from sale | Exchange fails |
| S-Corp election (without relief) | March 15 | Miss current year (though late relief exists) |
| Tax-loss harvesting | December 31 | Losses not available until following year |
| RMD (post-first year) | December 31 | 25% excise tax on shortfall (10% if corrected timely) |
| Qualified small business stock (Section 1202) | Must hold 5 years | Cannot retroactively meet holding period |
| Charitable bunching year | December 31 | Cannot bunch retroactively |
| Form 8832 entity classification election | 75 days retroactive max | Effective date limited to 75 days before filing date; cannot be backdated further. Critical for LLC-to-C-Corp conversions for QSBS qualification |
| Qualified Opportunity Zone 180-day investment | 180 days from sale/exchange | Must invest capital gains in a QOF within 180 days of the gain event. The 180-day window start date varies (e.g., K-1 gains may start at partner's tax year end or other dates). Window is irrecoverable once passed |
| SIMPLE IRA rollover to non-SIMPLE plan | 2 years from first contribution | 60-day rollover window applies generally, but rolling a SIMPLE IRA to a non-SIMPLE plan (401(k), Traditional IRA) before the 2-year waiting period triggers a 25% penalty (not 10%). The 2-year clock starts from the date of the first employer contribution |
| Form 8283 written acknowledgment (non-cash >$250) | Before return filing date | For non-cash charitable contributions over $250, a contemporaneous written acknowledgment must be obtained BEFORE the return is filed. Cannot be obtained retroactively after filing — deduction disallowed without it |

## Extension Strategy and Safe Harbor Rules

### Extension Mechanics

- **Form 4868:** Automatic 6-month extension for individuals (to October 15)
- **Form 7004:** Automatic extensions for business entities
- Extensions extend **filing** only, NOT **payment**
- Underpayment interest accrues from April 15 regardless of extension
- Late payment penalty: 0.5% per month (max 25%) on unpaid balance
- Late filing penalty: 5% per month (max 25%) -- much more severe than late payment
- **Always file an extension rather than filing late**

### Safe Harbor for Estimated Taxes

Avoid underpayment penalty by meeting one of:

| Safe Harbor | Requirement | Best For |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **100% prior year tax** | Pay 100% of prior year's total tax liability | Lower income (<$150K AGI) |
| **110% prior year tax** | Pay 110% of prior year's total tax liability | AGI > $150K ($75K MFS) |
| **90% current year tax** | Pay 90% of current year's actual liability | Declining income years |
| **Annualized income method** | Calculate tax based on income received in each quarter | Very uneven income |

**Key Points:**
- W-2 withholding counts as paid evenly throughout the year (even if withheld in December)
- Estimated tax payments are date-specific (must be timely per quarter)
- Can increase W-2 withholding in Q4 to avoid penalty for missed quarterly payments
- No penalty if tax owed is less than $1,000

## Quarterly Review Cadence Checklist

### Q1 Review (January - March)
- [ ] Gather all prior year tax documents
- [ ] Review prior year return for carryforwards
- [ ] Calculate Q1 estimated tax payment
- [ ] Evaluate S-Corp election (deadline: March 15)
- [ ] Evaluate state PTET election (check state deadline)
- [ ] Make prior year IRA contribution if not yet done
- [ ] Make prior year HSA contribution if not yet done
- [ ] File or extend entity returns by March 15
- [ ] Review W-4 withholding for current year
- [ ] Assess retirement plan type and contribution strategy

### Q2 Review (April - June)
- [ ] File or extend individual return by April 15
- [ ] Make current year IRA contribution (can start immediately)
- [ ] Calculate Q2 estimated tax payment
- [ ] Mid-year income projection
- [ ] Review investment portfolio for tax-loss harvesting candidates
- [ ] Evaluate Roth conversion strategy based on YTD income
- [ ] Check state-specific deadlines (vary widely)

### Q3 Review (July - September)
- [ ] Calculate Q3 estimated tax payment
- [ ] Comprehensive mid-year tax projection
- [ ] File extended entity returns by September 15
- [ ] Begin year-end planning strategy modeling
- [ ] Review and update beneficiary designations
- [ ] Evaluate equipment/vehicle purchases for depreciation
- [ ] Assess whether cost segregation study is warranted for real property
- [ ] Evaluate defined benefit plan establishment if appropriate

### Q4 Review (October - December)
- [ ] File extended individual return by October 15
- [ ] Execute tax-loss harvesting by December 31
- [ ] Execute Roth conversions by December 31
- [ ] Maximize retirement contributions (Solo 401k, SEP, defined benefit)
- [ ] Make charitable contributions (especially appreciated stock to DAF)
- [ ] Verify owner-only 401(k) adoption and contribution deadlines for the taxpayer's entity type
- [ ] Review and execute SALT strategies (PTET payments, property tax timing)
- [ ] Review and execute gift tax annual exclusion gifts
- [ ] Calculate Q4 estimated tax payment (due January 15)
- [ ] Evaluate income deferral/acceleration for year-end
- [ ] Spend FSA balances
- [ ] Clear traditional IRA for backdoor Roth (roll to 401k by Dec 31)
- [ ] Take Required Minimum Distributions (if applicable)
- [ ] Section 179 / bonus depreciation asset purchases
- [ ] Final estimated tax true-up (adjust W-2 withholding if needed)
- [ ] Document all year-end positions and rationale

## Advanced Strategy Deadlines

### Augusta Rule (Section 280A(g))
- **Setup:** Document FMV rental rate before any rental events occur
- **Execution:** Rent personal residence to own business for up to 14 days
- **Documentation:** Written lease, board minutes/business purpose, FMV comparable analysis
- **Deadline:** Each rental event must be documented contemporaneously; no retroactive application
- **Amount:** Up to 14 days at FMV, income excluded from homeowner's return; deductible by business

### R&D Credit (Section 41)
- **Claim with:** Timely filed return (including extensions)
- **Amended claim:** Within 3 years of original filing date or 2 years of payment
- **Startup credit election:** Up to $500,000 against payroll tax (Form 6765, Part III)
- **Documentation:** Must be contemporaneous; four-part test must be met for each activity

### Qualified Small Business Stock (Section 1202)
- **Holding period:** Must hold for 5+ years
- **Exclusion:** Up to 100% of gain (for stock acquired after 9/27/2010)
- **Cap:** Greater of $10M or 10x basis
- **Planning:** No deadline per se, but must plan acquisition and holding period; cannot convert entity type after issuance

### Opportunity Zone (Section 1400Z-2)
- **Investment deadline:** 180 days from capital gain recognition
- **Holding for basis step-up:** Originally 5/7 years for 10%/15% basis increase (expired for new investments)
- **10-year exclusion:** Hold for 10+ years to exclude gain on OZ investment itself
- **Sunset:** Program sunsets December 31, 2026 (current law)

### Net Operating Loss (NOL) Planning
- **Carryforward:** Indefinite (post-2017 NOLs)
- **Limitation:** 80% of taxable income (post-2017 NOLs)
- **Pre-2018 NOLs:** 20-year carryforward, no percentage limitation
- **No carryback** for most taxpayers (farming exception: 2-year carryback)

### State-Specific Advanced Deadlines
- **CA LLC fee:** Due by June 15 (estimated) and with return
- **NY PTET:** Election by March 15; payments quarterly
- **NJ BAIT:** Election annually with return; estimated payments quarterly
- **CT PE Tax:** Mandatory for most pass-throughs; estimated payments
- **States with no income tax:** AK, FL, NV, NH (interest/dividends only through 2024, then fully none), SD, TN (none as of 2021), TX, WA (capital gains tax on high earners), WY

## Holiday and Weekend Adjustments

When a deadline falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal federal holiday, it moves to the next business day. Common adjustments:

- **Emancipation Day (DC holiday, April 16):** When April 15 falls on a weekend and April 16 is Emancipation Day, the filing deadline may shift to April 17 or 18
- **Patriots' Day (MA/ME, third Monday of April):** Can affect return mailing from those states
- **State holidays:** May independently shift state deadlines

Always verify the exact deadline for the current tax year using the IRS calendar and relevant state tax authority.
