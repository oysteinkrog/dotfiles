# New York Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes (NYS + NYC + Yonkers)
- Top marginal rate: 10.9% (NYS) + 3.876% (NYC) = 14.776% combined for NYC residents
- Filing requirement threshold: $4,000 (Single), $0 if NYC/Yonkers resident with income

## IMPORTANT: New York is the most complex state for individual income tax. NYS, NYC, and Yonkers each have separate tax calculations. Part-year and nonresident rules are particularly intricate.

## Yonkers Surcharge
- Yonkers residents: **16.75% surcharge** on their NYS tax liability (not on income — on the computed NYS tax)
- Yonkers nonresidents who work in Yonkers: **0.5%** of wages earned in Yonkers
- Filed on Form Y-203 (nonresident) or computed on IT-201

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- NYS standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $8,000
  - MFJ: $16,050
  - HOH: $11,200
  - MFS: $8,000
  - Dependent: $3,100
- Personal exemption: $1,000 per dependent (NY uses dependent exemption, not personal exemption for taxpayer/spouse)
- NYC: Uses the same standard deduction as NYS (no separate NYC standard deduction)
- NYS allows itemized deductions with modifications (see below)
- NY starts with federal AGI

### NYS Itemized Deduction Modifications
- State/local income taxes: NOT deductible on NY return (add back required)
- Real property taxes: Deductible on NY return
- Mortgage interest: Deductible
- Charitable contributions: Deductible
- Medical expenses: Deductible (following federal thresholds)
- High-income limitation: Itemized deductions are limited for AGI over $525,000 (50% reduction for those over $1M)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits

### Major Credits
1. **STAR credit/exemption**: School Tax Relief for primary residence. Basic STAR: homeowners with income up to $500,000. Enhanced STAR: age 65+ with income up to $107,300 (2025-26; adjusted annually). New applicants receive a check (credit) rather than exemption
2. **NYC School Tax Credit**: $63 (Single) or $125 (MFJ) for full-year NYC residents with income under $250,000
3. **NYS/NYC Earned Income Credit**: NYS: 30% of federal EITC (refundable). NYC: additional 5% of federal EITC for NYC residents
4. **NYS Child and Dependent Care Credit**: Refundable; percentage varies by income (20-110% of federal credit)
5. **College Tuition Credit/Deduction**: Up to $400 credit or $10,000 deduction for qualifying tuition
6. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $5,000 ($10,000 MFJ) for contributions to NY 529 plans
7. **NYC Household Credit**: $15-$30 per person in household (income-limited)
8. **Real Property Tax Credit**: For homeowners/renters with income < $18,000 and property tax > specific thresholds
9. **Long-term care insurance credit**: 20% of premiums paid
10. **NYC UBT credit**: Credit against NYC personal income tax for Unincorporated Business Tax paid

### Deductions
- **529 Plan**: $5,000 per taxpayer ($10,000 MFJ) for NY 529 contributions
- **Public employee 414(h) contributions**: Subtracted from federal AGI
- **NYC flexible benefits (IRC 125)**: Added back for NYC tax (NY conforms but NYC does not for certain items)

## Filing Requirements

### Forms
- **IT-201**: Full-year resident return (NYS, NYC, Yonkers)
- **IT-203**: Nonresident and part-year resident return
- **IT-360.1**: Change of City Resident Status (NYC part-year filers)
- **IT-201-ATT**: Additional taxes and credits
- **IT-225**: NY State modifications (additions/subtractions)
- **IT-196**: NY itemized deductions
- **IT-214**: Real property tax credit
- **IT-215**: Earned Income Credit
- **IT-2**: W-2 summary

### Due Date
- April 15

### Extension
- Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) — file IT-370
- Must pay estimated tax with extension request
- NYC has no separate extension; follows NYS

---

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $300 (after withholding and credits)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax. For high-income filers (AGI > $150,000 in prior year): must pay 110% of prior year
- Form: IT-2105 (NYS/NYC/Yonkers combined)
- NYC and Yonkers estimated taxes are included on the same voucher as NYS

---

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- **Available**: Yes (enacted 2021, effective for 2021+)
- **Election deadline**: **March 15** of the tax year (this is EARLY and easy to miss)
- **Annual election**: Must be made each year; irrevocable once made
- **Rate**: Graduated rates on PTE taxable income:
  - Up to $2M: 6.85%
  - $2M - $5M: 9.65%
  - $5M - $25M: 10.30%
  - Over $25M: 10.90%
- **Mechanics**: Entity pays PTET on the aggregate NY-source income of electing members; members receive a refundable credit on their individual returns
- **NYC PTET**: Separate NYC PTET available for partnerships and S corps with NYC income; rate is 3.876% (flat); separate election required
- **Estimated payments**: Entity must make quarterly estimated payments (March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15 — note: different schedule than individual estimates)
- **Who can elect**: Partnerships and S corps (not sole proprietorships or single-member LLCs taxed as disregarded entities)

## LLC Filing Fees
New York imposes an **annual filing fee** on LLCs based on New York-source gross income (NOT net income):
| NY-Source Gross Income | Annual Fee |
|------------------------|-----------|
| $0 - $100,000 | $25 |
| $100,001 - $250,000 | $50 |
| $250,001 - $500,000 | $175 |
| $500,001 - $1,000,000 | $500 |
| $1,000,001 - $5,000,000 | $1,500 |
| $5,000,001 - $25,000,000 | $3,000 |
| Over $25,000,000 | $4,500 |

This fee applies to LLCs and LLPs regardless of whether they are taxed as partnerships or S corps. Filed on Form IT-204-LL.

## Local/City Income Taxes

### New York City
- Full separate tax system with its own rates (see NYC rates above)
- Only applies to NYC residents (all 5 boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island)
- Nonresidents working in NYC do NOT owe NYC income tax (they owe NYS tax on NYC-source income)
- NYC Unincorporated Business Tax (UBT): 4% on business income over $95,000 for businesses operating in NYC

### Yonkers
- Residents: 16.75% surcharge on NYS tax
- Nonresidents working in Yonkers: 0.5% of Yonkers wages

### MTA Surcharge (Metropolitan Commuter Transportation Mobility Tax)
- 0.34% on net self-employment earnings for self-employed individuals in the MTA district (NYC + Dutchess, Nassau, Orange, Putnam, Rockland, Suffolk, Westchester counties)
- Employers in MTA district pay MCTMT on payroll

## Convenience of the Employer Rule
**CRITICAL for remote workers**: New York applies the "convenience of the employer" doctrine:
- If your employer is in NY and you work remotely from another state for your OWN convenience (not the employer's necessity), NY will tax that income as NY-source
- This rule affects remote workers who moved out of NY during/after COVID but still work for NY employers
- Few states follow this rule (only NY, PA, NE, and a handful of others)
- Can result in double taxation if the remote worker's home state also claims the income
- NY provides NO credit for taxes paid to the home state; the home state may or may not provide a credit for NY tax
- Exception: If the employer assigns the employee to an out-of-state office and the remote work is for the employer's necessity, the income may not be NY-source

## Special Features and Gotchas

1. **NYC + NYS combined rate**: NYC residents face a combined state + city rate of up to 14.776% (10.9% + 3.876%), among the highest in the nation. Adding federal (37%), the top combined marginal rate exceeds 50%

2. **PTET March 15 deadline**: The NY PTET election must be made by March 15. Missing this deadline means the entity cannot elect PTET for the year. This is earlier than most states

3. **Convenience of employer rule**: Remote workers beware. NY aggressively applies this rule and audits out-of-state filers

4. **LLC annual filing fee**: Based on GROSS income, not net. An LLC with $5M+ gross but losses still owes $3,000-$4,500

5. **Supplemental tax recapture**: High earners face a recapture that effectively applies the supplemental rates to ALL income, not just income above the threshold. The computation is on IT-201, lines 39-43

6. **STAR program complexity**: New homeowners must register for STAR credit (no longer applied as an exemption for new applicants). Enhanced STAR requires annual income verification

7. **NY Publication 36**: Required reading for part-year residents and nonresidents — defines allocation rules for income sourcing

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from NYS and NYC income tax
- **NYS/NYC government pensions**: Fully exempt (NY, NYC, and federal government pensions are all exempt)
- **Federal government pensions**: Fully exempt
- **Military retirement**: Fully exempt
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: First $20,000 is exempt for filers age 59.5+. Amount above $20,000 is taxable
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (both tiers)

---

## Part-Year and Nonresident Rules
- **Part-year residents**: Must file IT-203 and allocate income between resident and nonresident periods. NYC part-year filers also file IT-360.1
- **Nonresidents**: Taxed only on NY-source income; file IT-203
- **Statutory residents**: Present in NY 183+ days AND maintain a permanent place of abode = treated as resident for full year (even if domiciled elsewhere). This is aggressively audited
- **Domicile vs. residence**: NY distinguishes between domicile (permanent home) and statutory residence (183-day + abode test). Both can trigger full-year resident treatment
- **Audit risk**: NY aggressively audits high-income filers who claim to have changed domicile. Document the change thoroughly (cell phone records, driver's license, voter registration, etc.)

---

## MTA Surcharge Details
- Self-employed individuals in MTA district: 0.34% of net self-employment earnings allocated to MTA district
- MTA district: NYC + Dutchess, Nassau, Orange, Putnam, Rockland, Suffolk, Westchester
- Reported on Form MTA-6 (for self-employed) or withheld by employers
- This is a payroll/SE tax, not an income tax, but is reported on the individual return
