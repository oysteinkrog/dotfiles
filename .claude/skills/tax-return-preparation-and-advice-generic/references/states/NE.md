# Nebraska Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.84%
- Filing requirement threshold: $7,100 (Single), $14,200 (MFJ)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single and MFJ (same brackets, different thresholds)
| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|------|--------|-----|
| 2.46% | $0 - $3,700 | $0 - $7,390 |
| 3.51% | $3,701 - $22,170 | $7,391 - $44,350 |
| 5.01% | $22,171 - $35,730 | $44,351 - $71,460 |
| 5.84% | Over $35,730 | Over $71,460 |

### Head of Household
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 2.46% | $0 - $6,860 |
| 3.51% | $6,861 - $35,560 |
| 5.01% | $35,561 - $51,600 |
| 5.84% | Over $51,600 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: Nebraska follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption credit: $157 per exemption

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Social Security benefits**: Phased exclusion underway; for 2025, taxpayers with AGI under $100,000 (MFJ) can exclude a significant portion; full exemption targeted by 2025
2. **Military retirement pay**: Fully exempt from Nebraska income tax (effective 2022+)
3. **Property tax credit**: Nebraska Property Tax Incentive Act credit (refundable)
4. **Community Development Assistance Act credit**: Contributions to approved projects
5. **Beginning Farmer credit**: Tax credit for qualifying agricultural activities
6. **Child/Dependent Care credit**: Percentage of federal credit
7. **Earned Income Tax Credit**: 10% of federal EITC (refundable)
8. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $10,000 ($5,000 if MFS) for NEST 529 contributions

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 1040N (resident), Form 1040XN (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 7-month extension with federal extension (file by November 15)
- e-File: Available through state portal and commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $500
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: 1040N-ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Due with the entity's return (annually)
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays tax at highest marginal rate (5.84%) on members' distributive shares; members receive credit on individual return
- Election is irrevocable for the tax year once made

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Nebraska does not impose local or city income taxes
- However, local option sales taxes exist in many jurisdictions

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Social Security taxation transition**: Nebraska has been phasing out SS taxation; verify current-year exclusion amounts carefully as they change annually
2. **Property tax credit on income tax return**: The refundable property tax credit (from LB 1107) is claimed on the income tax return, not separately
3. **Nebraska sources**: Income from Nebraska sources includes real property, business operations, and services performed in state
4. **Agricultural emphasis**: Multiple credits target farming operations; verify client eligibility
5. **AMT**: Nebraska does not have its own AMT

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Being phased to full exemption; check current year thresholds (AGI-based phaseout)
- **Military retirement**: Fully exempt
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable, no special exclusion
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- **Railroad Retirement Tier II**: Taxable
