# Louisiana Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.25%
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds personal exemption + applicable standard deduction; generally $12,500 (Single) / $25,000 (MFJ)
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Louisiana Department of Revenue (LDR)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $12,500 | 1.85% |
| $12,501 - $50,000 | 3.5% |
| Over $50,000 | 4.25% |

### Married Filing Jointly
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $25,000 | 1.85% |
| $25,001 - $100,000 | 3.5% |
| Over $100,000 | 4.25% |

Note: Louisiana reformed its tax brackets significantly in 2024, moving from a 2-4.25% system to 1.85-4.25% with different thresholds. Verify current year brackets.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Louisiana conforms to federal standard deduction for 2025:
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemption: Louisiana adopted the federal standard deduction in lieu of its previous combined exemption/deduction system as part of 2024 reforms
- Dependent credit: $1,000 per dependent (refundable for qualifying families)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Federal income tax deduction** — Louisiana allows deduction of federal income taxes paid (one of few states). After 2024 reform, this may be capped or modified — verify current rules.
2. **Excess itemized deductions** — Louisiana allows the excess of federal itemized deductions over the standard deduction as a deduction
3. **School readiness credit** — Credits for child care expenses and contributions to child care facilities
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $2,400 per beneficiary ($4,800 MFJ) for START Saving Program contributions
5. **Homestead exemption** — First $75,000 of assessed value exempt from property tax (assessment ratio = 10%, so protects first $7,500 of assessed value)
6. **Earned income credit** — 5% of federal EITC (refundable)
7. **Inventory tax credit** — Credit for ad valorem taxes paid on inventory (business-focused)
8. **Historic preservation credit** — 25% of qualified rehabilitation expenditures
9. **Child credit** — $1,000 refundable credit per dependent for qualifying lower-income families

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IT-540 (resident), IT-540B (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: May 15 (NOTE: Louisiana's deadline is May 15, not April 15)
- Extension: Automatic extension to November 15 (no form needed, but pay estimated tax by May 15)
- E-file options: Louisiana File Online (free for qualifying filers), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15 (NOTE: estimated payments still use federal schedule, even though filing deadline is May 15)
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2019, Act 442; expanded and modified)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's return, due by the 15th day of the 4th month after the close of the tax year
- Rate: Graduated rates matching individual brackets (1.85% / 3.5% / 4.25%)
- Mechanics: Electing entities (S-corps, partnerships, LLCs) pay tax at entity level. Members claim a credit on their Louisiana return. Entity must make the election annually.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Louisiana does not have local/city income taxes
- However, Louisiana has extensive **local sales and use taxes** — parishes and municipalities levy their own sales taxes
- Combined state + local sales tax can reach 11-12% in some parishes (state rate 4.45% + parish/city rates)
- Louisiana has some of the highest combined sales tax rates in the nation

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **May 15 filing deadline** — Louisiana uses May 15, not April 15. This is one of the latest state deadlines. Federal estimated payments are still due April 15.
2. **Federal tax deduction** — Louisiana is one of a handful of states that allows deducting federal income taxes paid. This significantly reduces effective state tax rates. Post-2024 reform details should be verified.
3. **Community property state** — Louisiana is a community property state; income must be split between spouses on separate returns
4. **2024 tax reform** — Major changes took effect in 2024-2025 (brackets, standard deduction adoption, credit restructuring). Always verify current-year rules.
5. **Parish sales tax complexity** — Local sales taxes are administered separately from the state; businesses operating in multiple parishes face significant compliance burden

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Louisiana income tax
- **State pensions (LASERS, TRSL)**: Fully exempt from Louisiana income tax
- **Federal pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income; may qualify for retirement income exclusion
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt from Louisiana income tax
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable, but up to $6,000 per person exclusion for age 65+ (Louisiana retirement income exclusion)
- **Louisiana is moderately retirement-friendly** — Low rates, SS exempt, state pensions exempt
