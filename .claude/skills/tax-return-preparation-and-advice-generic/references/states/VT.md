# Vermont Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 8.75%
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if required to file federal return and had VT income

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.35% | $0 - $45,400 |
| 6.60% | $45,401 - $110,050 |
| 7.60% | $110,051 - $229,550 |
| 8.75% | Over $229,550 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.35% | $0 - $75,850 |
| 6.60% | $75,851 - $183,400 |
| 7.60% | $183,401 - $279,450 |
| 8.75% | Over $279,450 |

### Head of Household
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.35% | $0 - $60,600 |
| 6.60% | $60,601 - $146,700 |
| 7.60% | $146,701 - $254,500 |
| 8.75% | Over $254,500 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: Vermont follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption: $4,850 per exemption (VT retained personal exemptions; phases out at higher AGI)
- Itemized deductions: VT allows itemized deductions but limits them for high-income filers
- VT starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Vermont Earned Income Tax Credit**: 38% of federal EITC (refundable)
2. **Child and Dependent Care Credit**: 72% of federal credit (24% refundable)
3. **Vermont Child Tax Credit**: $1,000 per qualifying child under 6 (income-phased, refundable)
4. **Social Security exemption**: Fully exempt from VT income tax (effective 2025)
5. **Capital gains exclusion**: VT excludes 40% of adjusted net capital gains (effective rate reduction)
6. **529 Plan credit**: 10% of first $2,500 contributed ($5,000 MFJ) to VT 529 plan, max credit $250/$500
7. **Charitable contribution credit**: 5% of charitable contributions (capped at certain levels)
8. **Renter rebate**: Property tax equivalent credit for qualifying renters
9. **Homeowner property tax credit**: Based on household income and property tax paid

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IN-111 (resident), IN-113 (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- VT starts with federal AGI; applies VT modifications
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $500
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: IN-114 (estimated payment voucher)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made annually; election on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays VT income tax at applicable rates on distributive shares; members receive credit
- Applies to S corps and partnerships
- Annual election; irrevocable for the year

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in Vermont
- Property taxes are the primary local revenue source (and are relatively high)
- Education property tax is significant (homestead rate and non-residential rate set by state)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **High tax state**: VT has one of the highest top marginal rates (8.75%); combined with federal, effective rates are very high for high earners
2. **Social Security now exempt**: Effective 2025, VT fully exempts Social Security benefits (previously had income-based partial taxation)
3. **Capital gains 40% exclusion**: VT's 40% exclusion of adjusted net capital gains significantly reduces effective rate on investment income
4. **Property tax credit / renter rebate**: Important programs for low-to-moderate income residents; filed with income tax return (Form HS-122)
5. **Small state, close conformity**: VT generally follows federal closely but has notable differences in treatment of capital gains, itemized deduction limits, and personal exemptions

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt (effective 2025; previously income-tested)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable at regular rates; no special exclusion
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no specific exemption in VT)
- **Capital gains exclusion**: 40% exclusion benefits retirees with investment gains
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal)
- **Property tax credit**: Retirees on lower incomes may qualify for substantial property tax relief
