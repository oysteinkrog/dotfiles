# Wisconsin Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 7.65%
- Filing requirement threshold: $12,760 (Single), $23,620 (MFJ) — WI-specific thresholds

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.50% | $0 - $14,320 |
| 4.40% | $14,321 - $28,640 |
| 5.30% | $28,641 - $315,310 |
| 7.65% | Over $315,310 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.50% | $0 - $19,090 |
| 4.40% | $19,091 - $38,190 |
| 5.30% | $38,191 - $420,420 |
| 7.65% | Over $420,420 |

### Head of Household
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.50% | $0 - $14,320 |
| 4.40% | $14,321 - $28,640 |
| 5.30% | $28,641 - $315,310 |
| 7.65% | Over $315,310 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $12,760
  - MFJ: $23,620
  - HOH: $16,740
  - Note: WI standard deduction is lower than federal and PHASES OUT at higher incomes
- Standard deduction phaseout: Begins at $18,810 (Single) / $24,680 (MFJ); fully phased out at higher levels
- Personal exemption: $700 per exemption (very small)
- Wisconsin starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Itemized deduction credit**: WI allows a 5% credit on certain itemized deductions (medical expenses, mortgage interest, charitable contributions) instead of a direct deduction
2. **College savings deduction**: Up to $3,860 per beneficiary ($1,930 MFS) for Edvest 529 contributions
3. **Wisconsin Earned Income Credit**: 4% (1 child), 11% (2 children), 34% (3+ children) of federal EITC (refundable)
4. **Homestead credit**: Refundable credit for low-income homeowners and renters (based on property taxes or rent equivalent)
5. **School property tax credit**: Available to most filers; claimed on Schedule H
6. **Working families credit**: For qualifying working families
7. **Veterans and surviving spouses credit**: Property tax credit for qualifying veterans
8. **Manufacturing and agriculture credit**: 7.5% of qualifying production income (effectively reduces rate to near 0% for qualifying income)
9. **Married couple credit**: Up to $480 for two-earner married couples

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 1 (resident), Form 1NPR (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 7-month extension (November 15) — note: longer than most states
- WI starts with federal AGI
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $200 (after withholding and credits)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: 1-ES
- Underpayment interest rate: Variable, set quarterly

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (WI PTET effective 2018; expanded)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity elects to pay WI tax on behalf of nonresident members; rate matches individual rates
- Primarily used for nonresident withholding; WI's PTET predates the SALT workaround trend but has been adapted for SALT cap purposes
- Entity files Form PW-1

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in Wisconsin
- Local revenue from property taxes and local sales taxes
- County sales tax: 0.5% allowed (most counties impose it); combined rate typically 5.5%
- Special district taxes: Stadium tax in 5-county Milwaukee area (0.1%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Standard deduction phaseout**: WI phases out the standard deduction at higher incomes, creating an effective marginal rate higher than the statutory rate in the phaseout range
2. **Itemized deduction credit (not deduction)**: WI converts certain federal itemized deductions into a 5% non-refundable credit. This is LESS valuable than a direct deduction for most filers
3. **Manufacturing and agriculture credit**: Effectively reduces tax on qualifying production income to near zero. If client has qualifying manufacturing/ag income, this is extremely valuable
4. **7-month extension**: WI grants a 7-month extension (through November 15), longer than the typical 6 months
5. **Alternative minimum tax**: WI has its own AMT (6.5% rate); check for AMT liability if client has significant preference items

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from WI income tax
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable at regular rates; no special exclusion
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no specific WI exclusion)
- **WI Retirement System (WRS)**: Taxable at regular rates
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal)
- **Federal government pensions**: Taxable
- **Homestead credit**: Low-income retirees may qualify for significant property tax relief
