# Tennessee Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No (Hall Tax on interest/dividends fully repealed effective January 1, 2021)
- Top marginal rate: N/A (no personal income tax)
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Tennessee imposes no personal income tax of any kind.

**History**: The Hall Income Tax previously taxed interest and dividend income at rates up to 6%. It was phased out:
- 2016: 6%
- 2017: 5%
- 2018: 4%
- 2019: 3%
- 2020: 2%
- 2021: **0% (fully repealed)**

Tennessee now has ZERO personal income tax on wages, salaries, interest, dividends, or any other personal income.

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Not applicable in the traditional SALT workaround sense (no individual income tax)
- However, Tennessee's Franchise & Excise Tax applies to many business entities directly

## Local/City Income Taxes
- None. No Tennessee municipality levies an income tax

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Highest combined sales tax in the nation**: Tennessee's combined state + local sales tax rates are among the highest (state: 7%, local: up to 2.75%, combined up to 9.75%)
2. **Reduced sales tax on food**: Grocery food is taxed at 4% state rate (plus local)
3. **No estate tax**: Repealed in 2016; Tennessee has no estate or inheritance tax
4. **Franchise & Excise Tax on businesses**: Even though there is no personal income tax, businesses face the F&E tax (see below)
5. **Professional privilege tax**: $400 annual tax on certain professionals (lawyers, doctors, accountants, architects, etc.); this is NOT an income tax but a flat license fee

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Military retirement**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- Tennessee is very favorable for retirees

## Business Tax Considerations
### Franchise & Excise Tax
- **Excise Tax**: 6.5% on net earnings (income attributable to Tennessee)
- **Franchise Tax**: 0.25% on the greater of net worth or real/tangible property in TN (minimum $100)
- Applies to: Corporations, LLCs, LPs, and other entities; sole proprietorships and general partnerships are EXEMPT
- Filing: Due April 15 (calendar year filers)
- S corps and LLCs pass-through for federal but still owe TN F&E Tax at the entity level

### Business Tax (Local)
- Tennessee cities and counties can levy a gross receipts business tax
- Rates vary by classification (0.05% to 0.3%)
- Most businesses with TN locations must register

## Property Tax
- Property taxes exist and are moderate (effective rate ~0.6-0.7%)
- No state property tax; all property tax is local
- Tennessee has relatively low property taxes compared to national average
- With no income tax, property + sales tax are the primary revenue sources

## Multi-State Considerations
- Tennessee residents working remotely for out-of-state employers: no TN tax on wages
- Non-residents with TN-source business income: subject to F&E Tax if operating through an entity
- Popular relocation destination for residents of high-tax states
