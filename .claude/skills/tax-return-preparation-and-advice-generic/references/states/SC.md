# South Carolina Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 6.0% (2025 top rate after recent statutory reductions)
- Filing requirement threshold: Federal filing requirement applies

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### All Filing Statuses
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 0% | $0 - $3,460 |
| 3% | $3,461 - $17,330 |
| 6.0% | Over $17,330 |

Note: South Carolina collapsed its rate structure. For tax year 2025, SCDOR describes the system as 0% / 3% / 6.0%, after a 6.2% top rate for 2024 and 6.4% for 2023.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: South Carolina follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Dependent exemption: $4,930 per eligible dependent
- SC starts with federal taxable income, then applies SC-specific modifications

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Retirement income deduction**: Up to $3,000 deduction for qualifying retirement income (increasing for those under 65); those 65+ get up to $15,000 total deduction ($30,000 MFJ with both 65+)
2. **Military retirement exemption**: 100% of military retirement pay is exempt
3. **Two-earner deduction**: Lesser of $2,120 or qualifying amount for MFJ with both spouses earning
4. **Child and Dependent Care Credit**: 7% of qualifying expenses
5. **SC Earned Income Credit**: 41.67% of federal EITC (refundable; one of the most generous state EITCs)
6. **Tuition tax credit**: Credit for contributions to exceptional needs children's educational fund
7. **529 Plan deduction**: Full deduction for contributions to SC Future Scholar 529 plan (no cap)
8. **Property tax credit**: For owner-occupied residences (4% assessment ratio)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: SC1040 (individual return), various schedules
- Due date: April 15 generally; for 2025 individual returns, SCDOR extended the filing deadline to October 15, 2026
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension with federal extension; pay any tax due by the original due date unless a special administrative extension applies
- SC starts with federal taxable income
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $100
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: SC1040ES
- Note: $100 threshold is very low compared to other states

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (SC PTET effective 2021+)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's timely filed return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays SC income tax on distributive shares at applicable rates; members receive credit on individual returns
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in South Carolina
- Local revenue comes primarily from property taxes and local option sales taxes
- Combined state + local sales tax: 6% - 9%

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Generous retirement income treatment**: SC is one of the most retirement-friendly states with the combination of SS exemption, $15,000 deduction for 65+, and military retirement exemption
2. **Military-friendly**: Full exemption of military retirement pay with no income cap
3. **529 unlimited deduction**: SC allows a full deduction for 529 contributions with no annual cap (one of the most generous nationally)
4. **Low estimated tax threshold**: Only $100 triggers estimated tax requirements; easy to miss
5. **Rate reduction schedule**: South Carolina is still on a multi-year rate-reduction path, but the 2025 top rate is 6.0% and should not be confused with the 2024 or 2023 tables

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from SC income tax
- **Age 65+ deduction**: Up to $15,000 per person ($30,000 if both spouses 65+) for retirement income; this is reduced by any Social Security exclusion claimed
- **Under-65 deduction**: Up to $3,000 per person for qualifying retirement income
- **Military retirement**: 100% exempt (no age or income requirement)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Subject to tax but eligible for the age-based deductions above
- **South Carolina Retirement System**: Eligible for retirement income deductions
