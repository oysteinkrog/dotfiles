# Connecticut Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 6.99% (plus up to 3% surcharge on CT AGI above certain thresholds)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeding $12,000 (Single) or filing status thresholds
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Connecticut Department of Revenue Services (DRS)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
| CT Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $10,000 | 3% |
| $10,001 - $50,000 | 5% |
| $50,001 - $100,000 | 5.5% |
| $100,001 - $200,000 | 6% |
| $200,001 - $250,000 | 6.5% |
| $250,001 - $500,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $500,000 | 6.99% |

### Married Filing Jointly
| CT Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $20,000 | 3% |
| $20,001 - $100,000 | 5% |
| $100,001 - $200,000 | 5.5% |
| $200,001 - $400,000 | 6% |
| $400,001 - $500,000 | 6.5% |
| $500,001 - $1,000,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $1,000,000 | 6.99% |

### Recapture Tax / Surcharge
Connecticut imposes a **tax recapture** (effectively a surcharge) that phases out the benefit of lower brackets for high earners:
- Single: CT AGI over $200,000 — recapture adds up to 3% on amounts above threshold
- MFJ: CT AGI over $400,000 — similar recapture
- This can result in an **effective marginal rate exceeding 6.99%** in the phaseout range

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Connecticut does not use a traditional standard deduction. Instead:
- **Personal exemption**: $15,000 (Single), $24,000 (MFJ) — phases out for high earners
- Phaseout: Exemption reduced by $1,000 for each $1,000 (or fraction) of CT AGI above $30,000 (Single) / $48,000 (MFJ), fully phased out at $45,000 / $72,000
- No separate standard deduction; Connecticut starts with federal AGI and applies its own modifications

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Property tax credit** — Up to $300 credit for property taxes paid on primary residence (income-limited; phases out above $103,500 Single / $130,500 MFJ)
2. **CT EITC** — 30.5% of federal EITC (refundable)
3. **Child tax rebate** — Periodic rebates enacted legislatively (not permanent; check current year)
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $5,000 ($10,000 MFJ) for CHET (Connecticut Higher Education Trust) contributions
5. **Angel investor tax credit** — 25% credit for investments in qualifying CT businesses
6. **Pension/annuity subtraction** — Percentage-based subtraction for pension and annuity income, phasing in over time (42% for 2025)
7. **Social Security exemption** — Full or partial exemption based on AGI thresholds
8. **Renewable energy credit** — Various credits for solar and energy improvements

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: CT-1040 (resident), CT-1040NR/PY (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 (must pay estimated tax due by April 15)
- E-file options: DRS myconneCT portal, approved commercial software
- Mandatory e-file for preparers filing 10+ returns

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior year CT AGI exceeds $150,000 / $300,000 MFJ)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Connecticut was one of the first states to enact PTET, effective 2018)
- Election deadline: Annual election required; made when the entity files its PE Tax return
- Rate: 6.99% on members' share of CT-source income
- Mechanics: Mandatory filing for affected entities (partnerships, S corps with CT-source income). Entity pays 6.99% at entity level. Members receive a credit against their CT individual income tax. This was designed specifically as a SALT cap workaround.
- **Note**: Connecticut's PTET is mandatory for applicable entities, unlike most states where it is elective

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Connecticut does not have local/city income taxes
- However, Connecticut has very high **property taxes** (among the highest in the nation)
- Average effective property tax rate: approximately 2.0%
- No local sales taxes (state rate is 6.35%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Recapture/surcharge creates surprise marginal rates** — The phaseout of lower-bracket benefits can push effective marginal rates well above the stated 6.99% top rate in certain income ranges
2. **PTET is essentially mandatory** — Unlike most states, Connecticut requires affected PTEs to pay the entity-level tax; it's not purely optional
3. **Personal exemption phaseout is aggressive** — The exemption phases out completely at relatively low income levels ($45,000 Single)
4. **Property tax credit has tight income limits** — Most middle-to-upper income filers do not qualify for the property tax credit
5. **Gift tax** — Connecticut is one of the few states with its own gift and estate tax (estate tax exemption approximately $13.99M, matching federal)
6. **Conveyance tax on real estate** — Additional real estate conveyance tax on sales over $800,000

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Exempt for CT AGI below $75,000 (Single) / $100,000 (MFJ); partially taxable above those thresholds; fully taxable at highest incomes
- **Pensions/annuities**: Partial subtraction — 42% of pension/annuity income can be subtracted (2025); percentage increasing annually toward 100%
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Qualify for pension subtraction above
- **Military pensions**: 100% exempt from Connecticut income tax
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
