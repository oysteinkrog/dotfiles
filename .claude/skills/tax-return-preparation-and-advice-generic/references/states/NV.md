# Nevada Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No
- Top marginal rate: N/A (no personal income tax)
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Nevada imposes no personal income tax. The Nevada Constitution prohibits a state income tax on individuals.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- N/A (no income tax)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
- N/A for individual income tax purposes
- **Commerce Tax**: Applies to businesses with Nevada gross revenue exceeding $4,000,000 at rates varying by industry (0.051% to 0.331%)
- **Modified Business Tax (MBT)**: Payroll tax on employers; 1.378% on wages over $50,000 per quarter (general), 2% for financial institutions
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax

## Filing Requirements
- No individual income tax return required
- Businesses with >$4M gross revenue must file Commerce Tax return (Form CTC)
- Employers must file Modified Business Tax returns quarterly
- Due date for Commerce Tax: 45 days after close of tax year

## Estimated Tax Payments
- N/A for individual income tax
- Commerce Tax is paid annually
- MBT is paid quarterly

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Not applicable (no individual income tax to work around)

## Local/City Income Taxes
- None. No city or county in Nevada levies an income tax
- Revenue is generated through sales tax, gaming tax, and property tax

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No income tax but high sales tax**: Combined state and local sales tax ranges from 6.85% to 8.375% depending on county
2. **Commerce Tax complexity**: Businesses operating in multiple industries must allocate revenue by NAICS code; rates vary significantly by sector
3. **Gaming and entertainment taxes**: Specific excise taxes on gaming operations (up to 6.75% on gross gaming revenue)
4. **Live entertainment tax**: 9% on admission to live entertainment in venues with 7,500+ capacity; lower rates for smaller venues
5. **Constitutional prohibition**: Nevada's constitution explicitly bars the state from enacting a personal income tax

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Military retirement**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- Nevada is a highly favorable state for retirees due to zero income tax on any income source

## Business Tax Considerations
- **Commerce Tax**: Gross revenue > $4M; rates by industry category
  - Most retail: 0.111%
  - Mining: 0.051%
  - Rail transportation: 0.331%
  - Real estate: 0.257%
- **Modified Business Tax**: Quarterly payroll tax; $50,000/quarter exemption for general businesses
- **No corporate income tax**: Nevada has no corporate income tax
- **Business license fee**: Annual state business license fee of $500 (most entities)

## Property Tax
- Property taxes exist but are relatively moderate
- Tax caps: 3% annual increase for owner-occupied, 8% for other property
- No estate tax or inheritance tax
- Nevada repealed its estate tax (was tied to federal credit, which was eliminated)

## Multi-State Considerations
- Residents of Nevada working remotely for out-of-state employers may owe tax to the employer's state depending on that state's rules
- Nevada has no reciprocity agreements (not needed since no income tax)
- Popular relocation destination for California and other high-tax-state residents
