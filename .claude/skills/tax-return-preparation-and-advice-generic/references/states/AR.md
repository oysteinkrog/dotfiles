# Arkansas Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.4% (reduced from 4.7% for 2025)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeding Arkansas standard deduction for filing status
- Fiscal year: Calendar year

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses (Same Brackets)
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $4,400 | 0% (effectively exempt) |
| $4,401 - $8,800 | 2.0% |
| $8,801 - $13,100 | 3.0% |
| $13,101 - $22,200 | 3.4% |
| $22,201 - $38,500 | 4.0% |
| Over $38,500 | 4.4% |

Note: Arkansas has been steadily reducing rates. The top rate dropped from 5.5% (2021) to 4.4% (2025). Further reductions to 3.9% are planned through revenue triggers.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $2,340 standard deduction; $29 personal tax credit
- MFJ: $4,680 standard deduction; $58 personal tax credit
- Head of Household: $2,340 standard deduction; $29 personal tax credit
- Dependent tax credit: $29 per dependent
- Low-income tax tables provide additional relief for filers with income under $26,200

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Federal income tax deduction (partial)** — Arkansas allows a deduction for federal income taxes paid, capped at $10,000 for individuals
2. **Retirement income exclusion** — $6,000 exclusion for qualified retirement income (per person)
3. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $5,000 ($10,000 MFJ) for contributions to GIFT Plan of Arkansas or iShares 529 Plan
4. **Child care credit** — 20% of the federal child and dependent care credit
5. **Earned income tax credit** — 20% of the federal EITC (nonrefundable)
6. **Military pension exclusion** — First $6,000 of military retirement pay is exempt
7. **Property tax credit (homestead)** — Up to $375 credit for qualifying homeowners
8. **Political contribution credit** — $50 ($100 MFJ) for political contributions
9. **Adoption expenses credit** — Arkansas offers its own adoption credit

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: AR1000F (full year resident), AR1000NR (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension if federal extension is filed; otherwise file AR1055
- E-file options: ATAP (Arkansas Taxpayer Access Point) portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year liability or 100% of prior year liability

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Act 362 of 2023)
- Election deadline: Annual election made on or before the due date of the entity's return
- Rate: Same as individual income tax rates (progressive, up to 4.4%)
- Mechanics: PTE pays tax at entity level on each member's share of income; members receive a credit on their individual Arkansas returns. Effective SALT cap workaround.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Arkansas does not have local/city income taxes
- Municipalities rely on local sales taxes (typically 1-3% on top of the 6.5% state sales tax)
- Total combined sales tax can reach 11.5%+ in some jurisdictions

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Federal tax deduction has a cap** — The $10,000 cap on federal tax deductibility limits the benefit for high earners, unlike Alabama which has no cap
2. **Low-income tax tables** — Filers with net income under $26,200 use special low-income tax tables that provide lower effective rates; crossing this threshold can create a cliff
3. **Steadily declining rates** — Arkansas has committed to rate reductions via revenue triggers; always verify the current year's rate
4. **Same brackets for all filing statuses** — Unlike most states, Arkansas uses the same bracket thresholds regardless of filing status
5. **Capital gains** — Taxed as ordinary income, but qualified capital gains on assets held 12+ months get a 50% exclusion (on Arkansas-sourced gains)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Arkansas income tax
- **Public pensions (state/local)**: $6,000 per person exclusion
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: $6,000 per person exclusion
- **Military pensions**: $6,000 per person exclusion
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- The $6,000 exclusion applies to total retirement income combined, not per source
