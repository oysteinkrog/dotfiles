# Pennsylvania Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 3.07% (flat rate)
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if PA-source income exceeds $1 (extremely low threshold)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Pennsylvania has a **flat income tax rate**:
- **3.07%** on all taxable income
- This rate has been unchanged for many years
- One of the lowest flat rates among states with income tax

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Pennsylvania does NOT have a standard deduction
- Pennsylvania does NOT have personal exemptions
- PA uses its own income classification system (8 classes of income)
- **No deduction for retirement plan contributions** (401k, IRA, etc.)
- Each class of income is computed separately; losses in one class generally cannot offset income in another

## Eight Classes of PA Income
1. Compensation (wages, salaries, tips)
2. Interest
3. Dividends
4. Net gains from disposition of property
5. Net income from business/profession
6. Net income from rents, royalties, patents
7. Estate/trust income
8. Gambling/lottery winnings

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Tax Forgiveness credit**: Income-based credit that effectively eliminates tax for very low-income filers (eligibility income up to ~$35,500 for family of 4)
2. **Resident credit for taxes paid to other states**: Dollar-for-dollar credit (with limitations)
3. **Employment Incentive Payment credit**: For qualifying employers
4. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $17,000 per beneficiary per year ($34,000 MFJ) — PA is unusually generous
5. **Opportunity Scholarship Tax Credit**: For contributions to qualifying organizations
6. **No deduction for contributions to retirement plans**: 401(k), IRA, and similar contributions are taxable when made (but distributions of previously taxed contributions are NOT taxed again)
7. **Unreimbursed employee business expenses**: Deductible against compensation income

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: PA-40 (all filers), plus various schedules by income class
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15); must pay estimated tax
- PA does NOT start with federal AGI — it uses its own income calculation system
- Separate schedules required for each income class with income

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $8,000
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: PA-40 ES
- Note: The $8,000 threshold is unusually high compared to other states

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (PA Act 53 of 2022, effective 2023+)
- Election deadline: Made annually by the entity
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays PA income tax at 3.07% on PA-source income; members receive credit
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs taxed as partnerships

## Local/City Income Taxes
### Earned Income Tax (EIT)
- **Most PA municipalities levy a local earned income tax** (over 2,500 taxing jurisdictions)
- Rates typically range from 0.5% to 3.1%
- Applies to earned income (wages + net business profits)
- Employers withhold for work location; residents may owe additional to resident municipality
- Credit: Generally credit for tax withheld at work location against resident location tax

### Philadelphia Wage Tax
- **Residents: 3.75%** (2025, declining slightly each year)
- **Non-residents working in Philadelphia: 3.44%**
- This is IN ADDITION to the PA state 3.07% flat tax
- Philadelphia also levies Business Income & Receipts Tax (BIRT) and Net Profits Tax (NPT)

### Pittsburgh
- Residents: 3% earned income tax
- Includes 1% municipal, 1% school district, 1% other

### School District Taxes
- Many school districts levy additional earned income or property taxes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No deduction for retirement contributions**: PA taxes 401(k)/IRA/403(b) contributions when made. This means distributions are partially tax-free (return of basis). Track basis carefully
2. **Eight classes of income**: Losses in one class cannot offset gains in another. A business loss cannot reduce wage income
3. **Local tax complexity**: With 2,500+ taxing jurisdictions, local compliance is extremely burdensome. Use Keystone Collections or Berkheimer to determine rates
4. **Philadelphia combined rate**: Residents face 3.07% state + 3.75% city = 6.82% combined on earned income, plus potential BIRT/NPT for business owners
5. **Inheritance tax**: PA has an inheritance tax (not income tax): 0% for spouse, 4.5% for lineal descendants, 12% for siblings, 15% for others

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Distributions of previously-taxed contributions are exempt; only the earnings portion is taxable. Since PA taxes contributions when made, much of the distribution may be tax-free
- **Military retirement**: Fully exempt
- **PA state pensions**: Exempt after meeting age/service requirements
- **Railroad Retirement**: Fully exempt (both tiers)
- **Key**: Because PA does not allow deductions for retirement contributions, the basis tracking for distributions is critical and different from federal
