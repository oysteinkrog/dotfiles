# Kentucky Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.0% (flat rate since 2018)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds Kentucky's current standard deduction or modified gross income filing threshold; verify the annually indexed figure from current instructions
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Kentucky Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All Kentucky taxable income | 4.0% |

Kentucky adopted a flat 4.0% rate effective 2024 (reduced from 4.5% in 2023). Further reductions to 3.5% or lower are planned via revenue triggers under HB 8 (2022).

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction is indexed annually; use the current Form 740 instructions instead of carrying forward prior-year figures
- Personal tax credit: $46.90 per person (flat credit, not deduction)
- Dependent credit: $46.90 per dependent
- Kentucky starts with federal AGI and applies its own standard deduction

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Pension exclusion** — Up to $31,110 per person for state/local/federal retirement plan distributions (all types combined)
2. **Family size tax credit** — 100% credit for filers with modified gross income at or below federal poverty level; phases out above
3. **Child and dependent care credit** — 20% of federal credit (nonrefundable)
4. **Education tuition credit** — Kentucky provides a credit for higher education tuition
5. **529 Plan** — Kentucky does not offer a state deduction for 529 contributions
6. **Recycling/composting equipment credit** — For qualifying investments in recycling equipment
7. **Historic preservation credit** — Kentucky offers rehabilitation tax credits
8. **Small business/angel investor credit** — For qualifying investments in Kentucky small businesses
9. **Military pension exclusion** — Fully included in the $31,110 pension exclusion

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 740 (resident full year), Form 740-NP (nonresident/part-year), Form 740-EZ (simplified)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form 740EXT
- E-file options: Kentucky ePay portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 70% of current year tax (Kentucky's current-year safe harbor is lower than many states)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, HB 360)
- Election deadline: Made on or before the 15th day of the 4th month of the tax year
- Rate: 4.0% (same as individual flat rate)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay 4.0% at entity level on electing members' share of Kentucky income. Members receive a nonrefundable credit on Form 740. Excess credit can be carried forward.

## Local/City Income Taxes
Kentucky has significant local income taxes:
- **Louisville/Jefferson County**: Occupational license tax of 2.2% on all wages/net profits earned in Louisville Metro
- **Lexington/Fayette County**: Occupational license tax of 2.5% on wages/net profits
- **Covington**: 2.5% occupational tax
- **Bowling Green**: 1.85% occupational tax
- **Most Kentucky cities and counties** levy occupational taxes ranging from 0.5% to 2.5%
- These are **in addition to** the 4.0% state rate
- School boards may also levy occupational taxes (typically 0.5-1.0%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Local taxes are substantial** — A Louisville resident pays 4.0% state + 2.2% local = 6.2% total. A Lexington resident pays 4.0% + 2.5% = 6.5%. The flat 4.0% state rate understates the true burden.
2. **Pension exclusion is generous but has a cap** — The $31,110 per person exclusion covers all retirement plan distributions combined (state, federal, private, IRA, 401k) — not per source
3. **Occupational taxes apply to net self-employment income** — Self-employed individuals must file local occupational tax returns and pay on net profits
4. **Revenue trigger rate reductions** — The 4.0% rate is scheduled to decrease further if state revenue targets are met; verify the current year rate
5. **No 529 deduction** — Kentucky is one of the states that offers no state tax benefit for 529 plan contributions

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Kentucky income tax
- **State/local government pensions (KERS, CERS, TRS)**: Exempt up to $31,110 per person
- **Federal pensions (CSRS/FERS)**: Exempt up to $31,110 per person
- **Military pensions**: Exempt up to $31,110 per person
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Exempt up to $31,110 per person
- **Total exclusion is $31,110 per person across ALL retirement sources combined**
- **Kentucky is retirement-friendly** — The generous exclusion, Social Security exemption, and low flat rate benefit retirees
