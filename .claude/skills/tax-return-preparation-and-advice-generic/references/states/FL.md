# Florida Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No
- Top marginal rate: N/A (no state income tax — constitutionally prohibited)
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A
- Fiscal year: N/A

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Florida does not impose a state individual income tax. Article VII, Section 5 of the Florida Constitution prohibits a state personal income tax.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Not applicable — no state income tax.

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
Not applicable for income tax purposes. Notable state tax features:
1. **Homestead exemption** — Up to $50,000 property tax exemption on primary residence ($25,000 on first portion + $25,000 on assessed value $50K-$75K). Must file with county property appraiser.
2. **Save Our Homes cap** — Assessed value of homesteaded property cannot increase more than 3% or CPI per year, whichever is less
3. **No estate tax** — Florida does not impose a state estate or inheritance tax
4. **Intangibles tax** — Repealed in 2007; no longer applies
5. **No gift tax** — Florida has no state gift tax
6. **Senior homestead exemption** — Additional $50,000 exemption for qualifying seniors (65+, income limit approximately $36,614 (indexed; verify current year), 25+ year resident)
7. **Veteran exemptions** — Disabled veterans may receive full property tax exemption

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: No (no individual income tax to bypass)
- Florida does impose a corporate income tax (5.5%) on C-corporations, but S-corporations and other pass-through entities are generally not subject to Florida corporate income tax unless they have C-corp characteristics

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No city or county in Florida levies a local income tax
- Local governments rely on property taxes, tourist development taxes, and local option sales taxes
- Local sales tax (discretionary surtax) ranges from 0% to 2.5% depending on county, added to the 6% state sales tax

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Constitutional prohibition** — Florida's constitution explicitly bans a personal income tax, providing strong long-term certainty
2. **Homestead exemption requires filing** — Must affirmatively file with the county property appraiser by March 1; not automatic
3. **Tangible personal property tax** — Businesses must file DR-405 annually for tangible personal property (equipment, furniture); first $25,000 is exempt
4. **Documentary stamp tax** — Tax on real estate transfers ($0.70 per $100 of consideration, except Miami-Dade at $0.60) and on notes/mortgages
5. **No tax reciprocity needed** — Since Florida has no income tax, residents working in other states may still owe income tax to those states with no Florida credit to offset
6. **Popular for domicile changes** — Many taxpayers establish Florida residency to avoid state income tax; other states (NY, CA, CT) aggressively audit such moves

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions**: Not taxed
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Not taxed
- **All retirement income**: Only subject to federal taxation
- **Florida is one of the most retirement-friendly states** — No income tax, no estate tax, and significant property tax exemptions for seniors

## Florida Corporate Income Tax (Reference)
While not an individual tax, practitioners should be aware:
- Rate: 5.5% on adjusted federal income apportioned to Florida. Note: temporarily reduced to 3.535% through 2026 under SB 7074 (2023)
- Filing threshold: Not required if taxable income is $0 or less after $50,000 exemption
- S-corporations: Generally exempt from Florida corporate income tax
- Partnerships/LLCs: Not subject to corporate income tax (flow through to individual members)
- Form F-1120 is the corporate income tax return
