# Alabama Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5%
- Filing requirement threshold: $4,000 (Single), $7,700 (MFJ) or gross income exceeds Alabama personal exemption + standard deduction
- Fiscal year: Calendar year

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $500 | 2% |
| $501 - $3,000 | 4% |
| Over $3,000 | 5% |

### Married Filing Jointly
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $1,000 | 2% |
| $1,001 - $6,000 | 4% |
| Over $6,000 | 5% |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $2,500 standard deduction; $1,500 personal exemption
- MFJ: $7,500 standard deduction; $3,000 personal exemption
- Head of Household: $4,700 standard deduction; $3,000 personal exemption
- Dependent exemption: $1,000 per dependent (under 19 or full-time student under 24)
- Standard deduction phases out for higher-income filers (AGI above $20,499 Single / $30,499 MFJ)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Federal income tax deduction** — Alabama allows a full deduction for federal income taxes paid (one of few states)
2. **Property tax deduction** — All ad valorem taxes paid are deductible
3. **Retirement income deduction** — State/local/federal pensions and Social Security fully exempt
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $5,000 per taxpayer ($10,000 MFJ) for CollegeCounts contributions
5. **Child/Dependent credit** — $1,000 per qualifying child (income-limited)
6. **Itemized deductions** — Alabama allows itemized deductions similar to federal, including charitable contributions
7. **Military pay** — Active-duty pay for full-time service outside Alabama is exempt

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 40 (resident), Form 40NR (nonresident), Form 40A (short form)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form 4868A
- E-file options: My Alabama Taxes (MAT) portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2021, Act 2021-1)
- Election deadline: Due with the entity's return (by the 15th day of the 3rd month after tax year end)
- Rate: 5% (same as top individual rate)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level; members receive a credit on their Alabama return

## Local/City Income Taxes
- **Occupational taxes**: Many Alabama cities and counties levy occupational taxes (typically 1-2%)
- Notable: Birmingham (1%), Bessemer (1%), Gadsden (2%), Prichard (2%), Macon County (1%)
- These are employer-withheld in most jurisdictions
- Some municipalities also levy a license/privilege tax on businesses

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Federal tax deduction is powerful** — Because Alabama allows deducting federal income tax paid, effective rates are significantly lower than nominal rates; coordinate estimated payments across both returns
2. **Low bracket thresholds** — The top 5% rate kicks in at just $3,000 (Single) / $6,000 (MFJ), so almost all taxable income is taxed at 5%
3. **Standard deduction phaseout** — Higher earners lose their standard deduction entirely, pushing them to itemize
4. **Occupational taxes are separate** — City/county occupational taxes are filed and paid separately from the state return
5. **No capital gains preference** — Alabama taxes capital gains as ordinary income at full rates

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Alabama income tax
- **Federal pensions**: Fully exempt (including Civil Service, military)
- **State/local pensions**: Fully exempt (Alabama Teacher Retirement, state employee pensions)
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable as ordinary income (no special exclusion)
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
