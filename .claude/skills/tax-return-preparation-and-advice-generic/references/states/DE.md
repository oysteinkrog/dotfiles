# Delaware Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 6.6%
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds $9,400 (Single under 60) or filing status-specific thresholds
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Delaware Division of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses (Same Brackets)
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $2,000 | 0% |
| $2,001 - $5,000 | 2.2% |
| $5,001 - $10,000 | 3.9% |
| $10,001 - $20,000 | 4.8% |
| $20,001 - $25,000 | 5.2% |
| $25,001 - $60,000 | 5.55% |
| Over $60,000 | 6.6% |

Note: Delaware uses the same brackets for all filing statuses.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $3,250 standard deduction
- MFJ: $6,500 standard deduction
- Head of Household: $3,250 standard deduction
- Personal credit: $110 per person ($220 MFJ)
- Dependent credit: $110 per dependent
- Over 60 additional credit: $110 per qualifying person
- Itemized deductions: Delaware allows itemized deductions; generally follows federal rules with some modifications

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **No sales tax** — Delaware has no state or local sales tax, reducing overall tax burden
2. **Charitable contributions** — Deductible if itemizing (follows federal rules)
3. **529 Plan deduction** — No state deduction for 529 contributions (Delaware does not offer this)
4. **Real property tax deduction** — Deductible when itemizing
5. **Volunteer firefighter credit** — $400 credit for active volunteer firefighters
6. **Child care credit** — 50% of the federal child and dependent care credit
7. **Earned income tax credit** — Delaware does not have a state EITC
8. **Land and historic preservation credit** — Up to $50,000 for qualifying conservation easements
9. **Pension exclusion** — Up to $12,500 per person under 60; $12,500 for 60+

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 200-01 (resident), Form 200-02 (nonresident), Form 200-03 (part-year)
- Due date: April 30 (NOTE: Delaware deadline is April 30, not April 15)
- Extension: Automatic 5.5-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed
- E-file options: Delaware Division of Revenue portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $400 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 30, June 15, September 15, January 15 (note first quarter aligns with DE filing deadline)
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: No — Delaware has not enacted a PTET election as of 2025
- Delaware does impose a composite return requirement for nonresident PTE members in some situations

## Local/City Income Taxes
- **Wilmington** — Earned income tax of 1.25% on wages earned in the city (Wilmington Earned Income Tax)
- Wilmington residents pay 1.25% on all earned income regardless of where earned
- Non-residents working in Wilmington pay 1.25% on Wilmington-source earned income
- **No other Delaware city** imposes a local income tax
- School districts do not levy income taxes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **April 30 filing deadline** — Delaware's income tax deadline is April 30, NOT April 15. Easy to confuse with the federal deadline.
2. **No sales tax is a major benefit** — Delaware is one of five states with no sales tax, making it attractive for businesses and shoppers
3. **Same brackets for all filing statuses** — No marriage benefit; MFJ filers use the same brackets as Single filers
4. **Wilmington earned income tax** — Easily overlooked; employees working in Wilmington must file and pay separately
5. **Low bracket thresholds** — Top rate of 6.6% kicks in at just $60,000, meaning most middle-income filers pay the top rate
6. **Corporate franchise tax** — Delaware is famous for corporate incorporation; franchise taxes are a major state revenue source but do not affect individual returns directly

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Delaware income tax
- **Pensions**: Exclusion of up to $12,500 per person for those 60 and older
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Qualify for the $12,500 exclusion (age 60+)
- **Under 60**: $2,000 exclusion on eligible retirement income
- **Military pensions**: Qualify for the pension exclusion amounts above
- **Delaware is moderately retirement-friendly** — Social Security exemption and pension exclusion help, but the relatively high top rate on other income is a consideration
