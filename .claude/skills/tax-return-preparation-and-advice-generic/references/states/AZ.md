# Arizona Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 2.5% (flat rate as of 2023)
- Filing requirement threshold: $15,750 (Single/MFS), $31,500 (MFJ), $23,625 (HOH) — mirrors the 2025 federal standard deduction
- Fiscal year: Calendar year

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All taxable income | 2.5% |

Arizona transitioned to a flat 2.5% rate effective 2023 (previously had progressive brackets up to 4.5%). The flat rate applies to all filers regardless of income level.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Arizona conforms to federal standard deduction amounts (indexed annually):
- Single: $15,750
- MFJ: $31,500
- Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemptions: Arizona eliminated separate personal exemptions when conforming to TCJA; replaced with increased standard deduction
- Dependent exemption: $100 per dependent (Arizona-specific)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $2,000 per beneficiary ($4,000 MFJ) for contributions to any state's 529 plan
2. **Private school tuition credit** — Dollar-for-dollar credit up to $769 (Single/HOH) / $1,535 (MFJ) for donations to School Tuition Organizations (STOs) for private school scholarships
3. **Public school tax credit** — Up to $200 (Single) / $400 (MFJ) for fees paid to public schools for extracurricular activities
4. **Charitable tax credit** — Up to $495 (Single/MFS/HOH) / $987 (MFJ) for qualifying charitable organizations, with a separate foster care credit bucket
5. **Military pension subtraction** — Up to $3,500 subtraction for military retirement pay
6. **Property tax credit** — Up to $502 for qualifying elderly/disabled residents
7. **Increased excise tax credit** — $100 per household member for low-income filers (AGI under $12,500 single / $25,000 MFJ)
8. **Solar energy credit** — 25% of cost, up to $1,000, for solar or wind energy devices installed on residence
9. **Premium tax credit (state)** — For qualifying small employers providing health insurance

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 140 (resident full), Form 140A (short), Form 140EZ (simplest), Form 140NR (nonresident), Form 140PY (part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (no form needed if federal extension filed); otherwise file Form 204
- E-file options: AZTaxes.gov (free for qualifying filers), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, SB 1783)
- Election deadline: Made on timely filed PTE return
- Rate: 2.5% (same as individual flat rate)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level; members claim a dollar-for-dollar credit against their Arizona individual income tax. Useful for SALT deduction cap workaround.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Arizona cities do not impose a local income tax
- However, many cities levy a **Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)** — a sales/use tax on businesses, typically 1.5-2.5% on top of the 5.6% state TPT
- Phoenix: 2.3% city TPT; Tucson: 2.6%; Scottsdale: 1.75%; Mesa: 2.0%
- The TPT is on the seller, not the buyer, though it is typically passed through

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **School tuition credit is dollar-for-dollar** — Donating to STOs for private school scholarships gives a full tax credit (not deduction). One of the most generous in the US.
2. **Flat rate simplicity** — The 2.5% flat rate makes Arizona one of the lowest-tax income tax states in the country
3. **Arizona does not conform to all federal provisions** — Check conformity date; Arizona often has a static conformity to the IRC as of a specific date
4. **No deduction for federal taxes paid** — Unlike Alabama/Louisiana, Arizona does not allow a deduction for federal income taxes
5. **Community property state** — Arizona is a community property state, which affects how income is split between spouses on separate returns

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Arizona income tax
- **Federal/state/local pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income (no general exclusion)
- **Military pensions**: Subtraction of up to $3,500
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Taxable as ordinary income
- **Arizona is moderately retirement-friendly** due to the low 2.5% flat rate and Social Security exemption
