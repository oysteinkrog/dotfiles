# Oregon Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 9.9%
- Filing requirement threshold: Varies by filing status and age; generally must file if gross income exceeds threshold

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 4.75% | $0 - $4,050 |
| 6.75% | $4,051 - $10,200 |
| 8.75% | $10,201 - $125,000 |
| 9.9% | Over $125,000 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 4.75% | $0 - $8,100 |
| 6.75% | $8,101 - $20,400 |
| 8.75% | $20,401 - $250,000 |
| 9.9% | Over $250,000 |

### Head of Household
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 4.75% | $0 - $4,050 |
| 6.75% | $4,051 - $10,200 |
| 8.75% | $10,201 - $125,000 |
| 9.9% | Over $125,000 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $2,835
  - MFJ: $5,670
  - HOH: $4,560
  - Note: Oregon's standard deduction is MUCH lower than federal
- Personal exemption credit: $236 per exemption (phased out at higher incomes)
- Oregon does NOT follow federal standard deduction amounts

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Kicker refund credit**: When state revenue exceeds forecast by 2%+, taxpayers get a "kicker" refund as a credit on next year's return (unique to Oregon)
2. **Federal tax subtraction**: Oregon allows subtraction of federal income tax paid (up to $7,050 Single / $14,100 MFJ for 2025)
3. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $300 per beneficiary ($600 MFJ) for Oregon 529 contributions; contributions over this can be carried forward
4. **Oregon EITC**: 12% of federal EITC (refundable); 15% for filers with child under 3
5. **Working Family Household and Dependent Care Credit**: Refundable, more generous than federal
6. **Political contribution credit**: Up to $50 ($100 MFJ)
7. **Retirement income credit**: Up to 9% of qualifying retirement income for lower-income elderly
8. **Portland Arts Tax**: $35/year per resident of Portland (separately administered; age 18+ with income >$1,000)
9. **Residential energy credit**: For qualifying energy-efficient improvements

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form OR-40 (resident), Form OR-40-N (nonresident), Form OR-40-P (part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15); must pay estimated tax by April 15
- Oregon starts with federal AGI, then applies Oregon-specific modifications

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $1,000 (after credits and withholding)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: OR-40-V (payment voucher) or OR-40-ES
- Underpayment penalty: Calculated on Form OR-10

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Oregon PTE-E, effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made annually; entity must elect on a timely filed return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays tax at 9% on distributive income; members receive credit on individual returns
- The 9% flat rate is a compromise rate (between lower and higher brackets)
- Quarterly estimated payments required if tax will exceed $1,000

## Local/City Income Taxes
### Portland/Multnomah County
- **Portland Arts Tax**: $35/year for Portland residents age 18+ with income > $1,000 (administered separately; file Arts Tax return with City of Portland)
- **Multnomah County Preschool for All**: 1.5% on taxable income over $125,000 (Single) / $200,000 (MFJ); additional 1.5% on income over $250,000/$400,000
- **Metro Supportive Housing Services**: 1% on taxable income over $125,000 (Single) / $200,000 (MFJ)

### Lane County (Eugene area)
- Lane Transit District payroll tax on employers

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No sales tax**: Oregon has no general sales tax, making income tax the primary state revenue source — hence the high rates
2. **Kicker credit**: Unique to Oregon; when actual revenue exceeds forecasted revenue by 2%+, the surplus is returned as an income tax credit. Can be significant (2023 kicker was ~17% of 2022 tax liability)
3. **Federal tax subtraction**: Oregon is one of very few states allowing a deduction for federal income taxes paid (capped); this partially offsets the high rates
4. **Portland triple tax**: Portland residents face the Arts Tax + Metro SHS tax + Multnomah County PFA tax in addition to state tax — effective combined rates among the highest in the nation
5. **Oregon-specific standard deduction**: Much lower than federal ($2,835 vs $15,750 for Single); do NOT confuse with federal amounts

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Oregon income tax (Oregon starts with federal AGI which already excludes the non-taxable portion; Oregon subtracts any remaining federally taxable SS)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable; retirement income credit available for lower-income seniors
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no special exclusion)
- **Federal tax subtraction**: Retirees benefit from subtracting federal tax paid, which partially reduces effective rate on retirement income
- **Oregon PERS**: Taxable at regular rates
