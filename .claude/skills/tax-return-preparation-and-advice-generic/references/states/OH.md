# Ohio Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 3.5%
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if Ohio AGI exceeds $0 (effectively all filers with income)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Ohio uses a progressive rate structure with a $0 rate on the first bracket:

| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 0% | $0 - $26,050 |
| 2.75% | $26,051 - $46,100 |
| 3.5% | Over $46,100 |

Note: Ohio significantly simplified brackets in recent years (was 5+ brackets). The 0% bracket means low-to-moderate earners owe nothing.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Ohio does NOT use a standard deduction
- Personal exemption: $2,400 per exemption (taxpayer, spouse, dependents)
- Additional exemptions for age 65+ and blind
- Ohio starts with federal AGI, then applies Ohio-specific adjustments

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Business income deduction**: First $250,000 of business income is exempt from Ohio income tax (huge benefit for pass-through business owners)
2. **Joint filing credit**: 2-20% credit for lower-earning spouse (MFJ only, based on qualifying income ratio)
3. **Personal/dependent exemption credit**: $2,400 per exemption claimed
4. **Ohio Earned Income Credit**: Based on Ohio AGI (much smaller than federal EITC)
5. **Retirement income credit**: Up to $200 credit based on retirement income
6. **Senior citizen credit**: $50 credit for age 65+
7. **Lump sum retirement credit**: For qualifying lump sum distributions
8. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $4,000 per beneficiary for Ohio 529 contributions (unlimited carryforward)
9. **Disability/survivorship credit**: For qualifying disability income
10. **Political contribution credit**: Up to $50 ($100 MFJ)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IT 1040 (all individual filers), Schedule A (adjustments), Schedule of Credits
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Ohio grants automatic extension to October 15 with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- Ohio starts with federal AGI (not federal taxable income)
- School district returns filed separately (SD 100)

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 (after withholding and credits)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: IT 1040ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Ohio PTET effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity elects to pay tax at 3% on Ohio-source income; owners receive refundable credit
- The flat 3% PTET rate is different from individual rates
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs

## Local/City Income Taxes
### Municipal Income Taxes (CRITICAL)
- **Most Ohio cities and villages levy their own income tax** (over 600 municipalities)
- Rates range from 0.5% to 3% (most common: 1.5-2.5%)
- Major city rates:
  - Columbus: 2.5%
  - Cleveland: 2.5%
  - Cincinnati: 1.8%
  - Dayton: 2.25%
  - Toledo: 2.25%
  - Akron: 2.5%
- Credit for taxes paid to work city against resident city tax (varies)
- Municipal returns are filed SEPARATELY from state return

### School District Income Taxes
- ~200 school districts levy income tax (0.25% to 2%)
- Two types: **traditional** (based on Ohio taxable income) and **earned income** (wages/business income only)
- Filed on Form SD 100 with the state return
- MUST verify if client's school district levies a tax (use OH Dept of Tax lookup by address)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **$250K business income deduction**: The first $250,000 of qualifying business income is 100% exempt; business income above $250K is taxed at 3.5%. This is one of the most generous business deductions in any state
2. **Municipal tax complexity**: Clients may owe tax to both their resident city AND their work city, with partial credits. Each city has its own forms and filing requirements
3. **School district tax**: Easy to miss; use Ohio's address lookup tool to determine if applicable
4. **Ohio AGI starting point**: Ohio starts with federal AGI, NOT federal taxable income; Ohio adjustments differ from federal
5. **Commercial Activity Tax (CAT)**: Businesses with >$150,000 Ohio gross receipts owe CAT (0.26%); this is separate from income tax

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt (not included in Ohio AGI)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable at regular rates; retirement income credit of up to $200 available
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no specific exclusion beyond the $250K business income deduction if applicable)
- **Ohio public employee retirement (OPERS/STRS)**: Taxable
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt
- **Senior citizen credit**: Additional $50 credit for 65+
