# Rhode Island Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.99%
- Filing requirement threshold: Federal filing requirement applies; must file if RI-source income

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### All Filing Statuses (same brackets)
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 3.75% | $0 - $73,450 |
| 4.75% | $73,451 - $166,950 |
| 5.99% | Over $166,950 |

Note: Rhode Island uses the same bracket thresholds for all filing statuses (Single, MFJ, HOH).

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $10,550
  - MFJ: $21,150
  - HOH: $15,850
  - Note: RI standard deduction is lower than federal and phases out for higher incomes
- Personal exemption: $4,700 per exemption (phases out at higher AGI)
- Phaseout: Standard deduction and exemptions phase out starting at $241,850 AGI
- Rhode Island starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Property tax circuit breaker credit**: Refundable credit for renters and homeowners with household income under $30,000; covers property tax exceeding a percentage of income
2. **RI Earned Income Credit**: 15% of federal EITC (partially refundable)
3. **Child and dependent care credit**: 25% of federal credit (non-refundable)
4. **Residential lead abatement credit**: For qualifying lead paint removal
5. **Historic preservation credit**: For rehabilitation of historic structures
6. **RI Education credit**: For qualifying tuition expenses
7. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $500 per beneficiary ($1,000 MFJ) for CollegeBoundfund contributions
8. **Credit for taxes paid to other states**: For RI residents with income taxed by other states

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: RI-1040 (resident), RI-1040NR (nonresident)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- RI starts with federal AGI, applies modifications, then computes RI taxable income
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $250 (after withholding and credits)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or lesser of 90% current year / 110% prior year for high-income
- Form: RI-1040ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (effective 2019, expanded 2022)
- Election deadline: Made annually; entity must elect on timely filed return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays RI income tax on behalf of members at entity level; members receive credit on individual return
- Rate: Graduated rates up to 5.99% (matching individual rates)
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in Rhode Island
- Property taxes are the primary local revenue source (rates are relatively high)
- Statewide property tax levy does not exist; all property tax is local

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Standard deduction and exemption phaseout**: Both phase out at higher AGI ($241,850+), meaning high-income filers effectively lose their deductions
2. **Property tax circuit breaker**: One of the more generous circuit breaker programs; ensures low-income residents are not overburdened by property taxes (including renters who claim a portion of rent as property tax)
3. **Small state, simple system**: RI generally follows federal closely; fewer unique adjustments than many states
4. **Car tax**: Rhode Island's motor vehicle excise tax has been a significant local tax burden (being phased out in many municipalities)
5. **RI does not fully conform to federal bonus depreciation**: Check Section 179 and bonus depreciation adjustments

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Exempt for filers with federal AGI under $101,000 (Single) / $126,250 (MFJ); taxable above those thresholds
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable; no special exclusion
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no specific exclusion)
- **State/municipal pensions**: Taxable at regular rates
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal)
