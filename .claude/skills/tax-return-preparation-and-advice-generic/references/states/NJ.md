# New Jersey Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 10.75%
- Filing requirement threshold: $10,000 (Single), $20,000 (MFJ)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 1.4% | $0 - $20,000 |
| 1.75% | $20,001 - $35,000 |
| 3.5% | $35,001 - $40,000 |
| 5.525% | $40,001 - $75,000 |
| 6.37% | $75,001 - $500,000 |
| 8.97% | $500,001 - $1,000,000 |
| 10.75% | Over $1,000,000 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 1.4% | $0 - $20,000 |
| 1.75% | $20,001 - $50,000 |
| 2.45% | $50,001 - $70,000 |
| 3.5% | $70,001 - $80,000 |
| 5.525% | $80,001 - $150,000 |
| 6.37% | $150,001 - $500,000 |
| 8.97% | $500,001 - $1,000,000 |
| 10.75% | Over $1,000,000 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- New Jersey does NOT use a standard deduction
- Personal exemption: $1,000 per taxpayer
- Dependent exemption: $1,500 per dependent
- Additional exemptions: $1,000 if age 65+ or blind

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Property tax deduction/credit**: Deduct up to $15,000 in property taxes OR take a $50 credit (homeowners); $50 credit for renters (18.75% of rent = property tax equivalent)
2. **Pension exclusion**: Up to $100,000 for MFJ ($75,000 Single, $50,000 MFS) if 62+ or disabled AND total income under $150,000
3. **NJ Earned Income Tax Credit**: 40% of federal EITC (refundable)
4. **Child and Dependent Care Credit**: Percentage of expenses based on NJ income
5. **Excess unemployment/disability/FLI contributions**: Refundable credit if overwithheld
6. **Other retirement income exclusion**: Separate from pension exclusion for qualifying income
7. **529 Plan**: NJ does not offer a state income tax deduction for 529 contributions
8. **Charitable contribution deduction**: Not deductible on NJ return (NJ does not allow itemized deductions)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: NJ-1040 (resident), NJ-1040NR (nonresident)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (file by October 15); must pay estimated tax by April 15
- NJ does NOT have itemized deductions; uses its own system of above-the-line deductions

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $400
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax (80% if farmer/fisherman)
- Form: NJ-1040-ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Business Alternative Income Tax - BAIT)
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays tax at graduated rates on distributive share; members receive refundable credit
  - BAIT rate brackets:
    - Up to $250,000: 5.675%
    - $250,001 - $1,000,000: 6.52%
    - $1,000,001 - $5,000,000: 9.12%
    - Over $5,000,000: 10.75%
- Election deadline: Must be made on or before the entity's original return due date. First estimated payment due March 15 of the tax year
- Quarterly estimated payments required
- Both S corps and partnerships eligible

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in New Jersey
- However, property taxes are among the highest in the nation (average effective rate ~2.2%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No standard deduction or itemized deductions**: NJ uses its own system; property tax deduction is the main item. Mortgage interest is NOT deductible on NJ return
2. **Millionaire's tax**: The 10.75% rate kicks in at $1M; income just over $1M faces a sharp marginal increase
3. **NJ does not follow federal on many items**: No 401(k)/IRA deduction for contributions; NJ taxes Roth conversion differently
4. **Retirement contributions NOT deductible**: 401(k), IRA, and similar contributions are included in NJ gross income (basis is tracked for later exclusion)
5. **Exit tax**: NJ requires estimated tax payment on sale of real property by sellers who are leaving the state (2% of sale price or estimated tax, whichever is greater)
6. **NJ ANCHOR Program**: Property tax benefit program providing rebates to homeowners (income under $150K: $1,750; $150K-$250K: $1,000) and renters (income under $150K: $450). Filed separately; check current year availability and deadlines

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from NJ income tax
- **Pension/annuity exclusion**: Up to $100,000 MFJ / $75,000 Single / $50,000 MFS if age 62+ or disabled AND total income ≤ $150,000
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Taxable, but since contributions were taxed going in, NJ allows exclusion of the previously-taxed basis portion
- **Military retirement**: Fully exempt if received for service-related disability; otherwise subject to pension exclusion rules
- **Railroad Retirement**: Tier I exempt; Tier II taxable
