# West Virginia Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.12% (rates are being reduced)
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if required to file federal return and had WV-source income

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### All Filing Statuses
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 2.36% | $0 - $10,000 |
| 3.15% | $10,001 - $25,000 |
| 3.54% | $25,001 - $40,000 |
| 4.72% | $40,001 - $60,000 |
| 5.12% | Over $60,000 |

Note: West Virginia has been reducing rates. HB 2526 (2023) enacted a phased reduction plan. Rates listed are approximate for 2025; further reductions are scheduled if revenue triggers are met. Verify current year rates.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: West Virginia follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption: $2,000 per exemption (taxpayer, spouse, dependents)
- WV starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Social Security exemption**: Fully exempt from WV income tax (effective 2024+)
2. **Military retirement**: Partially exempt; WV allows a subtraction for a portion of military retirement income
3. **WV Earned Income Credit**: Available for qualifying filers
4. **Senior citizen credit**: Tax credit for qualifying elderly residents
5. **Property tax credit**: Homestead exemption for 65+ or disabled (first $20,000 of assessed value)
6. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $5,000 per account per year ($10,000 MFJ) for WV SMART529 contributions
7. **Low-income family tax credit**: For families below certain income thresholds
8. **Historic rehabilitation credit**: 25% of qualified expenses for certified historic structures
9. **Neighborhood Investment Program credit**: For contributions to approved organizations

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IT-140 (resident), IT-140NR (nonresident)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- WV starts with federal AGI; applies WV-specific adjustments
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $600
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: IT-140ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (WV PTET effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made annually on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays WV income tax on distributive shares at applicable rates; members receive credit on individual returns
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs
- Entity files Form PTE-100

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in West Virginia
- Municipal business and occupation taxes exist in some cities (gross receipts tax on businesses)
- Kanawha County (Charleston) and other localities have B&O taxes on businesses

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Rate reduction schedule**: WV has enacted phased rate reductions contingent on revenue performance; rates may drop further than shown. Always verify current year rates
2. **Social Security now fully exempt**: Effective 2024, ALL Social Security benefits are exempt from WV income tax regardless of income level
3. **Severance tax**: WV levies a severance tax on natural resources extraction (coal: 5%; oil: 5%; natural gas: 5%; timber: 3.22%); this is separate from income tax
4. **Same brackets for all filing statuses**: Unlike many states, WV uses identical brackets regardless of filing status
5. **Federal AGI starting point**: WV closely follows federal; most federal adjustments flow through

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt (effective 2024+; no income cap)
- **Military retirement**: Partial subtraction available (verify current year amount; WV has been expanding this exclusion)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable at regular rates; no broad exclusion
- **Federal government pensions**: Taxable
- **WV public employee pensions (CPRB)**: Taxable at regular rates
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal)
- **Senior citizen credit**: Available for qualifying elderly with low income
