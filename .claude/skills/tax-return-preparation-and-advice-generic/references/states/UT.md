# Utah Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.5% (flat rate)
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if required to file federal return

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Utah has a **flat income tax rate**:
- **4.5%** on all Utah taxable income (2025)
- Rate reduced from 4.65% to 4.5% effective January 1, 2025
- Utah calculates tax on all income, then applies a taxpayer tax credit that effectively creates progressivity

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Utah does NOT use a standard deduction in the traditional sense
- **Taxpayer tax credit**: 6% of the federal standard deduction amounts used on the TC-40 return. For 2025 this is about $945 (Single/MFS), $1,417.50 (HOH), and $1,890 (MFJ/QSS) before phaseout
- The taxpayer tax credit phases out from base amounts of $18,213 (Single/MFS), $27,320 (HOH), and $36,426 (MFJ/QSS)
- This mechanism effectively makes the flat tax progressive for lower-income filers
- Utah starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Taxpayer tax credit**: Primary credit (6% of federal deduction amounts); phases out at higher incomes
2. **Retirement tax credit**: Additional credit for taxpayers 65+ with retirement income (up to $450/$900 MFJ)
3. **At-home parent credit**: $100 credit for qualifying parents
4. **529 Plan credit**: 4.5% of qualifying contributions up to the Utah annual limit; use the current TC-40 instructions for the exact per-beneficiary cap
5. **Earned Income Tax Credit**: 15% of federal EITC (non-refundable; excess becomes refundable at reduced rate)
6. **Renewable energy systems credit**: For residential solar/wind installations
7. **Recycling market development zones credit**: For qualifying businesses
8. **Historic preservation credit**: For rehabilitation of qualifying structures
9. **Live organ donation expenses credit**: Up to $10,000 for qualifying donation expenses

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: TC-40 (individual return), various schedules
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- Utah starts with federal AGI and applies Utah-specific modifications
- e-File: Available and encouraged

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $1,000 (after withholding and credits)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: TC-546 (payment coupon)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (effective 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made annually on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays Utah tax at 4.5% on Utah-source income; members receive credit on individual returns
- Applies to S corps and partnerships
- Entity makes estimated payments on behalf of electing members

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in Utah
- Local revenue from property tax and local option sales tax
- Combined state + local sales tax: 6.1% to 9.05% depending on jurisdiction

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Flat tax with progressive effect**: The taxpayer tax credit mechanism means low-income filers can pay effectively 0%, while higher-income filers pay the full 4.5%. The credit phaseout creates effective marginal rates above 4.5% in the phaseout range
2. **Taxpayer tax credit phaseout**: In the phaseout range, the effective marginal rate exceeds 4.5% because the credit is being reduced simultaneously. This creates a "bump zone"
3. **Utah starts with federal AGI**: Most federal deductions do NOT flow through. Utah adds back itemized deductions and instead provides the taxpayer tax credit
4. **State-level Social Security taxation**: Utah taxes Social Security benefits but provides a partial credit (see Retirement section)
5. **Mining and extraction**: Utah has severance taxes on mining (2.6% on metalliferous minerals, various rates on oil/gas)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Included in Utah taxable income (follows federal inclusion), BUT a non-refundable credit offsets the tax for lower-income filers. Credit phases out starting at $37,500 (Single) / $75,000 (MFJ) in modified AGI
- **Retirement tax credit**: Age 65+ filers can claim additional credit of up to $450 ($900 MFJ) against tax on retirement income
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable at the 4.5% flat rate; retirement tax credit partially offsets for qualifying seniors
- **Military retirement**: No special exclusion (taxed at 4.5%)
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
