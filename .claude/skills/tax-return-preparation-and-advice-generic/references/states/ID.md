# Idaho Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.695% (flat rate as of 2023)
- Filing requirement threshold: Generally follows current federal filing-threshold concepts; verify Form 40 instructions for filing-status specifics
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Idaho State Tax Commission

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All Idaho taxable income | 5.695% |

Idaho transitioned to a flat 5.695% rate effective 2023 (previously had progressive brackets up to 6%). The flat rate applies uniformly to all taxable income.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Idaho generally conforms to federal standard deduction amounts:
- Single: $15,750
- MFJ: $31,500
- Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemption: Idaho allows personal exemptions (unlike federal post-TCJA) — $4,650 per person
- Dependent exemption: $4,650 per dependent
- Idaho starts with federal taxable income and makes Idaho-specific adjustments

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Grocery tax credit** — $120 per person ($60 for dependents) refundable credit to offset sales tax on groceries (Idaho taxes groceries)
2. **Property tax reduction (Circuit Breaker)** — Up to $1,500 for qualifying homeowners (age 65+, disabled, or widowed with income under $35,260)
3. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $6,000 ($12,000 MFJ) for contributions to IDeal Idaho 529 plan
4. **Child tax credit** — Idaho enacted a state child tax credit; $205 per qualifying child
5. **Charitable contribution deduction** — Deductible when itemizing; follows federal rules
6. **Investment tax credit** — For qualifying business investments in Idaho
7. **Technology equipment credit** — For qualifying technology investments
8. **Adoption expenses credit** — Idaho offers its own adoption tax credit

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 40 (resident), Form 43 (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form 51
- E-file options: Idaho State Tax Commission TAP portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2023, HB 292)
- Election deadline: Made on timely filed PTE return
- Rate: 5.695% (same as individual flat rate)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level on members' pro rata share of Idaho income; members receive a credit on their individual Idaho return. Available for partnerships and S-corporations.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Idaho does not have local/city income taxes
- Local governments rely on property taxes and local option sales taxes
- State sales tax is 6%; no additional local sales tax in most areas
- A few resort cities (Sun Valley, McCall) levy local option non-property taxes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Groceries are taxed** — Idaho is one of few states that taxes groceries at the full sales tax rate (6%); the grocery tax credit is meant to offset this
2. **Flat rate simplicity** — The 5.695% flat rate simplifies filing but provides no bracket benefit for lower-income filers
3. **Idaho allows personal exemptions** — Unlike the federal return post-TCJA, Idaho still grants personal exemptions
4. **Federal conformity** — Idaho generally conforms to the IRC with a rolling conformity date; check current conformity for recent federal changes
5. **Capital gains deduction** — Idaho allows a deduction for up to 60% of qualifying net capital gains from Idaho property (held 12+ months), significantly reducing effective rate on Idaho-source capital gains

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Idaho income tax
- **Public pensions (state/local)**: Taxable as ordinary income (no exclusion)
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable as ordinary income
- **Military pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income (no special exclusion)
- **Railroad Retirement**: Tier I exempt (follows federal); Tier II taxable
- **Idaho is moderately retirement-friendly** due to Social Security exemption and the flat rate, but lacks pension exclusions
