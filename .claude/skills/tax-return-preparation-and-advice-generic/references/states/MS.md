# Mississippi Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5% (after reform; first $10,000 exempt)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds $8,300 (Single) or $16,600 (MFJ)
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Mississippi Department of Revenue (MSDOR)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $10,000 | 0% (exempt) |
| Over $10,000 | 5% |

Mississippi significantly reformed its brackets starting 2023-2024. The previous 3-bracket system (0%/3%/4%/5%) was simplified. The first $10,000 of taxable income is exempt, and all income above $10,000 is taxed at 5%. The rate is scheduled to decrease to 4% by 2026 via revenue triggers.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $2,300 standard deduction; $6,000 personal exemption
- MFJ: $4,600 standard deduction; $12,000 combined personal exemption
- Head of Household: $3,400 standard deduction; $8,000 personal exemption
- Dependent exemption: $1,500 per dependent
- Mississippi itemized deductions are available as an alternative; generally follow federal rules

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **First $10,000 exempt** — The 0% bracket on the first $10,000 of taxable income functions like an additional deduction
2. **Retirement/pension exclusion** — All retirement income from qualified plans is fully exempt (see Retirement section)
3. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $10,000 ($20,000 MFJ) for contributions to Mississippi Affordable College Savings (MACS) program
4. **Child care credit** — Available for qualifying child care expenses
5. **Earned income credit** — Mississippi does not have a state EITC
6. **Charitable contributions** — Deductible when itemizing
7. **Property tax deduction** — Deductible when itemizing (follows federal rules)
8. **MS Homestead exemption** — First $7,500 of assessed value exempt from property tax for homeowners (age 65+ or disabled: full exemption on first $75,000 of assessed value)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 80-105 (resident), Form 80-205 (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form 80-106
- E-file options: Mississippi TAP (Taxpayer Access Point), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $200 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, HB 1691)
- Election deadline: Made on the entity's return by the original due date (including extensions)
- Rate: 5% (same as individual rate)
- Mechanics: Electing entities pay tax at entity level on electing members' share of Mississippi income. Members receive a credit on their Mississippi return. Available for partnerships, S-corps, and LLCs.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Mississippi does not have local/city income taxes
- Municipalities rely on property taxes and local sales taxes
- State sales tax is 7% (one of the highest in the nation); no additional local sales tax in most areas
- Some tourism/hospitality taxes apply in specific areas

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Retirement income is fully exempt** — Mississippi exempts ALL qualified retirement income (Social Security, pensions, 401k, IRA), making it one of the most retirement-friendly states in the US
2. **Rate dropping to 4%** — Mississippi is on track to reduce the rate from 5% to 4% by 2026. Verify the current year rate.
3. **$10,000 zero bracket** — The first $10,000 of taxable income is tax-free, providing significant relief for lower-income filers
4. **High sales tax** — Mississippi's 7% sales tax (including on groceries) is among the highest in the nation, partially offsetting low income tax rates
5. **Separate return required for married couples** — Mississippi generally requires married couples to file separate returns, though combined returns are available. MFJ is the default but verify advantages of MFS.

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Mississippi income tax
- **State/local pensions (PERS)**: Fully exempt
- **Federal pensions**: Fully exempt
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt
- **Private pensions**: Fully exempt
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Fully exempt from qualified plans
- **Railroad Retirement**: Fully exempt
- **Mississippi is one of the MOST retirement-friendly states** — virtually all qualified retirement income is exempt from state income tax
