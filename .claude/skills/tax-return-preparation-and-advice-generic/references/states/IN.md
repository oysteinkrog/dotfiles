# Indiana Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 3.05% (flat state rate; county taxes add 0.5-2.9% on top)
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if Indiana AGI exceeds exemptions, or if state/county tax was withheld
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Indiana Department of Revenue (DOR)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### State Rate — All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All Indiana adjusted gross income | 3.05% |

Indiana has a flat state income tax rate. The rate was reduced from 3.15% to 3.05% effective 2025 (scheduled to continue decreasing to 2.9% by 2027).

### County Income Tax (in addition to state)
- **91 of 92 Indiana counties** levy a county income tax (only Ohio County does not)
- Rates range from 0.5% to 2.9%
- Common rates: Marion County (Indianapolis) 2.02%; Hamilton County 1.0%; Allen County (Fort Wayne) 1.48%; Lake County 1.5%
- County tax is based on county of residence as of January 1

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Indiana does not use a standard deduction in the traditional sense
- **Personal exemption**: $1,000 per person
- **Dependent exemption**: $1,500 per dependent
- **Additional exemptions**: $1,000 for age 65+; $1,000 for blindness
- **Renters deduction**: Up to $3,000 for qualifying renters
- **Mortgage interest deduction**: Not separately available (Indiana does not allow itemized deductions — uses its own exemptions/deductions)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **529 Plan credit** — 20% of contributions to Indiana CollegeChoice 529 plans, up to $1,500 credit per year ($750 per taxpayer). This is a CREDIT, not a deduction, making it very valuable.
2. **Unified tax credit for the elderly** — Credit against tax for qualifying seniors
3. **Earned income credit** — 10% of federal EITC (refundable)
4. **Homeowner's residential property tax deduction** — Up to $2,500 for property taxes paid on Indiana residence
5. **Renter's deduction** — Up to $3,000 of rent paid on principal residence
6. **County tax credit** — Credit for county taxes paid (automatic; offsets county liability)
7. **Military service deduction** — Up to $5,000 for active-duty military pay
8. **Human services tax credit** — For contributions to neighborhood organizations
9. **Adoption credit** — Indiana provides an adoption tax credit

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IT-40 (resident full year), IT-40PNR (part-year/nonresident), IT-40EZ (simplified)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 60-day extension (to June 15, not October); for longer extension, must file Form IT-9 by April 15 for extension to November 15
- E-file options: Indiana DOR INtax portal (INfreefile for qualifying filers), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected state and county tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding/credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2023, HB 1001)
- Election deadline: Made annually on or before the 15th day of the 4th month following close of tax year
- Rate: 3.05% state rate (county rates do NOT apply at entity level)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay 3.05% on electing members' share of Indiana income. Members claim a credit on their IT-40. Does not cover county tax — members still owe county tax individually.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- **County income taxes** are the primary local tax — see rates above
- County tax is administered by the state (filed on the same IT-40 return)
- No separate city income taxes (county tax covers the entire county including cities)
- County rates are set annually; check current year rates at DOR website
- Marion County (Indianapolis) has one of the higher county rates at 2.02%

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **County taxes add significantly to the burden** — While the 3.05% state rate is low, adding county taxes of 1-2.9% can nearly double the effective rate. A Marion County resident pays 5.07% total.
2. **529 credit is among the best in the US** — The 20% credit (up to $1,500) on CollegeChoice contributions is more valuable than deductions offered by other states
3. **No itemized deductions** — Indiana does not allow federal-style itemized deductions. It has its own system of exemptions and deductions (property tax, renter's, etc.)
4. **Extension is only 60 days by default** — Indiana's automatic extension is shorter than most states; must affirmatively request a longer extension
5. **PTET does not cover county tax** — The entity-level tax only covers the 3.05% state rate; county taxes remain the individual member's responsibility
6. **January 1 residency determines county** — County tax is based on where you lived on January 1 of the tax year, not where you live when filing

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Indiana income tax
- **Federal/state/local pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income (no exclusion)
- **Military pensions**: Deduction of up to $5,000 for military retirement pay
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Taxable as ordinary income
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- **Indiana is moderately retirement-friendly** due to low flat rate and SS exemption, but lacks pension exclusions
