# Kansas Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.7%
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds $5,225 (Single) or filing status thresholds (Kansas-specific, lower than federal)
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Kansas Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
| Kansas Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $15,000 | 3.1% |
| $15,001 - $30,000 | 5.25% |
| Over $30,000 | 5.7% |

### Married Filing Jointly
| Kansas Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $30,000 | 3.1% |
| $30,001 - $60,000 | 5.25% |
| Over $60,000 | 5.7% |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $3,500 standard deduction
- MFJ: $8,000 standard deduction
- Head of Household: $6,000 standard deduction
- Personal exemption: $2,250 per person
- Dependent exemption: $2,250 per dependent
- Kansas itemized deductions are available and generally follow federal rules

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Social Security exemption** — Fully exempt for Kansas AGI of $75,000 or less; proportionally reduced above that
2. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $3,000 ($6,000 MFJ) for contributions to Kansas Learning Quest 529 plan or any state's 529
3. **Property tax credit (Homestead)** — Refundable credit for qualifying homeowners/renters (age 55+, disabled, or with dependent child; household income under $37,750)
4. **Food sales tax credit** — Refundable credit to offset sales tax on food ($125 per person; income-limited)
5. **Child and dependent care credit** — 25% of the federal credit (nonrefundable)
6. **Earned income credit** — Kansas does not have a state EITC (as of 2025)
7. **Adoption credit** — Kansas provides its own adoption credit
8. **Swine facility improvement credit** — Agricultural credit unique to Kansas
9. **Historic preservation credit** — 25% of qualified rehabilitation expenditures
10. **Military compensation subtraction** — Various subtractions for military pay and death benefits

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: K-40 (all individual filers — resident, nonresident, part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file K-40V with payment
- E-file options: WebFile (Kansas free e-file system), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, SB 347)
- Election deadline: Made on or before the 15th day of the 4th month of the tax year (effectively April 15 for calendar year entities)
- Rate: Progressive rates matching individual brackets (3.1% / 5.25% / 5.7%)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay Kansas income tax at the entity level on electing members' shares. Members receive a credit on their K-40. Available for partnerships and S-corps.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Kansas does not have local/city income taxes
- Kansas cities levy local sales taxes (1-3% range on top of the 6.5% state rate)
- Combined sales tax can exceed 10% in some jurisdictions (e.g., parts of Overland Park, Wichita)
- Intangibles tax: Some counties levy an intangibles tax on interest, dividends, and securities profits (rates vary 0.25-0.75%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Social Security exemption has an AGI cliff** — Fully exempt below $75,000 Kansas AGI, but subject to phaseout above that; can create a planning opportunity near the threshold
2. **Low bracket thresholds** — Top rate of 5.7% kicks in at just $30,000 (Single) / $60,000 (MFJ), so most filers pay the top rate on most of their income
3. **Intangibles tax is unusual** — Some Kansas counties still impose an intangibles tax on investment income, which is rare nationally. Check county rules.
4. **Kansas does not conform to all federal provisions** — Kansas uses a static conformity date; verify which IRC version Kansas conforms to for the current year
5. **No state EITC** — Unlike many neighboring states, Kansas does not offer a state earned income credit

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt if Kansas AGI is $75,000 or less; taxable above that threshold
- **Public pensions (KPERS)**: Exempt from Kansas income tax
- **Federal pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income (no exclusion)
- **Military pensions**: Exempt from Kansas income tax
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable as ordinary income
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- **Kansas is moderately retirement-friendly** due to Social Security and KPERS exemptions, but private retirement income is fully taxable
