# South Dakota Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No
- Top marginal rate: N/A (no personal income tax)
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
South Dakota imposes no personal income tax and no corporate income tax. The state constitution does not prohibit an income tax, but the legislature has never enacted one.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- N/A (no income tax)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
- N/A for individual income tax purposes
- **Bank franchise tax**: 0.4% on net income for banks and financial institutions
- **Insurance company premium tax**: 2.5% on premiums
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax

## Filing Requirements
- No individual income tax return required
- Businesses may have other filing obligations (sales tax, bank franchise tax)
- Employers must withhold and remit payroll taxes for federal purposes only (no state withholding)

## Estimated Tax Payments
- N/A for individual income tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Not applicable (no individual income tax to work around)

## Local/City Income Taxes
- None. No city or county in South Dakota levies an income tax

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No income tax but moderate sales tax**: State sales tax is 4.2% (was 4.5%; reduced in 2023); combined with local rates can reach ~6.5%
2. **Trust-friendly state**: South Dakota is one of the premier trust jurisdictions in the US:
   - No state income tax on trust income
   - Dynasty trusts allowed (no rule against perpetuities)
   - Strong asset protection trust statutes
   - Trust decanting permitted
   - Privacy-friendly trust laws
3. **No estate or inheritance tax**: Combined with no income tax, makes SD very favorable for wealth transfer planning
4. **Agricultural state**: Farm income is not subject to state income tax, making SD attractive for agricultural operations
5. **Sales tax on services**: SD is unusual in taxing many services that other states exempt (e.g., accounting, legal, engineering services)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Military retirement**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- South Dakota is highly favorable for retirees

## Business Tax Considerations
- **No corporate income tax**: SD does not tax corporate income
- **Sales tax**: 4.2% state rate; applies broadly to goods and many services
- **Contractor's excise tax**: 2% on gross receipts for prime contractors
- **Tourism tax**: 1.5% on lodging and amusements in addition to sales tax
- **Bank franchise tax**: 0.4% on net income for financial institutions
- **No business income tax of any kind**: Sole proprietors, LLCs, partnerships, and S corps pay no state income tax

## Trust and Estate Planning Advantages
- No income tax on trust income (even for non-grantor trusts)
- No rule against perpetuities: trusts can last indefinitely (dynasty trusts)
- Domestic Asset Protection Trusts (DAPTs) available with 2-year statute of limitations
- Directed trusts statute: allows separation of investment, distribution, and administrative functions
- Trust privacy: trust documents are not public record
- Trust decanting: can modify irrevocable trusts
- Many national trust companies have SD offices specifically for these advantages

## Property Tax
- Property taxes exist but are moderate (effective rate ~1.2%)
- Agricultural land assessed at productivity value
- Owner-occupied homes get lower assessment
- No estate tax (SD repealed its estate tax)
