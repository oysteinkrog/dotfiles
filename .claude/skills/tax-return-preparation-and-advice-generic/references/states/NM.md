# New Mexico Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.9%
- Filing requirement threshold: Federal filing requirement applies; must file if NM income exceeds threshold

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 1.7% | $0 - $5,500 |
| 3.2% | $5,501 - $11,000 |
| 4.7% | $11,001 - $16,000 |
| 4.9% | $16,001 - $210,000 |
| 5.9% | Over $210,000 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 1.7% | $0 - $8,000 |
| 3.2% | $8,001 - $16,000 |
| 4.7% | $16,001 - $24,000 |
| 4.9% | $24,001 - $315,000 |
| 5.9% | Over $315,000 |

### Head of Household
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 1.7% | $0 - $6,750 |
| 3.2% | $6,751 - $13,500 |
| 4.7% | $13,501 - $20,000 |
| 4.9% | $20,001 - $262,500 |
| 5.9% | Over $262,500 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: New Mexico follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption: None (follows federal, which eliminated personal exemptions under TCJA)
- Low-income exemption: Additional exemption of up to $2,500 for lower-income filers

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Low Income Comprehensive Tax Rebate (LICTR)**: Refundable credit for low/moderate income; sliding scale based on income and dependents
2. **Social Security exemption**: Fully exempt from NM income tax (effective 2022+)
3. **Working Families Tax Credit**: 25% of federal EITC (refundable)
4. **Child income tax credit**: Up to $600 per child under 6 (refundable, income-phased)
5. **Medical care expense deduction**: Unreimbursed medical expenses exceeding a percentage of income
6. **Renewable energy credits**: Solar market development tax credit (10% of system cost, up to $6,000)
7. **Property tax rebate**: For low-income residents (claimed on PIT-RC)
8. **Veterans property tax exemption**: Various exemptions for disabled veterans
9. **Angel Investment credit**: 25% credit for investments in NM high-tech or manufacturing businesses

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: PIT-1 (resident), PIT-1 (nonresident uses same form with Schedule PIT-B)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15); must pay estimated tax by April 15
- All income of NM residents is subject to NM tax regardless of source

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $1,000 (after credits and withholding)
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: PIT-ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (effective 2022)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's return; must be elected annually
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity elects to pay NM income tax on behalf of owners at entity level; owners receive credit on individual return
- Applies to S corporations, partnerships, and LLCs taxed as partnerships

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in New Mexico
- Local gross receipts tax (GRT) rates vary by municipality (combined state + local GRT ranges from 5.0% to ~9.4%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Gross receipts tax is NOT a sales tax**: NM GRT is imposed on the seller/business, not the buyer (though it is commonly passed through). This affects nexus and sourcing rules differently than a sales tax
2. **Social Security fully exempt**: Since 2022, all Social Security benefits are exempt regardless of income
3. **Community property state**: Income splitting rules apply for MFS filers
4. **Film production credits**: Significant credits for film/TV production (25-35% refundable credit)
5. **Tribal income**: Special rules apply for income earned on tribal lands by enrolled members

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt (since 2022)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable at regular rates; no special exclusion
- **Military retirement**: Exempt up to $30,000 for residents who are 65+ (or disabled)
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal)
- **Federal government pensions**: Taxable at regular rates
