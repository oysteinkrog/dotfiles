# Alaska Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No
- Top marginal rate: N/A (no state income tax)
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A
- Fiscal year: N/A

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Alaska does not impose a state individual income tax. There is no state-level filing requirement for individual income tax.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Not applicable — no state income tax.

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
Not applicable for income tax purposes. However, notable state programs:
1. **Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD)** — Annual payment to eligible residents from Alaska Permanent Fund oil revenues. 2024 PFD was $1,702. PFD is taxable on federal returns but has no state tax impact.
2. **Senior property tax exemption** — Residents 65+ may be exempt from property tax on first $150,000 of assessed value (varies by municipality)
3. **Education tax credit** — Alaska has no personal income tax, so no state-level education credits
4. **No estate or inheritance tax** — Alaska has neither
5. **Alaska Community Dividend** — Some municipalities provide additional local dividends

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: None required for state income tax
- Due date: N/A
- Extension: N/A
- E-file options: N/A
- **Important**: Alaska residents must still file federal income tax returns. PFD income is reported on federal Form 1040.

## Estimated Tax Payments
Not applicable — no state income tax.

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: No (no individual income tax to bypass)
- Note: Alaska does impose a corporate income tax (0-9.4% progressive) on C-corporations, but this does not apply to individuals or pass-through entities for individual income purposes.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No city or municipality in Alaska levies a local income tax
- Municipalities rely on property taxes and sales taxes instead
- Some boroughs have no sales tax either (e.g., parts of the Matanuska-Susitna Borough)
- Anchorage has no local sales tax; Juneau has a 5% sales tax

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **PFD is federally taxable** — The Permanent Fund Dividend must be reported as income on the federal return (Form 1040, line 8 or Schedule 1). Many residents overlook this.
2. **PFD garnishment** — PFD can be garnished for child support, prior PFD overpayments, student loans, and other state debts
3. **Residency for PFD** — Must have been physically present in Alaska for the entire qualifying year and intend to remain permanently. Military members have special rules.
4. **Corporate income tax exists** — While individuals pay no income tax, Alaska is the only state with no sales tax AND no income tax. But it does tax corporate net income at 0-9.4%.
5. **Municipal taxes vary widely** — Property tax rates range from roughly 0 to 20 mills depending on borough/city; some areas have no property tax at all
6. **No state estate tax** — Alaska has no estate tax, though Alaska trusts (dynasty trusts) are popular for estate planning nationwide

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed at state level (no income tax)
- **Pensions**: Not taxed at state level
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Not taxed at state level
- **All retirement income** is only subject to federal taxation for Alaska residents
- Alaska is one of the most tax-friendly states for retirees
