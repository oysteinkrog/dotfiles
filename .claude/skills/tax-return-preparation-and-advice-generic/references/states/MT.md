# Montana Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 6.75%
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds $5,580 (Single) or filing status thresholds
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Montana Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Montana Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $3,600 | 1% |
| $3,601 - $6,300 | 2% |
| $6,301 - $9,600 | 3% |
| $9,601 - $13,000 | 4% |
| $13,001 - $16,800 | 5% |
| $16,801 - $21,600 | 6% |
| Over $21,600 | 6.75% |

Note: Montana reformed its brackets in 2024 under HB 222/SB 121. The previous system had 7 brackets from 1% to 6.75%. Montana uses the same brackets for all filing statuses. Verify current year brackets as further reforms may adjust thresholds.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Montana conforms to federal standard deduction amounts:
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemption: Montana adopted federal standard deduction under recent reforms, replacing its former 20% itemized deduction structure
- Dependent exemption: $2,800 per dependent (Montana-specific)
- Montana allows itemized deductions as an alternative

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **No sales tax** — Montana has no state or local sales tax, reducing overall tax burden
2. **Property tax assistance** — Montana Property Tax Assistance Program (PTAP) for lower-income homeowners/renters; reduces property tax rate
3. **Elderly homeowner/renter credit** — Refundable credit for qualifying seniors (62+) or disabled; based on property tax or rent relative to income
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $3,000 ($6,000 MFJ) for contributions to Montana's Achieve Montana 529 plan
5. **Capital gains credit** — Montana offers a 2% credit on net capital gains (reducing effective rate on capital gains)
6. **Earned income credit** — 3% of federal EITC (modest but refundable)
7. **Mineral royalty deduction** — Deduction for qualifying mineral royalty income
8. **Energy conservation credit** — For qualifying energy-efficient improvements to residential property
9. **Adoption credit** — Montana provides a credit for qualifying adoption expenses
10. **Charitable endowment credit** — 40% of contributions to permanent Montana charitable endowments (up to $10,000 credit)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 2 (individual income tax return — all filers), Form 2EC (elderly care credit), Form PTAP (property tax assistance)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form EXT
- E-file options: TransAction Portal (TAP), approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, HB 153)
- Election deadline: Made on the entity's return by the due date (including extensions)
- Rate: 6.75% (top individual rate) on electing members' share of Montana income
- Mechanics: Electing entities (S-corps, partnerships, LLCs) pay tax at entity level. Members receive a credit on their Montana return. The credit is nonrefundable but can be carried forward.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Montana does not have local/city income taxes
- No municipality in Montana imposes an income tax
- Local governments rely on property taxes
- **No sales tax** — Montana is one of five states with no sales tax (state or local)
- Some resort communities levy a local resort/bed tax on accommodations

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No sales tax is a major benefit** — Montana is one of only five states with zero sales tax (state or local), which partially compensates for the relatively high income tax rates
2. **Low bracket thresholds** — The top 6.75% rate kicks in at just $21,600, meaning most filers pay the top rate on the majority of their income
3. **Same brackets for all filing statuses** — MFJ filers get no bracket advantage over Single filers, creating a marriage penalty for joint filers
4. **Capital gains credit** — The 2% credit on net capital gains effectively reduces the tax rate on capital gains to 4.75%, a significant benefit for investors
5. **Resort tax** — Montana has a unique 3% resort tax in designated tourist areas (Big Sky, Red Lodge, West Yellowstone, etc.) on luxury goods and services
6. **Mineral/natural resource income** — Montana's mineral royalty deduction and oil/gas provisions are important for residents with natural resource income

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Partially exempt — Montana allows a subtraction for Social Security income, with income-based phaseout. Follows federal taxation rules with Montana modifications.
- **Public pensions (PERS, TRS)**: Taxable as ordinary income; may qualify for the $4,640 per person pension exclusion (for pensions where employee made contributions)
- **Federal pensions**: Taxable as ordinary income; may qualify for pension exclusion
- **Military pensions**: Partially exempt (up to $4,640 pension exclusion)
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable as ordinary income; pension exclusion may apply to contributory plans
- **Montana is NOT particularly retirement-friendly** — The high top rate and limited exclusions make it less attractive than many other states for retirees
