# Iowa Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 3.8% (flat rate)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds $9,000 (Single) or $13,500 (MFJ) for under 65
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Iowa Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
Iowa is transitioning to a flat rate system under SF 2417 (2022):

**2025 Rate**: 3.8% flat rate on all Iowa taxable income

Prior schedule for reference:
- 2023: Flat 4.4%
- 2024: Flat 3.9%
- 2025: Flat 3.8%
- 2026+: Flat 3.8% (permanent)

The old progressive brackets (up to 8.53%) were fully replaced by 2023.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Iowa standard deduction amounts are indexed and should be pulled from the current IA 1040 instructions
- Personal exemption credit: $40 per person
- Dependent credit: $40 per dependent
- Over 65/blind: Additional $20 credit per qualifying condition
- Iowa's standard deduction is much lower than federal

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Federal tax deduction — ELIMINATED** — Iowa previously allowed deduction of federal income taxes paid, but this was repealed as part of the flat tax transition. No longer available starting 2023.
2. **Retirement income exclusion** — Up to $6,000 per person ($12,000 MFJ) for qualifying retirement income if age 55+; or $12,000 per person if disabled
3. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $3,785 per beneficiary for contributions to College Savings Iowa or IAdvisor 529 plans
4. **Earned income credit** — 15% of federal EITC (refundable)
5. **Child and dependent care credit** — 75% of federal credit (one of the most generous state matches)
6. **Tuition and textbook credit** — Up to $250 ($500 MFJ) for K-12 tuition and textbook expenses
7. **Farm tenancy credit** — For cash rent farm tenants who are Iowa residents
8. **Research activities credit** — 6.5% of qualifying research expenditures in Iowa

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IA 1040 (all individual filers)
- Due date: April 30 (NOTE: Iowa's deadline is April 30, not April 15)
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 31 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file IA 4868
- E-file options: Iowa eFile & Pay portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $200 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 30, June 30, September 30, January 31 (aligned with Iowa's April 30 due date)
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior year Iowa net income exceeds $150,000)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2023, SF 2417)
- Election deadline: Made on the entity's Iowa return by the due date (including extensions)
- Rate: 3.8% (same as individual flat rate for 2025)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level on members' share of Iowa-source income. Members claim a credit on IA 1040. Available for partnerships, S-corps, and LLCs.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Iowa does not have local/city income taxes
- Local school districts levy property taxes (among the highest in the Midwest)
- Iowa has a local option sales tax (LOST) of up to 1% in addition to the 6% state sales tax
- Appanoose County has an additional 1% LOST for specific purposes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Federal tax deduction is gone** — Practitioners accustomed to Iowa's long-standing federal tax deduction must note it was eliminated with the flat tax reform. Do not claim it.
2. **April 30 filing deadline** — Iowa uses April 30 (not April 15). Estimated payment dates are also shifted.
3. **Flat rate transition complete** — Iowa has fully transitioned from a complex progressive system (9 brackets, top 8.53%) to a simple flat rate
4. **Low standard deduction** — Iowa's standard deduction is much lower than federal; this increases Iowa taxable income relative to federal
5. **Generous child care credit** — At 75% of the federal credit, Iowa's child care credit is among the most generous in the country
6. **Married filing separately on combined form** — Iowa allows MFS on a combined form, which can sometimes produce better results than MFJ

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Iowa income tax (became fully exempt in 2023)
- **Public pensions (state/local)**: Up to $6,000 exclusion per person (age 55+)
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Up to $6,000 exclusion per person (age 55+)
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt from Iowa income tax
- **Railroad Retirement**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- **Iowa is moderately retirement-friendly** with the low 3.8% flat rate, full SS exemption, and partial pension exclusion
