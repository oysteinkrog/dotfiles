# Illinois Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.95% (flat rate)
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if Illinois base income exceeds Illinois exemption allowance
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Illinois Department of Revenue (IDOR)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| All Illinois net income | 4.95% |

Illinois has a constitutionally mandated flat rate. A 2020 ballot measure to switch to graduated rates was defeated by voters.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Illinois does not use a standard deduction
- Illinois starts with federal AGI and makes modifications to arrive at Illinois base income
- **Personal exemption**: $2,625 (2025)
- **Dependent exemption**: Generally matches the current personal exemption amount
- These are subtracted from Illinois base income to arrive at Illinois net income

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Property tax credit** — 5% of property taxes paid on principal residence (nonrefundable)
2. **Illinois EITC** — 20% of federal EITC (refundable)
3. **Education expense credit** — 25% of qualifying K-12 education expenses, up to $750 per family (max credit $187.50) — expenses over $250
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $10,000 ($20,000 MFJ) for contributions to Bright Start or Bright Directions 529 plans
5. **Retirement income subtraction** — Federally taxed retirement income (Social Security, pensions, 401k, IRA) is subtracted from Illinois base income — making most retirement income exempt
6. **Volunteer emergency worker credit** — $500 for qualifying volunteer firefighters/EMS
7. **Film production credit** — 30% credit for qualified production expenditures (primarily businesses)
8. **Historic preservation credit** — 25% of qualified rehabilitation expenditures
9. **Child tax credit (new)** — Illinois has been expanding child-related credits; verify current year

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: IL-1040 (all individual filers — resident, nonresident, and part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file IL-505-I
- E-file options: MyTax Illinois portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $500 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2021, SB 2531)
- Election deadline: Made on the PTE's return, due on or before the original due date
- Rate: 4.95% (same as individual flat rate)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay 4.95% on net income of electing members. Members receive a credit against their Illinois individual income tax. Partnerships, S-corps, and LLCs taxed as partnerships are eligible.
- **Important**: The entity must make the election for EACH tax year; it is not automatic

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Illinois does not have traditional local income taxes
- **However**, Chicago and Cook County considerations are significant:
  - Chicago: No city income tax; high property taxes. (Note: Chicago's head tax on employers was repealed effective 2024.)
  - Cook County: Additional sales taxes (Cook County = 1.75% on top of state 6.25%)
  - No local income tax levied anywhere in Illinois

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Retirement income is essentially tax-free** — Illinois subtracts virtually all federally taxed retirement income (Social Security, pensions, 401k, IRA distributions), making it extremely retirement-friendly despite the 4.95% rate
2. **No standard deduction** — Illinois does not offer a standard deduction; the personal exemption ($2,625 for 2025) is the only above-the-line reduction, which is much lower than federal standard deduction
3. **Flat rate is constitutional** — Cannot be changed to graduated rates without a constitutional amendment (voters rejected this in 2020)
4. **High property taxes** — Illinois has some of the highest property taxes in the nation (effective rates 2-3%+), especially in Cook County
5. **No deduction for Illinois income tax paid** — Unlike some states, you cannot deduct state taxes on the state return
6. **Replacement tax** — PTEs (partnerships and S-corps) pay an additional 1.5% replacement tax on net income; C-corporations and trusts/estates pay 2.5% (separate from the PTET election)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt (subtracted from base income)
- **Federal/state/local pensions**: Fully exempt (subtracted from base income)
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Fully exempt (subtracted from base income)
- **Private pensions/annuities**: Fully exempt if previously taxed at the federal level
- **Illinois is one of the MOST retirement-friendly states** — virtually all retirement income is exempt from state income tax
