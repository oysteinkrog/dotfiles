# Virginia Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.75%
- Filing requirement threshold: $11,950 (Single), $23,900 (MFJ) — Virginia-specific thresholds

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### All Filing Statuses (same brackets)
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 2% | $0 - $3,000 |
| 3% | $3,001 - $5,000 |
| 5% | $5,001 - $17,000 |
| 5.75% | Over $17,000 |

Note: Virginia uses the same brackets for all filing statuses. The top rate hits at only $17,000 of taxable income, meaning most filers pay 5.75% on the bulk of their income.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $8,000
  - MFJ: $16,000
  - Note: Virginia's standard deduction is lower than federal
- Personal exemption: $930 per exemption (taxpayer, spouse, each dependent)
- Additional exemption: $800 for age 65+ and/or blind (per qualifying person)
- Itemized deductions: Virginia allows itemized deductions (generally follows federal with modifications)
- Virginia starts with federal AGI

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Age 65+ deduction**: Up to $12,000 deduction for each taxpayer age 65+. Phases out for AGI over $75,000 (Single) / $75,000 (each spouse). Eliminated above $87,000
2. **Military retirement subtraction**: Veterans under age 55 can subtract up to $20,000 of military retirement income; those 55+ can claim the age deduction instead
3. **529 Plan deduction**: $4,000 per account per year for Virginia529 contributions (unlimited carryforward of excess); age 70+ can deduct entire contribution in one year
4. **Virginia Earned Income Credit**: 15% of federal EITC (refundable)
5. **Land preservation credit**: Up to 40% of fair market value for qualifying conservation easements
6. **Historic rehabilitation credit**: 25% of eligible expenses
7. **Neighborhood Assistance credit**: For contributions to approved neighborhood organizations
8. **Long-term care insurance credit**: 15% of premiums paid
9. **Political contribution credit**: Removed (no longer available)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 760 (resident), Form 763 (nonresident), Form 760PY (part-year)
- Due date: May 1 (Virginia is one of the few states NOT on April 15)
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (November 1); must pay estimated tax by May 1
- Virginia starts with federal AGI
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $150 (very low threshold)
- Due dates: May 1, June 15, September 15, January 15 (note: first payment aligns with VA filing deadline, not April 15)
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: 760-ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Virginia PTET effective 2021+)
- Election deadline: Entity must make election by the due date of the return (including extensions)
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays tax at 5.75% on Virginia-source income; members receive refundable credit
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, and LLCs
- Quarterly estimated payments required

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No separate local income tax returns, but Virginia localities can impose taxes collected through the state return
- **Local taxes embedded in state system**: Virginia counties and cities can levy taxes on income, but these are typically property taxes, not income taxes
- **Business license taxes (BPOL)**: Many Virginia localities levy a gross receipts business license tax (rates vary by locality and business type, typically 0.1-0.5%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **May 1 filing deadline**: Virginia's individual return due date is May 1, NOT April 15. This catches many filers off guard
2. **Low top bracket threshold**: The 5.75% rate kicks in at just $17,000 of taxable income — effectively a near-flat tax for most filers
3. **Age 65+ deduction is generous but income-limited**: The $12,000 deduction phases out completely at $87,000 AGI; high-income seniors get nothing
4. **529 Plan unlimited carryforward**: VA allows unlimited carryforward of 529 contributions exceeding the $4,000 annual limit; age 70+ get unlimited deduction
5. **BPOL tax**: Virginia's Business Professional and Occupational License tax is a local gross receipts tax that surprises many business owners; rates and applicability vary by jurisdiction

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Virginia income tax
- **Age 65+ deduction**: Up to $12,000 per person for ALL income (not just retirement income); phases out at $75,000-$87,000 AGI
- **Military retirement**: Subtraction of up to $20,000 for veterans under 55; those 55+ use the age deduction
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable at regular rates; age 65+ deduction helps offset
- **Federal government pensions**: Taxable; age deduction applies
- **Virginia Retirement System (VRS)**: Taxable; age deduction applies
