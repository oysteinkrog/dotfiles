# Missouri Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.95% (reduced from 5.0% for 2025; continuing to decrease via revenue triggers)
- Filing requirement threshold: Missouri AGI exceeds $1,300 (Single) or $2,600 (MFJ) with modifications
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Missouri Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### All Filing Statuses (Same Brackets)
| Missouri Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $1,207 | 0% |
| $1,208 - $2,414 | 2.0% |
| $2,415 - $3,621 | 2.5% |
| $3,622 - $4,828 | 3.0% |
| $4,829 - $6,035 | 3.5% |
| $6,036 - $7,242 | 4.0% |
| $7,243 - $8,449 | 4.5% |
| Over $8,449 | 4.95% |

Note: Missouri uses the same brackets regardless of filing status. The top rate is scheduled to decrease by 0.05% per year via revenue triggers (SB 3, 2022) toward a potential floor of 4.5%.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
Missouri conforms to federal standard deduction amounts:
- Single: $15,750
- MFJ: $31,500
- Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemption: Missouri does not allow a separate personal exemption (follows TCJA)
- Missouri starts from federal AGI and applies the federal standard deduction (or Missouri itemized deductions)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Federal income tax deduction** — Missouri allows a deduction for federal income taxes paid, capped at $5,000 (Single) / $10,000 (MFJ)
2. **Pension/Social Security exemption** — Public pension income and Social Security subtraction for qualifying filers (income-limited)
3. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $8,000 ($16,000 MFJ) for contributions to MOST (Missouri's 529 Savings Plan)
4. **Property tax credit** — Refundable credit for qualifying renters/homeowners (age 65+ or disabled; income under $30,000 Single / $34,000 MFJ)
5. **Earned income credit** — Missouri does not have a state EITC (as of 2025; legislation has been proposed)
6. **Health insurance deduction** — Self-employed health insurance premiums are deductible
7. **Military income deduction** — Active-duty military pay earned outside Missouri is deductible
8. **Historic preservation credit** — 25% of qualified rehabilitation expenditures (capped)
9. **Neighborhood assistance credit** — 50-70% credit for contributions to qualifying nonprofits
10. **Champion for Children credit** — 50% credit for contributions to maternity homes and pregnancy resource centers

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: MO-1040 (all individual filers)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file MO-60
- E-file options: Missouri DOR online portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $100 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, SB 807)
- Election deadline: Made on or before the due date of the entity's return (March 15 for calendar-year entities, plus extensions)
- Rate: Top rate of 4.95% (matches individual top rate)
- Mechanics: Electing entities pay tax at entity level on electing members' share of Missouri income. Members receive a credit on MO-1040. Available for S-corps, partnerships, and LLCs.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- **Kansas City**: 1% earnings tax on wages earned in KC and on net profits of KC businesses. Resident and nonresident workers pay. Renewed by voter approval periodically.
- **St. Louis City**: 1% earnings tax on wages and net profits. Similar structure to Kansas City.
- These are the **only two** local income taxes in Missouri
- Both require separate city filings (not included on the state return)
- Both cities also levy local sales taxes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Federal tax deduction is capped** — The $5,000/$10,000 cap limits the benefit for high earners; still a valuable deduction but less impactful than Alabama's uncapped version
2. **Extremely low bracket thresholds** — The top 4.95% rate kicks in at just $8,449, meaning virtually all income above the standard deduction is taxed at the top rate
3. **Kansas City and St. Louis earnings taxes** — The 1% local earnings tax in both cities is significant and requires separate filing. Easily overlooked by new residents.
4. **Rate decreasing via triggers** — Missouri's top rate is declining; always verify the current year rate
5. **Social Security subtraction has income limits** — Missouri's SS subtraction is income-limited; high earners get less benefit
6. **Same brackets for all filing statuses** — MFJ filers use the same brackets as Single filers, creating a slight marriage penalty

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Subtracted from Missouri income if Missouri AGI is below $85,000 (Single) / $100,000 (MFJ); partial subtraction in phaseout range; fully taxable above thresholds
- **Public pensions (MOSERS, PSRS, etc.)**: Fully exempt from Missouri income tax
- **Federal pensions**: Partially exempt; may qualify for pension exemption
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt from Missouri income tax
- **Private pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Taxable as ordinary income (no special exclusion beyond the general pension exemption for public plans)
- **Missouri is moderately retirement-friendly** — Public pensions and military pensions exempt; SS subtraction for lower-income filers; low overall rates
