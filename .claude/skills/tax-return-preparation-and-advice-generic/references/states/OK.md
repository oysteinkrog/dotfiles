# Oklahoma Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.75%
- Filing requirement threshold: Current thresholds track the federal filing-threshold framework; verify current Oklahoma instructions for exact status-specific numbers

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
### Single
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 0.25% | $0 - $1,000 |
| 0.75% | $1,001 - $2,500 |
| 1.75% | $2,501 - $3,750 |
| 2.75% | $3,751 - $4,900 |
| 3.75% | $4,901 - $7,200 |
| 4.75% | Over $7,200 |

### MFJ
| Rate | Bracket |
|------|---------|
| 0.25% | $0 - $2,000 |
| 0.75% | $2,001 - $5,000 |
| 1.75% | $5,001 - $7,500 |
| 2.75% | $7,501 - $9,800 |
| 3.75% | $9,801 - $12,200 |
| 4.75% | Over $12,200 |

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: Oklahoma follows federal amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption: $1,000 per exemption (taxpayer, spouse, dependents)
- Oklahoma allows itemized deductions (generally follows federal with modifications)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Military retirement exemption**: 100% of military retirement pay is exempt from Oklahoma income tax
2. **529 Plan deduction**: Up to $10,000 ($20,000 MFJ) for contributions to Oklahoma 529 plan
3. **Child tax credit**: $100 per dependent child
4. **Earned Income Credit**: 5% of federal EITC (non-refundable)
5. **Property tax credit**: Up to $200 for homeowners with income < $12,000
6. **Volunteer firefighter credit**: $200 credit for qualifying volunteer firefighters
7. **Adoption credit**: Up to $2,500 per child
8. **Charitable contribution enhancement**: For qualifying donations above $1,000 to Oklahoma charities
9. **Oklahoma investment/new jobs credit**: For qualifying business investments
10. **Retirement income exclusion**: Up to $10,000 per person for qualifying retirement distributions (separate from military)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 511 (resident), Form 511NR (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension; must pay estimated tax
- Oklahoma starts with federal AGI and applies Oklahoma adjustments

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $1,000
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: OW-8-ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (Oklahoma Pass-Through Entity Tax Act, effective 2019+)
- Election deadline: Made on entity's return; annually elected
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays tax at 4.75% on Oklahoma-source income allocated to electing members; members receive credit
- Applies to S corps, partnerships, LLCs

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in Oklahoma
- Revenue is primarily from state income tax, sales tax, and oil/gas production taxes

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Military retirement fully exempt**: Oklahoma is one of the most military-friendly states; 100% exemption with no income cap
2. **Oil and gas production**: Gross production tax (GPT) at 2% (first 3 years for new wells) and 5% thereafter; this is separate from income tax
3. **Oklahoma starts with federal AGI**: Many federal adjustments flow through; check for specific Oklahoma additions/subtractions
4. **Itemized deductions allowed**: Unlike some states, Oklahoma allows itemized deductions (generally following federal, with some modifications including no state income tax deduction)
5. **Tribal considerations**: Oklahoma has significant tribal populations; income earned on tribal land by tribal members may have special tax treatment

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Oklahoma income tax
- **Military retirement**: 100% exempt — no income cap, no age requirement
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Up to $10,000 exclusion per person for qualifying retirement benefits (includes federal civil service, state/local government, and private pensions)
- **Railroad Retirement**: Tier I exempt; Tier II generally qualifies for retirement exclusion
- **Oklahoma state pensions (OPERS/TRS)**: Qualify for the $10,000 retirement income exclusion
