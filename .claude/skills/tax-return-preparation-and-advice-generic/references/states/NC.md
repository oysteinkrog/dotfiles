# North Carolina Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 4.25% (flat rate)
- Filing requirement threshold: $12,750 (Single/MFS), $25,500 (MFJ/QSS), $19,125 (HOH)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
North Carolina has a **flat income tax rate**:
- **4.25%** on all taxable income (2025)
- Rate has been decreasing: was 5.25% (2022), 4.75% (2023), 4.5% (2024), and 4.25% (2025)
- Scheduled to decrease further: 3.99% by 2026, and potentially lower in subsequent years

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction (2025):
  - Single: $12,750
  - MFJ: $25,500
  - HOH: $19,125
  - MFS: $12,750
- **North Carolina does NOT allow itemized deductions** (standard deduction only)
- No personal exemptions (NC eliminated these)
- Child deduction: $2,500 per qualifying child ($2,000 if AGI >$40K Single / >$60K MFJ)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **No itemized deductions**: NC is standard deduction only; no mortgage interest, charitable, or SALT deduction on state return
2. **Child tax credit**: $125 per child (income phased)
3. **Credit for children with disabilities**: $3,500 per qualifying child
4. **NC Earned Income Tax Credit**: Not available (NC repealed its state EITC)
5. **529 Plan deduction**: NC does not offer a state deduction for 529 contributions
6. **Tax credit for qualified business investments (Article 3J)**: Various credits for job creation
7. **Historic rehabilitation credit**: 15-25% of qualified expenditures
8. **Charitable contribution deduction**: NOT available (no itemized deductions)
9. **Bailey Settlement (government pensions)**: Vested government retirees (pre-8/12/1989) fully exempt

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form D-400 (individual return), D-400 Schedule S (additions/deductions)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) if federal extension is filed; must pay estimated tax
- e-File: Available and encouraged

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: NC-40

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (NC PTET enacted 2022, effective for 2022+)
- Election deadline: Made on the entity's timely filed return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays NC income tax at 4.25% on distributive shares; members receive credit on individual returns
- Applies to S corporations and partnerships

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in North Carolina
- Local sales taxes vary by county (combined state + local: 6.75% - 7.5%)

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No itemized deductions at all**: This is a major gotcha. Taxpayers with large mortgage interest, charitable gifts, or medical expenses get NO state benefit
2. **Declining rate schedule**: The rate is legislatively scheduled to decrease; always verify the current year rate
3. **Bailey Settlement**: Government retirees who vested before August 12, 1989 receive full exemption of their retirement benefits
4. **NC additions to federal income**: NC requires adding back any bonus depreciation exceeding pre-TCJA amounts and other federal deductions not recognized by NC
5. **S Corp built-in gains**: NC does not conform to the federal 5-year recognition period for S Corp BIG tax

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from NC income tax
- **Bailey Settlement pensions**: Government employees vested by 8/12/1989 receive full exemption
- **Other pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Fully taxable at 4.25% (no exclusion for non-Bailey retirees)
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no special exclusion in NC)
- **$4,000 deduction for government retirees**: NC allows a $4,000 deduction for qualifying state/local government retirees not covered by Bailey
