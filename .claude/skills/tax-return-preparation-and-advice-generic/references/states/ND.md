# North Dakota Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 2.5%
- Filing requirement threshold: Must file if required to file federal return and had ND income

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
North Dakota has among the lowest income tax rates in the nation.

### Single and MFJ (same rates, different brackets)
| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|------|--------|-----|
| 0% | $0 - $44,725 | $0 - $89,450 |
| 1.95% | $44,726 - $225,975 | $89,451 - $225,975 |
| 2.5% | Over $225,975 | Over $225,975 |

Note: North Dakota simplified its brackets significantly in 2023 (HB 1158), eliminating the lowest brackets and creating a near-flat structure. The 0% bracket effectively means most moderate-income filers owe nothing.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Standard deduction: North Dakota follows federal standard deduction amounts
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - HOH: $23,625
- Personal exemption: None (follows federal TCJA elimination)
- ND starts with federal taxable income as the base

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Renaissance zone credits**: Tax exemption for income earned in designated renaissance zones
2. **Endowment tax credit**: 40% credit for gifts to qualified ND endowments (up to $25,000 credit per year)
3. **Workforce recruitment credit**: For qualifying new employees relocating to ND
4. **Angel fund investment credit**: 45% credit for investment in ND angel funds
5. **Automated external defibrillator credit**: $3 per resident credit for qualifying purchases
6. **Biodiesel/green diesel blending credit**: For qualifying fuel blending operations
7. **Homestead property tax credit**: For low-income elderly/disabled homeowners

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form ND-1 (resident), Form ND-1NR (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension (October 15) with federal extension
- ND starts with federal taxable income; adjustments are minimal
- e-File: Available

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax
- Form: ND-1ES

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes
- Election deadline: Made annually on entity's return
- Rate/Mechanics: Entity pays ND income tax at applicable rates on distributive shares; owners claim credit on individual returns
- Applies to qualifying pass-through entities

## Local/City Income Taxes
- No local income taxes in North Dakota
- Local sales taxes and property taxes are the primary local revenue sources

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Very low rates / effective 0% bracket**: Many ND taxpayers owe zero state income tax due to the $44,725 (Single) / $89,450 (MFJ) zero-rate bracket
2. **Federal taxable income starting point**: ND begins with federal taxable income, so federal deductions flow through automatically
3. **Oil and gas state**: Significant oil extraction and production taxes, but these are at the business level (11.5% extraction tax + 5% production tax)
4. **Renaissance zones**: Income earned in designated zones can be fully exempt for up to 5 years; verify zone status
5. **Farm income averaging**: ND allows income averaging for farmers similar to federal rules

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Exempt from ND income tax (excluded at federal level and ND starts with federal taxable income; plus ND provides full subtraction for any amount included)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Generally taxable to the extent included in federal taxable income; no special ND exclusion
- **Military retirement**: Taxable (no specific exclusion, though the high zero-bracket shelters much of it)
- **Railroad Retirement Tier I**: Exempt (follows federal treatment)
- **Federal/state government pensions**: Taxable, but the effectively low rates minimize impact
