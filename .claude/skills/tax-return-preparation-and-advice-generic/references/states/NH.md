# New Hampshire Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Limited (interest and dividends only, being phased out)
- Top marginal rate: 3% on interest/dividends (2024); phaseout reduces to 0% effective January 1, 2025
- Filing requirement threshold: $2,400 (Single), $4,800 (MFJ) in interest/dividend income (if tax still applies)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
New Hampshire has NO tax on wages, salaries, or earned income. The Interest & Dividends Tax has been phased out:
- 2023: 4%
- 2024: 3%
- **2025: 0% (fully repealed effective January 1, 2025)**

For 2025 tax year and forward, New Hampshire has NO personal income tax of any kind.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- When the I&D tax was in effect: $2,400 exemption (Single), $4,800 (MFJ)
- Additional exemptions for age 65+, blind, disabled
- Now moot for 2025+ as tax is repealed

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Business Profits Tax (BPT)**: 7.5% on business income over $92,000 (applies to businesses, not wage earners)
2. **Business Enterprise Tax (BET)**: 0.55% on enterprise value tax base (compensation + interest + dividends paid)
3. **BET credit against BPT**: BET paid can be credited against BPT liability
4. **Education Tax Credit**: Business tax credit for contributions to scholarship organizations
5. **Research & Development credit**: Against BPT
6. **Community Development Finance Authority credit**: For qualifying investments
7. **Coos County job creation credit**: For businesses creating jobs in Coos County

## Filing Requirements
- **Individual**: No income tax return required for 2025+ (I&D tax repealed)
- **Business**: Form NH-1040 (BPT return) for businesses with gross income > $92,000
- **BET return**: Form BET due April 15
- Extension: 7-month automatic extension available for business returns

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Individual: No longer applicable (I&D tax repealed)
- Business (BPT/BET):
  - Required when: Tax liability expected to exceed $200
  - Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, December 15
  - Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year or 90% of current year

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Not applicable in the traditional SALT workaround sense (no individual income tax)
- Pass-through entities are subject to BPT at 7.5% and BET at 0.55% directly

## Local/City Income Taxes
- None. No municipality in New Hampshire levies an income tax
- Property taxes are the primary local revenue source and are among the highest in the nation

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No income tax does NOT mean no business tax**: Self-employed individuals, LLCs, and partnerships with >$92,000 gross income owe BPT at 7.5%
2. **High property taxes**: With no income or sales tax, NH relies heavily on property taxes (effective rates among highest nationally, ~1.5-2.5%)
3. **No sales tax**: New Hampshire has no general sales tax
4. **BPT applies to sole proprietors**: A self-employed individual with NH-source business income over $92,000 owes BPT
5. **Meals and rooms tax**: 8.5% tax on restaurant meals, hotel rooms, and car rentals (this is NOT a general sales tax)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Not taxed (no income tax on earned or retirement income; I&D distributions would have been taxed prior to 2025 but no longer)
- **Military retirement**: Not taxed
- **Interest/dividends from retirement accounts**: Not taxed for 2025+ (I&D tax repealed)

## Business Tax Details
### Business Profits Tax (BPT)
- Rate: 7.5% (2025)
- Applies to: Business organizations with gross income > $92,000
- Base: Net income from business activity attributable to NH
- Filing: Form NH-1040

### Business Enterprise Tax (BET)
- Rate: 0.55% (2025)
- Base: Sum of compensation, interest, and dividends paid, after adjustments
- Filing threshold: Enterprise value tax base > $281,000 or gross receipts > $281,000
- BET credit: 100% of BET paid is creditable against BPT (but not vice versa)
