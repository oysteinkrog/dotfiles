# Maine Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 7.15%
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds current federal-style filing thresholds (about $15,750 Single for 2025) or other filing-status thresholds
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Maine Revenue Services (MRS)

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
| Maine Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $26,050 | 5.8% |
| $26,051 - $61,600 | 6.75% |
| Over $61,600 | 7.15% |

### Married Filing Jointly
| Maine Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 - $52,100 | 5.8% |
| $52,101 - $123,250 | 6.75% |
| Over $123,250 | 7.15% |

Brackets are indexed annually for inflation.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Maine conforms to federal standard deduction amounts:
  - Single: $15,750
  - MFJ: $31,500
  - Head of Household: $23,625
- Personal exemption: $4,700 per person (Maine retained personal exemptions after TCJA)
- Dependent exemption: $4,700 per dependent
- Phaseout: Personal exemption phases out at higher income levels (beginning at ~$160,000 Single / $320,000 MFJ)

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Property Tax Fairness Credit** — Refundable credit for property taxes or rent paid exceeding 6% of income (max $1,000 for under 65; $1,500 for 65+). Income limits: $52,000 (Single) / $78,000 (MFJ).
2. **Maine EITC** — 12% of federal EITC (refundable)
3. **Child care credit** — 25% of federal child and dependent care credit (nonrefundable)
4. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $1,000 ($2,000 MFJ) for contributions to NextGen 529 plan
5. **Pension income deduction** — Up to $35,000 per person for qualifying pension income (reduced by Social Security received)
6. **Student loan interest deduction** — Follows federal $2,500 limit
7. **Maine Capital Investment Credit** — For qualifying business property investments
8. **Volunteer firefighter/EMS credit** — $100 per year for qualifying volunteers
9. **Clean energy credit** — For qualifying energy-efficient improvements

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 1040ME (all individual filers — resident, nonresident, part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed; otherwise file Form 1040ME-EXT
- E-file options: Maine FastFile, Maine Revenue Services portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior-year Maine AGI exceeds $150,000)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, LD 1data)
- Election deadline: Made on or before the original due date of the entity's return
- Rate: 7.15% on all taxable income (single rate, not progressive)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level. Members receive a refundable credit on their Maine return. The credit is refundable, which is more favorable than some states. Available for S-corps, partnerships, and LLCs.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Maine does not have local/city income taxes
- Municipalities rely on property taxes (Maine has relatively high property taxes)
- State sales tax is 5.5%; no local sales taxes are permitted

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Property Tax Fairness Credit is valuable** — This refundable credit can provide significant relief; many eligible taxpayers fail to claim it. Track property tax or rent paid carefully.
2. **Pension deduction is offset by Social Security** — The $35,000 pension deduction is reduced dollar-for-dollar by Social Security benefits received. If SS benefits exceed $35,000, the pension deduction is zero.
3. **Relatively high rates** — Maine's 7.15% top rate applies at a relatively low threshold ($61,600 Single), making it one of the higher-burden states for middle-income earners
4. **Maine retained personal exemptions** — Unlike the federal return, Maine still allows personal exemptions, providing additional relief
5. **No sales tax on groceries** — Maine does not tax food for home consumption, unlike some neighboring states

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Exempt for most filers; may be partially taxable at very high income levels (follows a modified federal calculation)
- **Pensions**: Up to $35,000 deduction per person, but reduced by Social Security benefits received
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Qualify for the $35,000 pension deduction (reduced by SS)
- **Military pensions**: Fully exempt from Maine income tax (separate from the $35,000 deduction)
- **Maine is moderately retirement-friendly** for those with limited Social Security (maximizing the pension deduction)
