# Washington Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: No (but 7% capital gains tax on long-term gains above the $278,000 2025 standard deduction amount)
- Top marginal rate: N/A for income tax; 7% capital gains tax
- Filing requirement threshold: N/A for income tax; capital gains return generally required if adjusted long-term gains exceed the $278,000 2025 standard deduction amount

## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Washington imposes no personal income tax on wages, salaries, interest, dividends, or business income.

**However**, Washington enacted a 7% tax on adjusted long-term capital gains exceeding the annual standard deduction amount. For tax year 2025, that amount is $278,000 (effective 2022, upheld by the Washington Supreme Court as an excise tax in March 2023).

### Capital Gains Tax
- **Rate**: 7% on adjusted long-term capital gains exceeding $278,000
- **Threshold**: $278,000 for tax year 2025 (indexed annually for inflation)
- **Filing status**: Individual threshold; does not double for MFJ
- **Exclusions**: Real estate sales, retirement account distributions, livestock/timber/fishing, sole proprietor goodwill (certain conditions)

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- N/A (no income tax)
- Capital gains tax: $278,000 standard deduction from long-term gains for tax year 2025 (effectively an exemption threshold)
- Charitable donations deduction: Available only after the $278,000 threshold is exceeded, and capped at $111,000 for tax year 2025

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
- N/A for income tax purposes
- **Capital gains tax charitable deduction**: Once the threshold is met, qualifying charitable donations can reduce taxable gains, but the 2025 deduction cap is $111,000
- **B&O Tax**: Business & Occupation tax (see Business section)
- No estate tax up to $2,193,000 (WA estate tax threshold, separate from federal)
- **Working Families Tax Credit**: Refundable credit for low-income WA residents (based on federal EITC eligibility); up to $1,255 depending on family size

## Filing Requirements
- No individual income tax return required
- **Capital gains excise tax return**: Generally due April 15 if gains exceed the annual standard deduction amount; Washington extended the 2025 return/payment deadline to May 1, 2026
- Businesses must file B&O tax returns (monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on revenue)

## Estimated Tax Payments
- N/A for income tax
- Capital gains tax: Paid with annual return (April 15); no quarterly estimated payments currently required
- B&O tax: Depends on filing frequency (monthly/quarterly/annual)

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Not applicable in the traditional SALT workaround sense (no individual income tax)
- The capital gains tax does not have a PTET election mechanism

## Local/City Income Taxes
- None. No Washington municipality levies an income tax
- Seattle has proposed income tax ordinances but none have survived legal challenges

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Capital gains tax is NOT an "income tax"**: WA Supreme Court upheld it as an excise tax on the sale of capital assets, not an income tax (which would be unconstitutional under WA law). This distinction matters legally
2. **Capital gains threshold does NOT double for MFJ**: The $278,000 threshold for 2025 applies per return, not per person. MFJ filers get the same threshold as single filers
3. **Real estate exempt**: Gains from sale of real property are excluded from the capital gains tax (huge exclusion)
4. **Retirement distributions exempt**: 401(k), IRA, pension distributions are not subject to the capital gains tax even if they include embedded gains
5. **High sales tax**: Combined state + local sales tax ranges from 7.0% to 10.6% (one of the highest in the nation)

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- **Pensions/401(k)/IRA**: Not taxed (no income tax; also excluded from capital gains tax)
- **Military retirement**: Not taxed (no income tax)
- Washington is favorable for retirees (but investment sales that push adjusted long-term gains above the annual deduction amount are taxed at 7%)

## Business Tax Considerations
### Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax
- **Major state business tax**: Gross receipts tax (no deductions for expenses)
- **Rates vary by classification**:
  - Retailing: 0.471%
  - Wholesaling: 0.484%
  - Manufacturing: 0.484%
  - Service: 1.5% (significantly higher)
  - Technology/R&D: 0.75% (preferential rate)
- **No deduction for COGS or expenses**: Tax is on gross receipts, not net income
- **Small business credit**: Reduces B&O tax for businesses with less than $125,000/month in gross receipts
- **Nexus**: Physical presence or economic nexus (>$100K receipts or 200+ transactions)

### Other Business Taxes
- **Sales and Use Tax**: 6.5% state; local additions up to ~4.1%
- **Real Estate Excise Tax (REET)**: 1.1% to 3% on sales of real property (graduated rates)

## Estate Tax
- Washington is one of few states with its own estate tax
- Exemption: $2,193,000 (2025, indexed)
- Rates: 10% to 20% graduated
- Applies to Washington residents' worldwide assets and non-residents' WA-situs property
- Estate tax return due 9 months after death

## Property Tax
- Property taxes are moderate to high depending on location
- Effective rates: 0.8% to 1.2% typically
- 1% annual increase limit on total levy (with exceptions for voter-approved measures)
- Senior/disabled exemption programs available
