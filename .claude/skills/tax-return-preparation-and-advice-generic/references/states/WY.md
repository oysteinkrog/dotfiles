## Income Tax Rates (2025)
Wyoming imposes no personal income tax and no corporate income tax. Wyoming's Constitution does not prohibit an income tax, but the legislature has never enacted one, and the political environment makes enactment extremely unlikely.

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **No income tax of any kind**: Wyoming has no personal income tax, no corporate income tax, and no gross receipts tax on businesses (beyond severance taxes)
2. **Mineral severance tax is the backbone**: Wyoming funds state government largely through mineral extraction taxes (oil, gas, coal, trona) and federal mineral royalties
3. **Low sales tax**: 4% state rate; combined with local rates up to 6%. Lower than most states
4. **No estate or inheritance tax**: Combined with no income tax, Wyoming is one of the most tax-friendly states overall
5. **Very business-friendly**: Wyoming is often ranked #1 for business tax climate (Tax Foundation) due to the absence of income, franchise, and gross receipts taxes

## Business Tax Considerations
### No Business Income Tax
- Wyoming has no corporate income tax
- No franchise tax
- No gross receipts tax
- Sole proprietors, LLCs, partnerships, S corps, and C corps all operate free of state income taxation

### Mineral Severance Taxes
- **Major revenue source** for the state
- Crude oil: 6% of fair market value
- Natural gas: 6% of fair market value
- Coal (surface): 7% of fair market value
- Coal (underground): 3.75% of fair market value
- Trona: 4% of fair market value
- Uranium: 4% of fair market value
- Other minerals: Various rates

### Other Taxes
- **Sales tax**: 4% state; local additions up to 2% (combined max ~6%)
- **Property tax**: Moderate; minerals and industrial property assessed at higher rates than residential
- **Lodging tax**: Up to 4% local option

## Property Tax
- Property taxes exist and are the primary local revenue source
- Effective residential rate: approximately 0.5-0.6% (below national average)
- Minerals and industrial property assessed at 100% of fair market value (vs 9.5% for residential)
- Agricultural land assessed at productive value

## LLC and Entity Advantages
- Wyoming pioneered the LLC structure (first state to enact LLC legislation, 1977)
- No franchise tax or annual report fee for LLCs (minimal $60 annual report fee)
- Strong charging order protection for single-member LLCs
- Privacy-friendly: no public disclosure of member/manager names in formation documents
- No requirement for operating agreements to be filed with the state
- Popular jurisdiction for forming LLCs and holding companies

## Trust Advantages
- No income tax on trust income
- Dynasty trusts permitted (1,000-year duration)
- Directed trust statute
- Asset protection trust provisions
- Privacy protections for trust information
- Similar advantages to South Dakota but with even lower costs
