# Georgia Tax Guide

## Overview
- Income tax: Yes
- Top marginal rate: 5.19% (flat rate for tax year 2025 under Georgia's scheduled rate reductions)
- Filing requirement threshold: Gross income exceeds Georgia standard deduction + personal exemptions for filing status
- Fiscal year: Calendar year
- Administering agency: Georgia Department of Revenue

## Income Tax Rates (2025)

### Single
Georgia is transitioning from progressive brackets to a flat-rate system under HB 1015 (2022):

**2025 Rate**: 5.19% flat rate on Georgia taxable income

### Married Filing Jointly
**2025 Rate**: 5.19% flat rate (same as single)

The rate is scheduled to decrease incrementally toward 4.99% by 2029, contingent on revenue triggers.

## Standard Deduction / Personal Exemption
- Single: $12,000 standard deduction
- MFJ: $24,000 standard deduction
- Married Filing Separately: $12,000 standard deduction
- Head of Household: $12,000 standard deduction
- Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $12,000 standard deduction
- Dependent exemption: $4,000 per dependent

## Key State-Specific Deductions and Credits
1. **Retirement income exclusion** — Up to $65,000 per person (age 62+) or $35,000 (under 62 if disabled or military retirement)
2. **529 Plan deduction** — Up to $8,000 per beneficiary ($16,000 MFJ) for Path2College contributions
3. **Low-income credit** — Based on Georgia taxable net income; reduces tax for low earners
4. **Child and dependent care credit** — 30% of federal credit
5. **Disaster contribution credit** — For contributions to qualified disaster relief funds
6. **Rural hospital tax credit** — Up to $5,000 ($10,000 MFJ) for contributions to qualifying rural hospitals
7. **Qualified education tax credit** — Dollar-for-dollar credit up to $1,000 ($2,500 MFJ) for donations to Student Scholarship Organizations (SSOs)
8. **Film/entertainment credit** — Georgia's famous film tax credit (primarily for businesses, but can affect PTE members)

## Filing Requirements
- Forms: Form 500 (resident), Form 500 with Schedule 3 (nonresident/part-year)
- Due date: April 15
- Extension: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15 if federal extension is filed
- E-file options: Georgia Tax Center (GTC) portal, approved commercial software

## Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when: Expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding and credits
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year tax

## Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)
- Available: Yes (enacted 2022, HB 149)
- Election deadline: Made on or before the due date of the entity's return (including extensions)
- Rate: 5.19% (same as individual rate, decreasing as the flat rate decreases)
- Mechanics: Electing PTEs pay tax at entity level on members' share of Georgia income; members receive an offsetting credit on their Georgia Form 500. Available for S-corps, partnerships, and LLCs taxed as partnerships.

## Local/City Income Taxes
- Georgia does not have local/city income taxes
- Local governments rely on property taxes and Local Option Sales Tax (LOST)
- Combined state + local sales tax ranges from 7% to 9% depending on county
- Education Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (ESPLOST) is common

## Special Features and Gotchas
1. **Flat rate transition in progress** — Georgia is mid-transition from progressive brackets to a flat rate. The 2025 rate is 5.19%, and future reductions still depend on the statutory schedule.
2. **Generous retirement exclusion** — The $65,000 per person (age 62+) exclusion is among the most generous in the US, making Georgia attractive for retirees
3. **Low old bracket thresholds** — Under the old progressive system, the top rate kicked in at just $7,000 (Single), making most income taxed at the top rate anyway
4. **Qualified education credit** — Dollar-for-dollar credit for SSO donations; similar to Arizona's STO credit. Funding caps may apply.
5. **Film industry impact** — Georgia's film tax credits attract significant entertainment industry activity; PTE members in film ventures may see these credits flow through

## Retirement Income Treatment
- **Social Security**: Fully exempt from Georgia income tax
- **Pensions (public and private)**: Up to $65,000 exclusion per person if age 62+ (or totally disabled)
- **Military pensions**: Up to $35,000 exclusion (under 62); $65,000 (age 62+)
- **401(k)/IRA distributions**: Included in the $65,000 retirement income exclusion
- **Under 62 (not disabled)**: No general retirement income exclusion (except military)
- **Georgia is very retirement-friendly** for those 62 and older
