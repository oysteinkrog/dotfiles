# Divorce -- Life Event Guide (TY 2025)

## 1. Why Divorce Needs Its Own Event Playbook

Divorce is not just a filing-status change. It is a forced reallocation of cash flow, children,
housing, retirement assets, basis, future deductions, and legal liability. The tax damage usually
comes from sequencing errors:
- signing a settlement before understanding basis,
- dividing retirement accounts without the correct transfer mechanics,
- losing Head of Household or dependency benefits by accident,
- mishandling the marital home,
- inheriting hidden tax liabilities on a joint return,
- forgetting to reset withholding and estimated taxes immediately.

This guide is the operational timeline. For the deep technical reference, use
[DIVORCE-TAX-PLANNING.md](../situations/DIVORCE-TAX-PLANNING.md).

## 2. First 30 Days: Triage Questions

### Filing Status
- Is the divorce final by December 31?
- If not final, is MFJ still economically superior and is there enough trust to file jointly?
- Does either spouse qualify as "considered unmarried" for Head of Household?

### Cash Flow
- Who will receive wages, bonuses, RSUs, or business income for the rest of the year?
- Are withholding and estimated taxes now wrong because the household split?

### Children
- Where do the children actually sleep?
- Who will claim Head of Household, CTC, EITC, dependent care credit, and Form 8332 releases?

### Assets
- Are there low-basis securities, concentrated stock, private-company shares, or business
  interests that look equal at FMV but are not equal after tax?

### Liability
- Is there concern about hidden income, bad deductions, payroll issues, or unpaid tax debt on a
  joint return?

---

## 3. The Settlement Should Not Be Signed Until These Tax Questions Are Modeled

### Property Division Must Be Done on an After-Tax Basis
- A dollar of cash is not the same as a dollar of low-basis stock.
- A home with gain, depreciation, or state-tax issues is not the same as a checking account.
- Business interests need basis, debt, and pass-through exposure modeled.

### Retirement Transfers Must Use the Right Mechanism
- Employer plans usually need a QDRO.
- IRAs use transfer-incident-to-divorce rules, not a QDRO.
- A cash withdrawal to "even things up later" can create tax and penalty where none was needed.

### Alimony / Support Language
- Pre-2019 vs post-2018 treatment matters for old agreements and modifications.
- Child-support contingencies can change characterization.
- Never assume family-law language automatically matches tax-law language.

### Home Strategy
- Sell now, hold jointly for a period, transfer to one spouse, or nest for the children?
- The answer changes Section 121 exclusion availability, cash needs, and carry costs.

## 4. The Year-of-Divorce Timeline

### Before the Decree Is Final
- Model MFJ vs MFS vs HoH where possible.
- Review prior-year AGI, carryforwards, and dependency patterns.
- Freeze a source-of-truth ledger for all major assets and liabilities.

### Around the Decree
- Coordinate QDRO drafting and IRA transfer language.
- Update payroll withholding, direct deposit, and estimated tax projections.
- Document custody and support terms with tax consequences in mind.

### After the Decree
- Change beneficiaries where legally permitted.
- Update address, name, filing status assumptions, and state residency facts.
- Rebuild household-level deductions and credits from the ground up; do not assume last year's
  pattern still applies.

---

## 5. The Highest-EV Areas To Get Right

### Filing Status and Dependency
- Head of Household is often the most valuable single post-separation filing-status question.
- Form 8332 releases only some benefits, not all child-related benefits.
- The custody calendar matters more than intentions or verbal agreements.

### Retirement Accounts
- Alternate payee treatment, early-distribution exceptions, and rollover mechanics should be
  decided before paperwork is signed.
- A mishandled account division can permanently destroy tax-deferred status.

### Marital Home
- Determine basis, improvement history, and anticipated gain before assigning the property.
- The home-sale exclusion is time-sensitive when one spouse moves out.

### Business Ownership
- Closely held businesses create valuation, basis, compensation, and control issues.
- One spouse may receive an illiquid asset with hidden tax and litigation risk.

### Joint Return Exposure
- Consider innocent-spouse or separation-of-liability angles early when there is distrust.

---

## 6. Common Mistakes

1. Treating property division as equal because FMV is equal.
2. Filing MFJ while separating without evaluating liability risk.
3. Using a distribution instead of a tax-free transfer method for retirement assets.
4. Forgetting to update withholding after support, custody, and wage patterns change.
5. Losing Head of Household because facts were not documented.
6. Assuming the divorce decree alone controls dependency claims without Form 8332 where needed.
7. Ignoring the tax consequences of the home sale until after closing.
8. Signing a settlement before modeling business, stock, and basis issues.

---

## 7. Document Checklist

- Draft and final decree / separation agreement.
- Custody calendar or parenting schedule.
- Prior 3 years of returns.
- Payroll records and projected year-end pay.
- Retirement-account statements and plan documents.
- Brokerage statements with cost basis.
- Home closing statements, refinance docs, and improvement records.
- Business tax returns, K-1s, basis schedules, and valuation reports.

---

## 8. Tactical Output the Skill Should Produce

For an active divorce matter, the skill should generate:
- a filing-status comparison,
- a dependency and child-credit memo,
- an after-tax property-division spreadsheet,
- a retirement-transfer checklist,
- a home-sale timing memo,
- a withholding and estimated-tax reset plan.
