# Tax Credits -- Comprehensive Master Reference

## Overview

Tax credits are the most powerful tools in the Internal Revenue Code because they reduce tax liability **dollar for dollar**, unlike deductions which only reduce taxable income. A $1,000 credit saves $1,000 in tax regardless of bracket; a $1,000 deduction saves only $370 for someone in the 37% bracket and only $100 for someone in the 10% bracket.

Understanding which credits are available, how they interact, and how to maximize them is fundamental to effective tax planning.

All figures and rules reflect the 2025 tax year.

---

## Refundable vs. Non-Refundable Credits

### Non-Refundable Credits

Can reduce tax liability to **$0** but cannot generate a refund. Unused amounts may carry forward (depending on the specific credit) or are lost.

### Refundable Credits

Can reduce tax liability below $0, generating a **refund** to the taxpayer even if they owe no income tax. These are effectively government payments to qualifying individuals.

### Partially Refundable Credits

Some credits have a non-refundable component and a refundable component (e.g., Child Tax Credit: non-refundable up to $2,200, with a refundable "Additional Child Tax Credit" component up to $1,700).

| Credit | Type | Carryforward |
|---|---|---|
| Child Tax Credit (CTC) | Partially refundable | No |
| Earned Income Credit (EIC) | Fully refundable | No |
| American Opportunity Credit (AOC) | 40% refundable | No |
| Lifetime Learning Credit | Non-refundable | No |
| Child/Dependent Care Credit | Non-refundable | No |
| Adoption Credit | Non-refundable | 5 years forward |
| Saver's Credit | Non-refundable | No |
| Foreign Tax Credit | Non-refundable | 1 year back, 10 years forward |
| General Business Credit | Non-refundable | 1 year back, 20 years forward |
| Premium Tax Credit | Refundable | No (reconciled annually) |
| Residential Energy Credits | Non-refundable | Forward (section 25D); No (section 25C) |
| AMT Credit | Non-refundable | Indefinite forward |

## Family Credits

### Child Tax Credit (IRC Section 24)

**Credit amount:** $2,200 per qualifying child under age 17

**Qualifying child requirements:**
- Under age 17 at end of tax year
- US citizen, US national, or US resident alien
- Claimed as dependent on the return
- Lived with taxpayer for more than half the year
- Did not provide more than half of their own support
- Has a valid Social Security number (ITIN does NOT qualify for CTC; may qualify for $500 Other Dependents Credit)

**Phase-out:**
| Filing Status | Phase-out Begins | Phase-out Rate |
|---|---|---|
| MFJ | $400,000 | $50 per $1,000 over threshold |
| All other | $200,000 | $50 per $1,000 over threshold |

The credit is fully phased out at approximately $440,000 MFJ (for one child) -- add ~$44,000 per additional child.

**Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) -- Refundable Component:**
- Up to **$1,700** per child is refundable as the ACTC
- Calculated as 15% of earned income over $2,500
- Reported on Schedule 8812

**Other Dependents Credit:**
- $500 non-refundable credit for dependents who do not qualify for the CTC (children 17+, elderly parents, etc.)
- Same phase-out thresholds as CTC

### Child and Dependent Care Credit (IRC Section 21)

**Purpose:** Credit for expenses paid for care of a qualifying individual (child under 13, disabled spouse, disabled dependent) to enable the taxpayer to work.

**Credit rate:** 20% to 35% of qualifying expenses, depending on AGI:

| AGI | Credit Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to $15,000 | 35% |
| $15,001 - $17,000 | 34% |
| $17,001 - $19,000 | 33% |
| Each additional $2,000 | Decrease by 1% |
| $43,001+ | 20% (floor) |

**Maximum qualifying expenses:**
- $3,000 for 1 qualifying individual
- $6,000 for 2+ qualifying individuals

**Maximum credit:** $3,000 x 35% = $1,050 (1 child) or $6,000 x 35% = $2,100 (2+ children)

**Key rules:**
- Both spouses must have earned income (or one must be a full-time student or disabled)
- Payments to a relative under age 19 do not qualify
- Must report care provider's name, address, and TIN on return
- Payments made through a Dependent Care FSA reduce eligible expenses dollar-for-dollar
- Non-refundable

### Dependent Care FSA Interaction (IRC Section 129)

The Dependent Care FSA allows **$5,000** ($2,500 MFS) in pre-tax contributions for dependent care expenses.

**Coordination with section 21 credit:**
- FSA contributions reduce the maximum qualifying expenses for the section 21 credit
- For 2+ dependents: $6,000 max expenses - $5,000 FSA = $1,000 remaining for credit
- Credit on $1,000 at 20% = $200

**Which is better -- FSA or Credit?**

| Income Level | FSA Tax Savings | Credit Savings | Better Option |
|---|---|---|---|
| $40,000 (22% bracket) | $5,000 x 29.65% = $1,483 | $3,000 x 25% = $750 (1 child) | FSA |
| $80,000 (22% bracket) | $5,000 x 29.65% = $1,483 | $3,000 x 20% = $600 | FSA |
| $200,000 (32% bracket) | $5,000 x 39.65% = $1,983 | $3,000 x 20% = $600 | FSA |
| Very low income (10%) | $5,000 x 17.65% = $883 | $3,000 x 35% = $1,050 | Credit |

FSA is almost always better for middle and high-income taxpayers because it also avoids FICA (7.65%).

### Earned Income Credit (IRC Section 32)

**Purpose:** Refundable credit for low-to-moderate income workers, designed to incentivize work.

**2025 Maximum Credits and Thresholds (Estimated):**

| Children | Max Credit | Earned Income for Max | Phase-out Begins (Single) | Phase-out Begins (MFJ) | Complete Phase-out (Single) | Complete Phase-out (MFJ) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | $632 | $8,490 | $10,330 | $17,250 | $18,590 | $25,510 |
| 1 | $4,213 | $12,730 | $22,080 | $29,010 | $49,080 | $56,010 |
| 2 | $6,960 | $17,880 | $22,080 | $29,010 | $55,770 | $62,700 |
| 3+ | $7,830 | $17,880 | $22,080 | $29,010 | $59,900 | $66,830 |

**Key requirements:**
- Must have **earned income** (wages, self-employment income, union strike benefits)
- **Investment income** must not exceed ~$11,600 (2025 estimate)
- Must be US citizen or resident alien for entire year
- Must not be a qualifying child of another person
- Cannot file Form 2555 (Foreign Earned Income Exclusion)
- Valid SSN required for taxpayer, spouse (if MFJ), and each qualifying child

**Common errors triggering audit:**
- Claiming children who do not live with the taxpayer
- Overstating self-employment income to maximize credit (IRS uses statistical modeling to detect this)
- Filing as single/HoH when actually married

**Due diligence requirements (for preparers):** Paid preparers must complete Form 8867 documenting due diligence for every return claiming EIC. Penalty for failure: $560 per return (2025).

### Adoption Credit (IRC Section 23)

**Maximum credit:** $17,280 per child (2025 estimate, indexed for inflation)

**For domestic adoption:**
- Credit for qualified adoption expenses paid or incurred
- Expenses include: adoption fees, attorney fees, court costs, travel, other directly related expenses
- Claimed in the year the adoption becomes final (unless expenses paid in prior year for a domestic adoption -- those are claimed the year after payment)

**For foreign adoption:**
- All expenses claimed in the year the adoption becomes final

**For special needs adoption:**
- Full $17,280 credit regardless of actual expenses incurred (even if expenses are $0)
- "Special needs" determined by the state, typically children who are older, part of a sibling group, have disabilities, or are minority race

**Phase-out:** Begins at MAGI ~$259,190, complete at ~$299,190 (2025 estimates)

**Carryforward:** 5 years (non-refundable, so excess credit carries forward)

**Employer adoption assistance:** Up to $17,280 excluded from income (section 137), but reduces the adoption credit dollar-for-dollar for the same expenses. Different expenses can qualify separately for each benefit.

## Education Credits

### American Opportunity Tax Credit (IRC Section 25A(i))

**Credit amount:** 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 = **$2,500 maximum per student**

**Refundable portion:** 40% of the credit (up to **$1,000**) is refundable

**Requirements:**
- First 4 years of postsecondary education only
- Student must be enrolled at least half-time
- Student must be pursuing a degree or recognized credential
- Student must not have completed 4 years of postsecondary education
- Student must not have a felony drug conviction
- Cannot be claimed for more than 4 tax years per student

**Qualifying expenses:** Tuition, fees, course materials (books, supplies, equipment needed for course)

**NOT qualifying:** Room and board, transportation, insurance, non-academic fees, expenses paid with tax-free scholarships

**Phase-out (2025 estimates):**
| Filing Status | Phase-out Range |
|---|---|
| Single / HoH | $80,000 - $90,000 MAGI |
| MFJ | $160,000 - $180,000 MAGI |

**Cannot claim if:** MFS filing status, nonresident alien (unless electing to be treated as resident), or claiming the Lifetime Learning Credit for the same student

### Lifetime Learning Credit (IRC Section 25A(c))

**Credit amount:** 20% of up to $10,000 in qualifying expenses = **$2,000 maximum per return** (not per student)

**Requirements:**
- Any year of postsecondary education (undergraduate, graduate, professional, continuing education)
- No minimum enrollment requirement (even one course qualifies)
- No limit on number of years claimed
- Available for courses to acquire or improve job skills

**Phase-out (2025 estimates):**
| Filing Status | Phase-out Range |
|---|---|
| Single / HoH | $80,000 - $90,000 MAGI |
| MFJ | $160,000 - $180,000 MAGI |

**Cannot claim if:** MFS filing status

### AOC vs. LLC Decision Matrix

| Factor | AOC | LLC |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum credit | $2,500/student | $2,000/return |
| Per student or per return | Per student | Per return |
| Refundable? | 40% ($1,000) | No |
| Year limit | First 4 years | Unlimited |
| Enrollment | Half-time minimum | Any |
| Graduate school | No | Yes |
| Income phase-out | $80K-90K / $160K-180K | $80K-90K / $160K-180K |

**Strategy:** Claim AOC for the first 4 years, then switch to LLC for subsequent years (graduate school, professional development).

### Student Loan Interest Deduction (IRC Section 221)

**Not a credit but an above-the-line deduction** -- included here because of education tax planning interaction.

**Maximum deduction:** $2,500

**Phase-out (2025 estimates):**
| Filing Status | Phase-out Range |
|---|---|
| Single / HoH | $75,000 - $90,000 MAGI |
| MFJ | $155,000 - $185,000 MAGI |

**Cannot claim if:** MFS, claimed as dependent, or nonresident alien

**Qualifying interest:** Interest paid on qualified education loans (loans taken out solely to pay qualified higher education expenses)

### Saver's Credit (IRC Section 25B)

**Purpose:** Credit for low-to-moderate income taxpayers who contribute to retirement accounts.

**Credit rate:** 10%, 20%, or 50% of retirement contributions up to $2,000 ($4,000 MFJ)

| Filing Status | 50% Rate | 20% Rate | 10% Rate | No Credit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MFJ | AGI <= $47,500 | $47,501 - $51,000 | $51,001 - $78,750 | > $78,750 |
| HoH | AGI <= $35,625 | $35,626 - $38,250 | $38,251 - $59,063 | > $59,063 |
| Single/MFS | AGI <= $23,750 | $23,751 - $25,500 | $25,501 - $39,375 | > $39,375 |

**Maximum credit:** $2,000 MFJ ($1,000 single) at 50% rate

**Qualifying contributions:** IRA (Traditional or Roth), 401(k), 403(b), 457, SIMPLE, SEP, ABLE account

**Distributions reduce credit:** Distributions from retirement accounts within the 2-year period prior to the credit year, the credit year itself, and the testing period reduce the contribution amount dollar-for-dollar.

**Non-refundable:** Cannot generate a refund

## Business Credits (IRC Section 38 -- General Business Credit)

The General Business Credit is a collection of individual business credits, combined and subject to unified limitations.

### Overall Limitation (IRC Section 38(c))

The General Business Credit cannot exceed:

```
Net income tax + AMT - Greater of:
  (a) Tentative minimum tax, OR
  (b) 25% of net regular tax liability exceeding $25,000
```

Excess credits: **1 year back, 20 years forward** (section 39)

### R&D Credit (IRC Section 41)

See **R-AND-D-TAX-CREDIT.md** for comprehensive coverage. Summary:
- 20% regular credit or 14% ASC on qualified research expenses
- Startup payroll tax offset up to $500,000/year
- Heavily audited -- documentation critical

### Work Opportunity Tax Credit (IRC Section 51)

**Purpose:** Credit for hiring individuals from targeted groups facing barriers to employment.

**Credit amounts:**

| Target Group | Credit | Maximum Wages | Maximum Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Most targeted groups | 40% of first-year wages | $6,000 | $2,400 |
| Long-term family assistance recipient (Year 1) | 40% of first-year wages | $10,000 | $4,000 |
| Long-term family assistance recipient (Year 2) | 50% of second-year wages | $10,000 | $5,000 |
| Designated community resident (18-39, empowerment zone) | 40% of first-year wages | $6,000 | $2,400 |
| Veteran (unemployed 6+ months) | 40% of first-year wages | $14,000 | $5,600 |
| Disabled veteran (unemployed 6+ months) | 40% of first-year wages | $24,000 | $9,600 |
| Summer youth (16-17, empowerment zone) | 40% of wages | $3,000 | $1,200 |

**Targeted groups:**
1. TANF recipients
2. Veterans (various subcategories)
3. Ex-felons (within 1 year of conviction or release)
4. Designated community residents
5. Vocational rehabilitation referrals
6. Summer youth employees
7. SNAP (food stamp) recipients (18-39)
8. SSI recipients
9. Long-term unemployment recipients (27+ weeks)
10. Long-term family assistance recipients

**Filing requirement:** **Form 8850** must be filed with the state workforce agency within **28 days of the employee's start date**. This is a HARD deadline -- missing it forfeits the credit entirely.

**Minimum hours:** Employee must work at least 120 hours for 25% credit rate, 400+ hours for full 40% rate.

### Small Employer Health Insurance Credit (IRC Section 45R)

**Credit:** 50% of employer-paid premiums (25% for tax-exempt employers)

**Requirements:**
- Fewer than 25 full-time equivalent employees (FTEs)
- Average annual wages less than ~$58,000 (2025, indexed)
- Employer pays at least 50% of employee-only premium cost
- Coverage purchased through SHOP (Small Business Health Options Program) marketplace

**Phase-out:** Credit is reduced for employers with >10 FTEs or average wages >$29,000 (2025 estimate)

**Duration:** Maximum of 2 consecutive years in the SHOP marketplace

### Disabled Access Credit (IRC Section 44)

**Credit:** 50% of eligible access expenditures between $250 and $10,250

**Maximum credit:** $5,000 per year

**Eligible businesses:** Gross receipts < $1,000,000 OR fewer than 30 full-time employees

**Qualifying expenditures:** Barrier removal, providing qualified interpreters/readers, acquiring/modifying equipment, providing materials in accessible formats -- for the purpose of ADA compliance.

### Employer-Provided Childcare Credit (IRC Section 45F)

**Credit:** 25% of qualified childcare facility expenditures + 10% of qualified childcare resource/referral expenditures

**Maximum credit:** $150,000 per year

**Recapture:** If the facility ceases to be a childcare facility within 10 years, a portion of the credit is recaptured.

### New Markets Tax Credit (IRC Section 45D)

**Credit:** 39% of investment made in a qualified Community Development Entity (CDE) over 7 years:
- Years 1-3: 5% per year
- Years 4-7: 6% per year

**Purpose:** Incentivize investment in low-income communities

**Allocated by CDFI Fund:** Limited allocation, competitive application process

**Typical structure:** Investor makes qualified equity investment in CDE, CDE makes qualified low-income community investments, investor receives credits over 7 years.

### Historic Rehabilitation Credit (IRC Section 47)

**Credit:** 20% of qualified rehabilitation expenditures (QREs) for certified historic structures

**Requirements:**
- Building must be a **certified historic structure** (listed on the National Register of Historic Places, or contributing building in a registered historic district)
- Rehabilitation must be **substantial** (QREs must exceed the greater of $5,000 or the adjusted basis of the building)
- Must retain 50-75% of external walls (depending on structure)
- Work must be certified by the National Park Service
- Must be used for income-producing purposes (not personal residence)

**Credit taken over 5 years:** 4% per year for 5 years (effective with TCJA change from immediate to 5-year recognition)

**Interaction with depreciation:** Building placed on 27.5 or 39-year MACRS; basis reduced by credit amount.

### Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (IRC Section 42)

**Credit:** Approximately 9% (new construction/substantial rehabilitation) or 4% (acquisition/tax-exempt bond financed) of eligible basis, for 10 years

**Allocated by state housing agencies:** Competitive process, limited annual allocation

**Compliance period:** 30 years (initial 15 years + 15-year extended use period)

**For investors:** LIHTC is the primary tax credit used in tax credit syndication. Limited partners in LIHTC partnerships receive credits and losses. Typical returns: 8-12% IRR from credits + depreciation.

## Foreign Tax Credit (IRC Section 901)

### Overview

Credit for income taxes paid or accrued to foreign governments, preventing double taxation of the same income.

### Election: Credit vs. Deduction

Taxpayers can elect to either:
1. **Credit** foreign taxes (Form 1116) -- usually better because dollar-for-dollar offset
2. **Deduct** foreign taxes (Schedule A) -- only beneficial if credit is limited

### Per-Basket Limitation

The credit is limited to the US tax attributable to foreign-source income, calculated separately for each "basket":
- **Passive category income** (dividends, interest, rents, royalties)
- **General category income** (wages, business income)
- **Section 901(j) income** (sanctioned countries -- no credit)
- **Certain income re-sourced by treaty**

**Limitation formula:**
```
FTC limit = US tax x (Foreign source taxable income in basket / Worldwide taxable income)
```

### Carryback/Carryforward

- **1 year back, 10 years forward**
- Excess credits in a basket can only offset US tax on foreign income in the same basket in carryover years

### Simplified Method (for Passive Income Only)

If total creditable foreign taxes do not exceed $300 ($600 MFJ) and all foreign income is passive (dividends, interest from mutual funds), the taxpayer can claim the credit directly on Form 1040 without filing Form 1116.

### High-Tax Kickout

If foreign tax on passive income exceeds the highest US tax rate, the income is re-characterized as general category (preventing waste of credits in the passive basket).

## AMT Credit (IRC Section 53)

### How It Is Generated

When a taxpayer pays Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) in a given year, the AMT attributable to **deferral preferences** (timing differences like ISO exercises, depreciation) generates an AMT credit.

AMT paid due to **exclusion preferences** (state/local tax deduction, private activity bond interest) does NOT generate an AMT credit.

### How It Is Used

In future years when **regular tax exceeds tentative minimum tax**, the AMT credit offsets the excess. The credit is carried forward **indefinitely** until fully used.

### Common Scenario

```
Year 1: Exercise ISOs, AMT triggered
  Regular tax: $50,000
  Tentative minimum tax: $80,000
  AMT paid: $30,000
  AMT credit generated: $30,000 (all from ISO deferral preference)

Year 2: No ISOs, normal year
  Regular tax: $60,000
  Tentative minimum tax: $20,000
  AMT credit available: $30,000
  AMT credit used: $60,000 - $20,000 = $40,000 available space, but only $30,000 credit
  Credit used: $30,000
  Net tax: $60,000 - $30,000 = $30,000

Total tax over 2 years: $80,000 + $30,000 = $110,000
(Same as if no AMT: $50,000 + $60,000 = $110,000 -- AMT was just a timing acceleration)
```

## Premium Tax Credit (IRC Section 36B)

### Overview

Refundable credit for individuals and families who purchase health insurance through the ACA marketplace (Healthcare.gov or state exchanges).

### Eligibility

- Household income between 100% and 400% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL)
- Not eligible for affordable employer-sponsored coverage
- Not eligible for government programs (Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, TRICARE)
- Must file a tax return (even if not otherwise required)
- Cannot file MFS (exception for certain domestic abuse situations)

### 2025 Federal Poverty Level Guidelines (48 Contiguous States)

| Household Size | 100% FPL | 150% FPL | 200% FPL | 300% FPL | 400% FPL |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $15,650 | $23,475 | $31,300 | $46,950 | $62,600 |
| 2 | $21,150 | $31,725 | $42,300 | $63,450 | $84,600 |
| 3 | $26,650 | $39,975 | $53,300 | $79,950 | $106,600 |
| 4 | $32,150 | $48,225 | $64,300 | $96,450 | $128,600 |

(2025 estimates -- actual numbers published by HHS)

### Enhanced Credits (IRA Extension Through 2025)

The IRA extended enhanced PTC provisions that:
- Cap premiums at **8.5% of household income** for those above 400% FPL (eliminating the "cliff")
- Provide more generous subsidies at all income levels
- Eliminate repayment caps for excess advance payments at lower income levels

**Planning note:** These 2025 enhanced-credit rules should not be projected forward blindly.
Verify the current Form 8962 instructions and current-law repayment rules before giving advice
for tax years after 2025.

### Reconciliation on Form 8962

Advance PTC payments are made directly to insurance companies throughout the year. At tax filing, the taxpayer reconciles on Form 8962:
- If advance payments exceeded actual PTC: **repay the excess** (subject to caps at lower incomes)
- If advance payments were less than actual PTC: **receive additional refund**

### Common Triggers for Repayment

- Income ended up higher than estimated (bonus, side income, capital gain)
- Marriage during the year (combined income exceeds threshold)
- A child ages out of household
- Availability of employer coverage mid-year

## Credit Ordering Rules

### Non-Refundable Personal Credits (Applied First)

Credits are applied against tax liability in this order:
1. Child Tax Credit (section 24) -- non-refundable portion
2. Credit for the elderly or disabled (section 22)
3. Education credits (section 25A)
4. Child and Dependent Care Credit (section 21)
5. Saver's Credit (section 25B)
6. Residential Energy Credits (sections 25C and 25D)
7. Adoption Credit (section 23)
8. Other non-refundable personal credits

### Foreign Tax Credit (Applied Second)

After personal credits reduce the tax, the Foreign Tax Credit (section 901/27) is applied, subject to its per-basket limitation.

### General Business Credit (Applied Third)

The section 38 General Business Credit is applied next, subject to its limitation (cannot reduce tax below tentative minimum tax or 25% threshold).

Within the General Business Credit, ordering is:
1. Carryforwards (FIFO by year generated)
2. Current-year credits (in the order listed in section 38(b))
3. Carrybacks from future years

### AMT (Calculated Fourth)

The Alternative Minimum Tax is computed on tentative minimum tax minus credits-reduced regular tax.

### Refundable Credits (Applied Last)

After all non-refundable credits and AMT:
1. Earned Income Credit (section 32)
2. Additional Child Tax Credit (section 24 refundable portion)
3. American Opportunity Credit refundable portion (section 25A)
4. Premium Tax Credit (section 36B)
5. Other refundable credits

## Strategic Credit Stacking

### Individual Taxpayer Maximum Credits (2025 Example)

A married filing jointly couple with 3 children under 17, earning $120,000, installing solar, buying an EV:

```
Child Tax Credit: 3 x $2,200 =                          $6,600
Child and Dependent Care Credit (2+ children):           $1,200  (20% of $6,000)
American Opportunity Credit (1 student):                 $2,500
Saver's Credit (both contribute $2,000 to IRA):         $   800  (20% of $4,000)
Section 25D (solar + battery, $37K):                    $11,100
Section 25C (heat pump):                                $ 2,000
Section 30D (new EV):                                   $ 7,500
Total credits:                                          $31,700

Tax liability (approx. for $120K MFJ, 3 dependents):   ~$7,200

Non-refundable credits applied:                          $7,200  (reduces tax to $0)
Refundable ACTC: 3 x $1,700 =                          $5,100
Refundable AOC portion: $1,000
Total refund:                                           $6,100

Carryforward:
  Section 25D unused: $11,100 - ~$4,000 used = ~$7,100 carried forward
  Section 30D unused: $7,500 - ~$0 used = $7,500 (lost -- no carryforward)

NOTE: EV credit (section 30D) has NO carryforward. Better strategy: transfer to dealer
at point of sale for immediate benefit.
```

### Business Taxpayer Credit Stacking

A small business owner (S Corp, sole proprietor, or partnership):

```
R&D Credit (section 41):                                $140,000
WOTC (section 51):                                      $ 12,000
Disabled Access Credit (section 44):                    $  5,000
Small Employer Health Insurance (section 45R):          $ 15,000
General Business Credit total:                          $172,000

Plus personal credits:
  Foreign Tax Credit:                                   $  3,000
  Residential Energy (section 25D):                     $ 11,100

Total credits against tax liability:                    $186,100
```

Subject to section 38(c) limitation -- GBC cannot reduce tax below TMT or 25% threshold.

## Anti-Abuse and Compliance Rules

### Due Diligence (Paid Preparers)

IRC section 6695(g) imposes **$560/return penalty** on paid preparers who fail to comply with due diligence requirements for:
- Earned Income Credit
- Child Tax Credit / ACTC / Other Dependents Credit
- American Opportunity Credit
- Head of Household filing status

Must complete Form 8867 for each return claiming these credits.

### Fraudulent Credit Claims

Taxpayers who fraudulently claim the EIC are barred from claiming it for **10 years** (section 32(k)(1)(B)). Taxpayers whose EIC is denied due to reckless or intentional disregard are barred for **2 years**.

Similar rules apply to the CTC (section 24(g)).

### Recapture Provisions

Several credits have recapture provisions if conditions change:
- **Section 48 ITC:** 5-year recapture period (see ENERGY-AND-GREEN-TAX-CREDITS.md)
- **Section 45F childcare:** 10-year recapture
- **Section 42 LIHTC:** 15-year compliance period with recapture
